Storyline Threads in Mid-Spring

The arcs the Connections Desk is currently tracking through the offseason.

By The Connections Desk 3 MIN READ VOL. I · NO. 19

A storyline thread is a multi-edition arc: something that's been written about before, has a named cast, and will be written about again[1]. The point of tracking them as threads is so each new chapter respects what came before — no character rewrites between editions, no quiet contradictions of last month's framing.

Five threads are currently active in mid-spring: the QB-room churn at programs whose veteran starters left for the draft; the spring-portal redistributions (who took, who lost, and which programs played the timing well)[2]; the coaching-staff additions that hinted at scheme shifts in fall; the recruiting reads that survived spring evaluation[3]; and the receipts on which preseason takes from the prior cycle actually held up.

Each gets its own chapter every two-to-three weeks until the next significant input arrives. Threads close when their named arc resolves; they go dormant when there's nothing new to say.
