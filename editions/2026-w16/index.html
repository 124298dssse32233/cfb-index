The Post-Draft Reset

Twenty-three first-round picks left college football in 72 hours. What got drafted was the last residue of the playoff-era roster build.

  • COVER ESSAY The Draft Pulled the Roster Curtain Back Twenty-three first-round picks left the sport in seventy-two hours. The programs that lost the most were the programs that were holding it together.
  • FEATURE What Texas Has Left After Four Picks Sarkisian lost his starting QB, two offensive linemen, and his best edge in seventy-two hours. The roster, on paper, is still top-five.
  • CONNECTION Boise's Zero-Loss Roster Echoes Cincinnati 2021 Cincinnati went undrafted in the 2021 first round and made the CFP that fall. Boise's 2026 silhouette is the same.
  • RECEIPT The Texas-Will-Drop-Three-Spots Prediction In February we said Texas would lose four first-rounders and drop to 3 in the post-draft polls. They did.
  • FAN VOICE A Boise Subreddit Comment that Said the Quiet Part Verbatim. Twenty-eight words. Three thousand upvotes.
  • DISAGREEMENT The 'Notre Dame Climbed Two Spots' Disagreement Notre Dame jumped 6→4 in the composite. Notre Dame fans think it should be 6→2. The stat folks think it should be 6→5.
