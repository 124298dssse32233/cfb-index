# Proprietary Fan Intelligence Ideation

Research date: 2026-04-21

## Purpose

This memo is a deliberate correction to an important drift in the offseason planning work.

The drift was:

- too much emphasis on analyst-style preseason products
- too much emphasis on acting like a national opinion shop

The desired direction is:

- lean harder into proprietary public-web collection
- build products from fan conversation, rivalry behavior, attention spikes, and narrative change
- make the site feel like a `college football fan lab`, not a generic preview magazine

## Core positioning shift

The site should not mainly say:

- `here is our expert opinion on your roster and schedule`

It should mainly say:

- `here is what college football fandom is doing, feeling, exaggerating, and fighting about`

That is a much better fit for:

- the user's taste
- the current conversation-intelligence work
- the actual moat created by scraped public data

## The strongest proprietary lane

The strongest lane is not `analytics commentary`.

It is:

- `fan anthropology`
- `narrative drift`
- `belief tracking`
- `rival hostility mapping`
- `hype vs structure`

That means the product should feel like:

- a mood lab
- a discourse observatory
- a fan-culture intelligence system

not:

- a preseason annual with some charts

## What counts as proprietary here

The proprietary part does **not** require secret data.

It comes from:

- collecting the right public signals consistently
- splitting them into meaningful audience buckets
- aggregating them over time
- packaging them into readable recurring products

The moat is:

- the collection design
- the classification design
- the recurring categories
- the visual language

## Product principle

If a feature depends on the site sounding like a trusted top-25 columnist, it is weak for this project.

If a feature depends on:

- careful collection
- interesting categorization
- behavior over time
- fan emotion
- disagreement

then it is strong.

## Best proprietary module families

### 1. Respect Gap

Definition:

- the gap between how a team's own fans feel and how the broader national conversation feels

Why it is strong:

- every fanbase instantly understands it
- it does not require the site to declare who is right
- it is explainable in one sentence

Best uses:

- homepage leaderboard
- team page anchor module
- weekly article / card

### 2. Panic Index

Definition:

- how much negative emotion, fear, and defensive language has spiked inside a fanbase

Why it is strong:

- offseason fandom is full of insecurity, dread, and cope
- panic is often more interesting than optimism

Best uses:

- post-spring overreaction content
- August camp anxiety cards
- "Most Panicked Fanbases" leaderboard

### 3. Delusion Meter

Definition:

- fan optimism minus structural reality

Structural reality can come from:

- your model
- returning production
- market expectations

Why it is strong:

- it is funny
- it creates shareable disagreement
- it lets the site be opinionated without pretending to be omniscient

This is one of the best examples of a proprietary blend:

- conversation data + football data + presentation

### 4. Rival Heat

Definition:

- how much a team is being mocked, feared, or obsessed over by rival audiences

Why it is strong:

- rivalry hostility is a native college-football emotion
- it is more fun than generic negativity

Best uses:

- rivalry pages
- homepage leaderboard
- player controversy cards for featured stars

### 5. Belief Shift

Definition:

- week-over-week or event-over-event change in fan confidence

Why it is strong:

- movement is more interesting than level
- it creates a recurring editorial rhythm

Best uses:

- `Biggest Vibe Shifts`
- `Who gained belief`
- `Who lost belief`

### 6. National Fascination

Definition:

- which teams outsiders cannot stop talking about, whether positively or negatively

Why it is strong:

- attention itself is a story
- some teams dominate the sport's conversation even when fans of other teams are tired of hearing about them

Best uses:

- `Main Character of the Week`
- `Most Discussed Rebuilds`
- `Programs everyone has an opinion on`

### 7. Fanbase Civil War

Definition:

- the amount of disagreement *within* a fanbase

Example patterns:

- split between optimism and despair
- QB battle infighting
- coach-trust fracture

Why it is strong:

- more interesting than average sentiment
- feels novel
- fits forums, Reddit threads, and comment sections very well

This is an especially good proprietary concept because mainstream sites almost never productize intra-fanbase disagreement.

### 8. Storyline Gravity

Definition:

- which themes keep pulling a team's conversation back in

Examples:

- offensive line panic
- portal optimism
- QB mystery
- recruiting paranoia
- defense skepticism

Why it is strong:

- it gets beyond polarity
- it explains what the fanbase is *about*, not just whether it is happy

### 9. Hope Inventory

Definition:

- the specific reasons a fanbase still believes

Examples:

- returning QB
- new coordinator
- portal class
- weak division
- star freshman

Why it is strong:

- positive framing
- helps the site avoid becoming only cynical
- gives each team a readable emotional fingerprint

### 10. Offseason Identity Card

Definition:

- a compact "this is what this fanbase is like right now" card

Could include:

- Fan Pulse
- Respect Gap
- Panic Index
- top three storylines
- what fans believe
- what rivals say

Why it is strong:

- compact
- readable
- team-page friendly
- very shareable

## The best recurring categories

These are not just modules. They are repeatable editorial containers.

### Weekly or recurring categories

- `Biggest Vibe Shifts`
- `Most Panicked Fanbases`
- `Most Delusional Fanbases`
- `Rival Heat Leaders`
- `Main Character of the Week`
- `Fanbase Civil War Watch`
- `Belief vs Reality`
- `What Everyone Is Arguing About`
- `What Fans Are Lying To Themselves About`
- `What Outsiders Keep Getting Wrong`

These all rely much more on collected behavior than on the site's expert authority.

## Best page concepts

### 1. Fan Lab homepage section

A dedicated homepage zone for:

- mood leaderboards
- storyline leaderboards
- obsession boards
- rivalry heat boards

This should feel like a live culture section, not a utility widget.

### 2. Team Mood pages

These should not be pure roster pages.

They should answer:

- what is the mood?
- why is that the mood?
- what are fans clinging to?
- what are rivals mocking?
- what changed lately?

### 3. Rivalry pages

This is a strong proprietary lane.

Not just:

- all-time record
- last game result

But:

- who fears whom more
- who is mocking whom more
- what each side thinks the other is delusional about

### 4. Weekly Narrative Board

A weekly page made of:

- vibe shifts
- panic spikes
- rivalry heat
- national fascination
- belief/reality gaps

This is probably one of the best recurring offseason pages.

### 5. Media Days Lie Detector

This is a much better tentpole than a standard media-days recap.

It would compare:

- team quote optimism
- fan optimism
- national skepticism
- structural support

This keeps the page in the fan-intelligence lane.

## Best visuals for this direction

### Best visual treatments

- dumbbell charts for disagreement
- annotated slope charts for belief change
- quadrant plots for hype vs structural support
- stacked emotion ribbons
- compact identity cards
- rivalry faceoff cards

### More distinctive visual ideas

- `fan fingerprint` cards
- `civil war` split bars
- `obsession wheel` or ranked storyline pills
- `cope meter` / `panic meter` / `respect gap` gauges, used sparingly and with taste

## Strongest concepts by feasibility

### Green-light ideas

These fit the current stack well.

- Respect Gap
- Panic Index
- Rival Heat
- Belief Shift
- Storyline Gravity
- Offseason Identity Card

Why:

- team-level
- built from current or near-current sources
- explainable
- batch-friendly

### Yellow-light ideas

These are strong, but need more care.

- Fanbase Civil War
- National Fascination
- player love / blame cards
- Media Days Lie Detector

Why:

- need better bucket classification
- need stronger storytelling and thresholds
- may need selective manual review

### Red-light ideas for now

- real-time alerts
- full player sentiment coverage
- universal message board ingestion across every fandom site
- causality-heavy betting claims
- anything that needs perfect sarcasm detection

## What to explicitly de-emphasize

These are fine as supporting context but should not be the brand center:

- traditional roster guides
- schedule survival explainers
- expert preseason columns
- generic top-25 pages
- broad national analysis with no proprietary hook

## Recommended voice

The tone should feel:

- curious
- slightly wicked
- emotionally literate
- playful but not stupid

Good voice:

- "Fans think this solves everything."
- "Rivals are not buying it."
- "The nation is fascinated, but not convinced."
- "This fanbase has moved from hope to bargaining."

Bad voice:

- "Our projection system has determined a 0.18 negative sentiment residual."

## Best one-sentence product description

The site is building a `fan intelligence layer for college football` that tracks how fanbases, rivals, and the wider sport talk about teams, then turns that into readable, funny, argument-fueling products.

## Best next build target

If we want to lean into this direction immediately, the best next public-facing module is:

- `Team Mood / Identity Card`

That one card can hold:

- Fan Pulse
- National Mood
- Respect Gap
- Panic Index
- Rival Heat
- top storylines

It is the cleanest bridge between:

- current data collection
- distinctive product voice
- fan delight
