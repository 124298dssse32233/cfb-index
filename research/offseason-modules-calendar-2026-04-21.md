# Offseason Modules Calendar

Research date: 2026-04-21

## Goal

Figure out the best fan-facing modules and editorial categories to run from `April` through `August`, with the answer grounded in:

- the real `2026` college football offseason calendar
- the current repo and data stack
- a realistic indie-site publishing rhythm
- a casual-but-nerdy CFB audience

## The most important 2026 calendar facts

### 1. There is no normal April football transfer window anymore

This is the single most important offseason product fact to internalize.

As of the current NCAA Division I materials checked on `April 21, 2026`:

- `FBS` football transfer window: `January 2, 2026` to `January 16, 2026`
- `FCS` football transfer window: `January 2, 2026` to `January 16, 2026`

Sources:

- NCAA Division I notification-of-transfer windows table: [LSDBi report 90008](https://web3.ncaa.org/lsdbi/reports/getReport/90008)
- NCAA Administrative Committee update: [NCAA, Oct. 8, 2025](https://www.ncaa.org/news/2025/10/8/media-center-di-administrative-committee-introduces-proposal-for-commercial-logos-on-uniforms-apparel-and-equipment.aspx)

Implication:

- an `April portal wire` is no longer the obvious core offseason football product
- portal modules should focus on `interpreting` roster change, not pretending there is a live April portal frenzy

### 2. Spring and early-summer recruiting still matter a lot

For `2025-26` FBS recruiting, the official NCAA recruiting calendar currently shows:

- `April 15` through `May 23`: `contact period`
- `May 24-27`: `dead period`
- `May 28` through `June 21`: `quiet period`
- `June 22` through `July 31`: `dead period`

Sources:

- NCAA FBS recruiting calendar PDF: [2025-26 FBS Recruiting Calendar](https://ncaaorg.s3.amazonaws.com/compliance/recruiting/calendar/2025-26/2025-26D1Rec_FBSMFBRecruitingCalendar.pdf)
- NCAA recruiting calendars index: [Division I and II Recruiting Calendars and Guides](https://www.ncaa.org/sports/2018/5/8/division-i-and-ii-recruiting-calendars.aspx)

Implication:

- `May` and `June` are strong for official-visit, class-shaping, and commitment storytelling
- `July` is weaker for visit-driven recruiting news and stronger for `state of the class` interpretation

### 3. Official visits are a real spring/summer content lane

NCAA football official-visit rules continue to matter in the offseason:

- prospects can take football official visits starting `April 1` of the junior year through the Sunday before the last Wednesday in June
- prospects may take unlimited official visits overall, but only `one official visit per school`

Sources:

- NCAA recruiting legislation summary: [Division I Council adopts recruiting legislation](https://www.ncaa.org/about/resources/media-center/news/division-i-council-adopts-recruiting-legislation)
- NCAA official-visits waiver context: [NCAA Nov. 8, 2024 update](https://www.ncaa.org/news/2024/11/8/media-center-fbs-blanket-waiver-suspends-school-limit-on-number-of-football-official-visits.aspx)
- NCAA bylaw references surfaced in LSDBi: [Official visit proposal references](https://web3.ncaa.org/lsdbi/search/proposalView?id=108200)

Important caution:

- the old school-level football official-visit cap still exists in the legislation unless separately waived for a given cycle
- the `April 1, 2025` through `March 31, 2026` waiver for unlimited school official visits is documented
- this memo does **not** assume a fresh `2026-27` waiver unless and until it is explicitly verified

Implication:

- recruiting modules should lean on `visit season`, `class momentum`, and `fit`
- they should not rely on unverified assumptions about current-cycle waivers

### 4. July is media-days season

Confirmed `2026` major-conference media-days timing:

- `Big 12`: `July 7-8, 2026`
- `ACC`: `July 15-17, 2026`
- `SEC`: `July 20-23, 2026`
- `Big Ten`: `July 28-30, 2026`

Sources:

- Big 12: [Big 12 Football Media Days to Return to The Star in 2026 and 2027](https://big12sports.com/news/2025/12/22/big-12-football-media-days-to-return-to-the-star-in-2026-and-2027.aspx)
- ACC: [2026 ACC Football Kickoff Returns to Charlotte July 15-17](https://theacc.com/news/2026/2/12/acc-football-takes-center-stage-2026-acc-football-kickoff-returns-to-charlotte-july-15-17.aspx)
- SEC: [SEC announces 2026 Football Media Days to take place in Tampa](https://www.secsports.com/news/2025/09/sec-announces-2026-football-media-days-to-take-place-in-tampa)
- Big Ten: [2026 Big Ten Football Media Days schedule](https://bigten.org/fb/article/59800/)

Implication:

- `July` should be a tentpole month for conference-race, preseason-belief, and quote-driven mood modules
- the site should feel like it is stepping into the public preseason argument cycle

### 5. August is camp season and preseason-launch season

Current NCAA Division I figure for football shows:

- `FBS` first practice date: `31 days before first scheduled intercollegiate game`
- `FBS` first contest date in `2026`: `September 3, 2026`, with exceptions

Sources:

- NCAA Figure 17-2: [First practice and first contest dates](https://web3.ncaa.org/lsdbi/search/bylawFigure?bylawId=102820)

Practical implication:

- for a standard early-September opener, camp meaningfully begins in early August
- August content should pivot hard toward `depth chart`, `camp risers/fallers`, `Week 0/1`, and `preseason certainty vs uncertainty`

### 6. Week 0 matters in 2026

The ACC has already announced a flashy `2026` Week 0 footprint:

- four `Week 0` games across three continents
- examples include North Carolina vs TCU in Dublin and NC State vs Virginia in Rio

Sources:

- [ACC Football Kicks Off 2026 Season with Four Week 0 Games Across Three Continents](https://theacc.com/news/2026/1/27/acc-football-kicks-off-2026-season-with-four-week-0-games-across-three-continents.aspx)
- [North Carolina to Kick Off 2026 Season Against TCU in Ireland](https://theacc.com/news/2025/4/22/football-north-carolina-to-kick-off-2026-season-against-tcu-in-ireland.aspx)

Implication:

- late August content should not only be generic countdown material
- it should be a real launchpad into actual games

### 7. Late July and early August are awards-watch season

The exact `2026` NCFAA preseason watch-list schedule is not yet confirmed in the sources checked today, but the `2025` coordinated schedule clustered watch-list releases from late July into mid-August.

Source:

- Maxwell/NCFAA coordinated watch-list calendar in `2025`: [Maxwell Football Club 2025 watch list announcement](https://www.maxwellfootballclub.org/news_article/show/1326626)

Inference:

- treat `late July` and `early August` as the natural window for awards-buzz modules
- do not hardcode specific `2026` dates until they are officially released

## What major media outlets emphasize in the offseason

The current `2026` mainstream/offseason content mix is pretty clear:

- `way-too-early top 25`
- `portal classes / newcomer classes`
- `returning production`
- `best-case / worst-case season scenarios`
- `coach pressure / hot seat`
- `post-spring projections`

Illustrative sources:

- ESPN way-too-early top 25: [ESPN](https://www.espn.com/college-football/story/_/id/47610677/college-football-way-too-early-top-25-2026-season)
- ESPN portal classes: [ESPN](https://www.espn.com/college-football/story/_/id/47684263/college-football-transfer-portal-2026-top-classes)
- ESPN SP+ rankings: [ESPN](https://www.espn.com/college-football/story/_/id/48306284/2026-college-football-sp%2B-rankings-138-fbs-teams)
- CBS best/worst-case scenarios: [CBS Sports](https://www.cbssports.com/college-football/news/college-football-top-25-teams-best-worst-case-scenarios-2026-season/)
- CBS coaches under pressure: [CBS Sports](https://www.cbssports.com/college-football/news/10-college-football-coaches-pressure-2026-mike-norvell-luke-fickell/)

Implication:

- fans already have those broad lanes in the market
- the site should not merely duplicate them
- the site should differentiate by making them more visual, more team-specific, more comparative, and more argument-friendly

## Product implications by month

### April

The calendar says:

- spring practice is ending or wrapping up
- there is no standard April football transfer window
- post-spring expectation markets and projection content start to matter

Best angles:

- post-spring stock watch
- roster continuity check
- coordinator/QB battle clarity
- best-case / worst-case arc
- who gained belief after spring

### May

The calendar says:

- recruit contact period is active into late May
- fans are far enough from spring ball to want synthesis, not raw notes

Best angles:

- schedule opportunity maps
- returning-production boards
- roster reset cards
- class momentum / visit season
- early pressure index

### June

The calendar says:

- quiet period through `June 21`
- official-visit season remains central
- fans still want roster talk, but not generic portal noise

Best angles:

- visit-season hub
- class fit and need matching
- biggest roster swings since last fall
- breakout teams and volatile teams
- "what has to go right" team primers

### July

The calendar says:

- media days dominate the month
- recruiting goes dead on the calendar
- awards-watch and preseason-race framing starts

Best angles:

- conference race dashboards
- media-days mood and quote interpretation
- awards-buzz pages
- belief vs market vs model boards
- preseason tiers and overreaction fuel

### August

The calendar says:

- camp begins
- depth charts matter
- Week 0 and opening-week storytelling arrives

Best angles:

- camp risers / fallers
- position-battle clarity
- certainty vs volatility boards
- Week 0 / Week 1 launch pages
- preseason confidence and upset radar

## Candidate module families

These are the main product families that seem strongest before Anthropic critique.

### 1. Roster Reality

A family for:

- returning production
- recruiting / talent
- transfer impact
- roster continuity

This is the best structural offseason family because it is:

- data-backed
- evergreen
- useful from April through August

### 2. Team Outlook

A family for:

- ceiling / floor
- most likely arc
- swing units
- volatility
- expectation pressure

This is strong because fans love asking:

- are we real?
- what is the path?
- what could go wrong?

### 3. Schedule And Path

A family for:

- schedule strength
- schedule shape
- trap games
- playoff path
- must-have swings

This gets stronger from May onward.

### 4. Recruiting And Class Shape

A family for:

- visit season
- commitment momentum
- position need match
- class balance
- pipeline geography

This is strongest in May and June, then becomes more summary-oriented in July.

### 5. Preseason Debate

A family for:

- way-too-early tiers
- respect gap
- over/underrated teams
- coach pressure
- awards buzz

This becomes strongest in July and August.

### 6. Conversation Intelligence

A family for:

- fan pulse
- national mood
- respect gap
- belief check
- vibe shift

This should not be the only offseason family, but it becomes a strong overlay once the base roster/schedule pages exist.

## Early product hypothesis

If the site can only push a few offseason families hard, the best ones appear to be:

1. `Roster Reality`
2. `Schedule And Path`
3. `Preseason Debate`

Those three together cover:

- structural football truth
- argument fuel
- repeat visitation
