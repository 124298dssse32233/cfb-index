Florida is a program that has held the crown twice and knows the weight of it. Three national championships — 1996 under Spurrier, then 2006 and 2008 under Meyer with Tebow as the cultural anchor — and three Heisman winners sit on the shelf, and they are not decoration. They are the standard. The 2025 season closed 4-8, which is the honest number, though the year ended with a 40-21 win over Florida State in Gainesville inside an 88,548-seat Ben Hill Griffin Stadium that still does what it has done since 1930. The Napier restoration, now into its fifth offseason, is the work in front of us: portal, spring, draft, repeat. It's great to be a Florida Gator.

Rituals

The rituals that anchor a Saturday in this program's world.

MTpre-game and post-

Mr. Two Bits

since 1949

The orange-and-blue-suited fan leads 'Two Bits, Four Bits, Six Bits, A Dollar / All for the Gators, Stand Up and Holler!' through the stadium. George Edmondson Jr. was Mr. Two Bits from 1949-2008; the role passed to guest leaders after his retirement. The cheer itself is older; the character defines it.

GCconstantly during

Gator Chomp

since 1981

Two-armed motion mimicking an alligator's jaws snapping shut, set to two notes from the JAWS theme. Invented by the Gator Band during a 1981 timeout; became the universal Florida fan motion within a season. Visible from every section of The Swamp on every defensive stop.

WAend of third quart

We Are The Boys (from Old Florida)

since 1919

Fans link arms with neighbors and sway through the alma-mater-adjacent song. Originated as a 1919 student composition; the swaying ritual stabilized in the 1970s. The most emotionally communal moment of a Florida home game — even casual fans participate.

AAsideline and stand

Albert and Alberta

since 1970

The costumed alligator mascots (Albert since 1970, Alberta since 1986) work the crowd. Distinct from the Gator Chomp — they ARE the chomp embodied, plus 50 years of accumulated mascot lore. The 'Albert push-ups after every score' tradition is non-negotiable.

BGkickoff

Bull Gator Walk

since 1990

Players walk from the team facility to The Swamp through a crowd-lined corridor. Less famous than Auburn's or Clemson's equivalents but essential to Florida game-day. The Steve Spurrier era turned it into an event; subsequent regimes preserved it.

Florida is the program that turned swamp humidity and SEC speed into a national-title formula — and never quite stopped believing the formula still works.

The Chronicle

2025 season · editorial observations
MOMENT

Florida Opens 2025 With 55-0 Shutout of Long Island University

The Swamp opened the Napier Restoration's fourth season with a statement: 55 points, nothing surrendered, Long Island University sent home quiet on August 30th. It's the first shutout win for Florida since the Mullen Era, and the margin sits 57 points above where this team finished on average a year ago. The signal is early, but Albert and Alberta haven't seen the scoreboard light up like that in a while.

gamelog · 2025 season · wk 1
ANOMALY

Florida's Explosive Play Rate Hasn't Been This Barren in Decades

Through 12 games in 2025, Florida's offense ranks at the 3rd percentile of FBS in explosive play rate — a number that would have been unthinkable during the Spurrier Era or the Meyer Dynasty. The Swamp has hosted some of college football's most electric attacks; right now it is generating almost none of them. The Napier Restoration needs a spark, and it needs one fast.

Savant card · 2025 season through postseason
ANOMALY

Florida's 2025 Split: The Swamp Giveth, the Road Taketh

Through eleven games in 2025, Florida is averaging a +10.3 margin inside Ben Hill Griffin Stadium and -17.4 away from it — a 28-point swing that is among the starkest home-road splits in recent Gator history. No Florida team since the late Muschamp years has looked this different depending on the zip code. The Swamp still works. Everything outside it is still a problem the Napier Restoration has to solve.

gamelog · 2025 season
FLASHPOINT

Georgia Takes Jacksonville Again, 24-20, Third Straight

Florida lost to Georgia 24-20 in 2025 — a four-point game that stings worse than the 23-point blowout in 2023, because this one was close enough to feel. The Gators have dropped three straight in the World's Largest Outdoor Cocktail Party, the longest such skid since the Zook Interval handed Georgia three in a row before Meyer arrived. The Swamp doesn't apologize; the Swamp rebuilds.

That LIU Blowout Now Reads Like a Warning Sign

Florida opened 2025 with a 55-0 rout of Long Island University and the Swamp felt like a promise. The season closed 4-8 — the program's worst record since the Zook Interval — and that opener looks less like a signal than a mirage. The Napier Restoration has a long way left to go.
