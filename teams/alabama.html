Alabama is the program the rest of college football measures itself against. Eighteen claimed national championships, thirty-three SEC titles, four Heisman winners from Ingram to Bryce Young, and a hundred-thousand-seat stadium that has spent a century watching the standard get set — that is the ledger the 2025 season closes against. Eleven and four, a #9 committee ranking, and a 3-38 finish at Indiana are not what the Saban inheritance was built to produce, and the program is not interested in pretending otherwise. DeBoer's second offseason is the honest read: spring practice, the portal, and the draft pipeline doing what they have always done, against a number that has to move. The process does not flinch at rankings.
