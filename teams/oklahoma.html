Oklahoma is a crown program crossing leagues and still carrying the crown. Seven national championships, seven Heisman winners — a count tied only with Notre Dame and Ohio State — and a second SEC season that closed at 10-3, ranked eighth across the AP, coaches, and committee polls. The year ended with a 24-34 loss to Alabama in Week 17, a result that reads as the cost of doing business in a new league rather than evidence against the project. Brent Venables, in his fourth offseason since December 2021, is now building rosters explicitly for this conference. The Schooner still rolls before kickoff at Gaylord Family Oklahoma Memorial Stadium; 83,489 still gather. Oklahoma's floor is what other programs call a ceiling. Boomer Sooner.

ANOMALY

Oklahoma's Run Defense Is Quietly Doing Something Special in 2025

Opponents are averaging -0.091 EPA per rushing attempt against the Sooners this season — a figure that ranks at the 98th percentile of all FBS programs in 2025 and the 96th percentile against all P4 competition. That's the kind of trench dominance Oklahoma defenses posted routinely under Bud Wilkinson and Barry Switzer, but hadn't recaptured at this level in years. The Venables Reinvention has a spine now.

ANOMALY

Oklahoma's Pass Defense Is Suffocating Offenses at a Historic Rate

Opponents are generating an EPA of just 0.039 per passing play against Oklahoma in 2025 — a figure that ranks at the 95th percentile of all FBS seasons on record. The Venables Reinvention promised a defense rebuilt from the foundation, and this unit is delivering on that promise harder than any Sooner defense since the Stoops era's peak. OU DNA runs through the secondary right now.

Oklahoma's Defense Holds Opponents to a 29.4% Success Rate in 2025

The Sooner defense is allowing a 29.4 percent success rate this season — better than 99.6 percent of every FBS program in 2025. That number ranks in the 96th percentile against all teams in the modern era, a standard Oklahoma hasn't touched since the Stoops defenses that made Norman a graveyard for opposing offenses. The Venables Reinvention is showing its teeth where it always promised to.
