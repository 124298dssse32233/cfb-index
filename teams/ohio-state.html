ANOMALY

Will Howard's Passing Attack Ranks Among Ohio State's All-Time Best

Ohio State's passing offense sits at the 98th percentile of all FBS programs in 2025 — a number that holds up even against the program's own decorated history, landing at the 87th percentile all-time. The Buckeyes haven't posted passing efficiency at this level since the peak of the Meyer Reconstruction. The Horseshoe didn't rebuild the offense to be good. It built it to be unreachable.

Lining Up Against Ohio State's Front Has Become a Bad Idea

Opponent rushing EPA sits at the 94th percentile of FBS through 14 games — the Buckeyes are suffocating opposing ground games at a rate better than 88 percent of Power Four programs this season. That minus-0.065 EPA per rush against is the kind of number that shows up in January résumés. The Horseshoe's defensive line hasn't made running the ball look this futile since the Tressel Era's nastiest fronts.

FLASHPOINT

Buckeyes Reclaim The Game, 27-9 Over Michigan in 2025

Ohio State ended a two-year skid against Michigan with a 27-9 dismantling — the largest margin in this series since before the Wolverines' recent run. The defense held Michigan to single digits for the first time in years, answering 2023 and 2024 with something that sounded less like a response and more like a correction. Go Bucks. Ohio State's Pass Defense Is Suffocating Quarterbacks in 2025

Opponent passing EPA sits at the 93rd percentile of FBS this season — better than nine in ten Power Four programs and the stingiest the Buckeyes have looked in the secondary since the Tressel-era bend-but-don't-break defenses gave way to something meaner.
