## program_history

Texas Tech football's modern peak was Mike Leach's 2008 11-2 + Cotton Bowl run — the year of Michael Crabtree's iconic last-second catch vs Texas. The Leach era (2000-2009) cemented the Air Raid as the program's permanent offensive identity. Kliff Kingsbury's 2013-2018 era produced Patrick Mahomes (2014-2016) but no sustained team-level success. Joey McGuire's 2022-present tenure brought back competitive cycles.

## fanbase_summary

Red Raider fans are Lubbock-air-raid-loyalists. Guns Up as the universal identity marker; tortilla-throwing as the chaotic-fan-tradition signature; the geographic isolation of Lubbock (Big 12's most-rural P5 city) concentrates the regional fanbase. The Air Raid blueprint identification gives the program a national offensive-philosophy footprint.

## rivalry_context

Texas (the historic Big 12-era structural rival) was lost to the 2024 SEC realignment. TCU and Baylor (in-state Big 12 partners) are the modern annual fixtures. Texas A&M (the College Station rival from 1990s era) was the lost-to-realignment fixture. Big 12 realignment continues to reshape.
