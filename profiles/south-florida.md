## program_history

South Florida football's all-time peak was Jim Leavitt's 2007 season (briefly #2 AP rank after wins over Auburn and West Virginia + Sun Bowl appearance). The program was founded in 1997 under Leavitt + went FCS → FBS → Big East → American Athletic across the next decade. Leavitt's 2009 firing was abrupt. Skip Holtz + Willie Taggart eras followed. Charlie Strong's 2017-2019 tenure (post-Texas) produced consecutive 10-2 and 7-6 seasons + AAC East contention. Jeff Scott (2020-2022) struggled; Alex Golesh's 2023-present rebuild produced 2024 7-6 + bowl eligibility.

## fanbase_summary

Bull fans are Tampa-Bay-Bulls-survivors. The Leavitt founding era + 2007 #2 AP peak + Strong rebuild + Raymond James Stadium NFL co-tenant venue + Tampa metropolitan-area split-loyalty (vs Florida / FSU / Miami) combine into a fanbase identity distinct from the larger flagship-state programs.

## rivalry_context

UCF (the In-State War on I-4 rivalry) is the structural Florida fixture. Cincinnati (the Big East / AAC era rival, now Big 12) is the lost-to-realignment fixture. The 2024 AAC reshuffle continues to develop annual rival cycles. Florida State / Miami / Florida fixtures remain wishlist non-conference targets but unattainable in current scheduling era.
