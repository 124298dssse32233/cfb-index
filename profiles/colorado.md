## program_history

Colorado football's 1990 national title under Bill McCartney is the program's only national peak, anchored by Rashaan Salaam's 1994 Heisman and McCartney's three Big 8 titles. The 2000s-2010s were the wilderness era — multiple coaching changes, conference realignment from Big 12 to Pac-12 (2011), no signature seasons. Deion Sanders' 2023 hire is the most aggressive program reset in modern CFB.

## fanbase_summary

Buffalo fans are mountain-west-cultural-loyalists, but the Coach Prime era added a national media-tourism layer that no other CFB fanbase has. Folsom Field tailgates with the Flatirons backdrop, Coach Prime's recruiting tornado, and Ralphie's pre-game charge are now distinct identities that coexist.

## rivalry_context

Nebraska (the historical Big 8 rival) was the 1990s gravitational center. Colorado State (Rocky Mountain Showdown) is the in-state structural rival. Utah is the Pac-12-era rival that the realignment to Big 12 (2024) reshuffled. Coach Prime's national profile makes every Saturday feel like a rivalry game.
