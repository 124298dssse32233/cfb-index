## program_history

West Virginia football's modern peak was 2007 — 11-2, Fiesta Bowl appearance, Pat White / Steve Slaton offensive dominance, top-5 ranking. The 2007 Pittsburgh upset (the Backyard Brawl that cost WVU a national championship spot) remains the program's most-cited what-if. Dana Holgorsen's 2011-2018 tenure stabilized the program; Neal Brown's 2019-2023 era was the transition; the modern era continues to seek the next Big 12 contender.

## fanbase_summary

Mountaineer fans are Appalachian-loyalist-loud. WVU is the only Power-5 football program in the state — total geographic monopoly creates Saturday densities at Milan Puskar that rival any SEC venue. Country Roads' communal singing at game's end ties the program directly to the state's identity in a way no other CFB program matches.

## rivalry_context

Pittsburgh (Backyard Brawl) is the historic structural rival — 125+ years, 60-mile geography, restored 2022 after the conference-realignment hiatus. Marshall (Friends of Coal Bowl) is the in-state secondary rivalry. The 2012 Big 12 move from Big East reshuffled every meaningful rivalry context; current Big 12 partners (Texas Tech, Kansas State, Iowa State) are the annual fixtures.
