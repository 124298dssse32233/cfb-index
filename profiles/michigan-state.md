## program_history

Michigan State football is the Mark Dantonio era's monument. The 2007-2019 tenure produced 3 Big Ten titles, a 2015 CFP appearance, and 8 wins in 11 attempts against Michigan during a stretch when that was supposed to be impossible. Pre-Dantonio and post-Dantonio eras share a 6-6-to-8-4 rhythm; the Dantonio peak is the program's modern reference standard.

## fanbase_summary

Spartan fans are chip-shoulder-Spartans. The Michigan rivalry is the spiritual center. The program's underdog identity is structural — recruiting the secondary tier of in-state talent, building defense first, treating every Big Ten road win as a referendum on the national perception of MSU. Mark Dantonio's 2007 hire is the canonical "before-and-after" moment.

## rivalry_context

Michigan (Paul Bunyan Trophy) is the calendar's gravitational center. Indiana (Old Brass Spittoon) is the cheerful low-stakes rivalry. Ohio State has been the Big Ten East structural blocker since divisional alignment. Penn State has compounded into a real-stakes meeting.
