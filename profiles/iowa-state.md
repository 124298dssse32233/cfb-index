## program_history

Iowa State football's modern peak is the Matt Campbell 2017-present era — the program's first sustained top-25 cycles, 2020 Fiesta Bowl appearance, Brock Purdy's three-year starter run, multiple bowl wins. The pre-Campbell century was largely competitive irrelevance with sporadic Big 8 winning seasons. Campbell's tenure is structurally the longest sustained run in program history.

## fanbase_summary

Cyclone fans are Ames-patient-underdog-loyalists. Jack Trice Stadium tailgates with the Iowa farm-country backdrop are among the Big 12's most-loved Saturday experiences. The fanbase's century of patience makes the modern Campbell-era success more emotionally resonant than any equivalent peak at a Blue Blood program.

## rivalry_context

Iowa (the Cy-Hawk Trophy) is the structural in-state rival — a non-conference annual fixture that defines preseason marketing for both programs. Kansas State (Big 12 partner) is the secondary rivalry. The 2024 Texas/Oklahoma SEC exits reshaped ISU's Big 12 positioning into senior-member status.
