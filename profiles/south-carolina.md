## program_history

South Carolina football is the SEC East's eternally-rising tide. Steve Spurrier's 2010-2013 peak (44-15 record, three straight 11-win seasons, two Outback Bowl wins) is the program's ceiling. Pre-Spurrier and post-Spurrier eras share a recurring 6-6 to 8-4 rhythm that the fanbase has internalized as 'gamecock football.'

## fanbase_summary

Gamecock fans are SEC-underdog-pugnacious. They show up to Williams-Brice in higher percentage than the actual stadium capacity, eat boiled peanuts pre-game, and identify more with the chip-on-the-shoulder narrative than the trophy case. Sandstorm is the spiritual peak; the 2010-2013 Spurrier run is the canonical reference.

## rivalry_context

Clemson (Clean Old-Fashioned Hate) is the only rivalry that matters. The annual November game defines both fanbases' calendars. Within the SEC, Georgia and Florida are the divisional structural blockers; no real cultural enmity, just the schedule.
