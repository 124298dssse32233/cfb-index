## program_history

Louisville football's modern peak was Bobby Petrino's 2014-2018 era — 2016 Lamar Jackson Heisman, 9-4 + Citrus Bowl, three top-25 finishes. The 2018 Petrino dismissal (multiple administrative issues) was the structural reset. Scott Satterfield's 2019-2022 tenure was the wilderness era; Jeff Brohm's 2023 return (Louisville native + Purdue alumnus) brought the program back to 10-4 + Holiday Bowl in his first year. The Brohm era is the active identity.

## fanbase_summary

Cardinal fans are Bluegrass-Louisville-loyalists. The L1C4 identity marker is the universal greeting; L&N Federal Credit Union Stadium tailgates skew urban-Louisville demographic. The basketball program's two national championships are the cultural anchor, but the football program post-Lamar has its own L1C4 fanbase identity.

## rivalry_context

Kentucky (Governor's Cup) is the in-state ACC-vs-SEC structural rivalry. The 2014 ACC move from Big East reshuffled every prior rivalry. Florida State and Clemson are the ACC blockers; Notre Dame (annual ACC partner) is the structural fixture. The 2024 ACC realignment continues to reshape.
