## program_history

Nebraska's three national titles in the 1990s under Tom Osborne (1994, 1995, 1997) are the high-water mark of modern college football excellence — 60 wins in 5 years, two undefeated seasons, the option offense at its terminal velocity. The 2003 Bob Devaney-Frank Solich firing was the program's pivot away from prestige; the Pelini-Riley-Frost wilderness 2008-2022 produced no conference titles, repeated 5-7 seasons, and the most patient suffering in the sport. Matt Rhule's 2023 hire is the latest bet.

## fanbase_summary

Husker fans are cornfield-patient-loyalists. The Sea of Red sells out Memorial Stadium for games the team has no chance of winning. Patient with the football product because Tom Osborne built three decades of equity; honest about the gap between 1995 and 2024.

## rivalry_context

Oklahoma (the Big 8 structural rival from the Osborne era) was lost to realignment. Iowa (the Heroes Game, post-2011 Big Ten move) is the calendar fixture but lacks the Big 8 stakes. Wisconsin and Michigan are the Big Ten East-meets-West annual cycle. The realignment-era 2020s reshuffled every meaningful Husker rivalry context.
