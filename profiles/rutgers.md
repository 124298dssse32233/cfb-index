## program_history

Rutgers football's most-unique historical anchor is the first-ever college football game — November 6, 1869, Rutgers 6 Princeton 4. The modern peak is Greg Schiano's first tenure (2001-2011), particularly the 2006 11-2 + Texas Bowl championship. His Tampa Bay Buccaneers detour + Ohio State assistant tenure ended with the 2020 return. The second Schiano era has rebuilt from a 2-10 floor through bowl-eligibility cycles + Big Ten conference relevance.

## fanbase_summary

Scarlet Knight fans are New-Brunswick-scarlet-loyalists. The 1869 founding-game identity + Schiano two-era program-building + Big Ten Northeast footprint create a fanbase distinct from the larger flagship-state programs. The Chop tradition + the SHI Stadium scarlet-out create modern identity overlay on the historical anchor.

## rivalry_context

Princeton (the 1869 founding-game opponent) is the historical fixture; the rivalry was paused after Princeton's Ivy League non-FBS era. Penn State (the Big Ten East rival by proximity) is the structural conference fixture. The Big Ten realignment continues to reshape rival cycles.
