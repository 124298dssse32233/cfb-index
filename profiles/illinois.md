## program_history

Illinois football's all-time historical peak is the Red Grange era (1923-1925) — particularly the October 18, 1924 Michigan game (4 TDs in 12 minutes). The program holds 5 national titles (1914, 1923, 1927, 1951, 2001 shared BCS). Modern peak is Bret Bielema's 2024 9-3 (his fourth year as head coach). Memorial Stadium (built 1923) and the Red Grange statue anchor the venue.

## fanbase_summary

Illini fans are Champaign-Galloping-Ghost-loyalists. The Red Grange historical anchor + Memorial Stadium architectural heritage + Big Ten conference identity + land-grant flagship-state status combine into a fanbase that values heritage equal to wins. The 2024 Bielema rebuild normalized higher expectations.

## rivalry_context

Northwestern (Land of Lincoln Trophy, the in-state Big Ten fixture) is the structural rival. Indiana (the historical Big Ten East rival) is the divisional fixture. Purdue (the historic Big Ten cycle partner) is the rotating fixture. The Big Ten realignment continues to reshape rival cycles.
