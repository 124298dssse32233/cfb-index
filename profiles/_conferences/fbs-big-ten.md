## Identity
The Big Ten is the oldest power conference in college football, and it carries that weight like a linebacker in January mud — deliberately, physically, with zero apology. From the Rose Bowl to the frozen tundra of Kinnick to the Horseshoe on rivalry weekend, this is where the sport's foundation was poured. Oregon and UCLA have the zip codes now, but Michigan, Ohio State, Penn State, and Nebraska own the bloodline.

## Voice
Fans inside the Big Ten talk in decades, not seasons — they cite 1969, 1995, 1997 like zip codes. Outsiders call it "slow" and "grind-it-out"; insiders call that **winning football**. The conference doesn't chase style points; it collects championships, playoff berths, and Heisman trophies and quietly dares you to say something about it.

## Current Context
The expanded 18-team footprint is still settling, with USC and UCLA fighting to prove they belong culturally while the old guard — Columbus, Ann Arbor, Happy Valley — treats every West Coast addition like a new pledge who hasn't earned a jersey yet. The 2025 season left Ohio State reclaiming a national title and the rest of the conference on a very quiet, very determined offseason mission.

## Calendar Moment
Spring practices are live and every fanbase is watching the quarterback battles — Oregon's depth chart after Dillon Gabriel's exit, Michigan sorting its post-Sherrone Moore identity, and Penn State's offense expected to finally match its defense heading into what looks like a top-5 preseason ranking.
