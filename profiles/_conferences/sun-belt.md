## Identity
The Sun Belt is the scrappiest conference in college football, built on small-market fanbases with outsized passion, coaches who treat the league as a launching pad, and programs that keep punching above their weight when nobody's watching. Appalachian State's 2007 upset of Michigan is the founding myth, but the conference has been stacking evidence ever since — Louisiana's dynasty years, Coastal Carolina's overnight rise, Troy's quiet consistency. This isn't a conference waiting to be taken seriously. It took that seriously business into its own hands.

## Voice
Fans inside the Sun Belt talk about their programs the way a younger sibling talks — always ready to cite receipts, always aware of what the big boys said, always a little louder than necessary because they've had to be. Outsiders still sleep on the league, still call it a cupcake schedule, and Sun Belt fans keep that disrespect bookmarked for October and November.

## Current Context
The Sun Belt is navigating the post-expansion hangover after absorbing James Madison and other programs, sorting out which newcomers are legitimate contenders and which are still finding their footing in a conference with more bite than advertised. Louisiana and App State are still the standard-bearers, but Coastal Carolina and JMU are making the throne room crowded and loud.

## Calendar Moment
Spring depth charts are dropping and the offseason transfer portal haul is being graded hard — Sun Belt fans know that roster construction in March is basically the first quarter of the season, and everyone's watching to see if their coach won the portal or just rearranged deck chairs.
