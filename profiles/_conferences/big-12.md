## Identity
The Big 12 is the most anarchic major conference in college football — a place where a 6-6 team beats a top-ten program on the road and nobody blinks. From Stillwater to Morgantown, from Tucson to Provo, this conference sprawls across time zones and climates but shares one gospel: you can't run the clock, you have to score. Texas and Oklahoma are gone but the DNA they left behind — high-wire offenses, hostile road environments, zero guaranteed wins — runs through every program left standing.

## Voice
Fans inside the Big 12 wear the volatility like a badge. A Kansas State fan tells you "we'll knock off the number one team and lose to someone you've never heard of" like it's a personality trait, not a problem. Outsiders look at the standings in October and call it a mess; Big 12 fans look at the same standings and call it the best conference in America.

## Current Context
Spring 2026 finds the Big 12 in full roster reconstruction mode, with Arizona, Colorado, and Utah still settling into conference culture and BYU firmly establishing itself as a legitimate Big 12 threat after back-to-back strong finishes. Colorado's Shedeur Sanders is gone to the NFL and the Buffs are navigating the post-Deion question every program dreads: what happens when the spectacle has to become a system?

## Calendar Moment
Spring games are wrapping and the transfer portal is still humming — Big 12 fans are watching quarterback rooms across the conference like hawks because in this league, whoever finds their signal-caller first wins the offseason, and the offseason matters almost as much as the season.
