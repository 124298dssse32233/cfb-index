## Identity
The Pac-12 is the most dramatic self-destruction story in modern college football — a Power Five king that watched USC, UCLA, Oregon, Washington, Utah, Colorado, and Arizona all walk out the door between 2022 and 2024. What's left is a four-team conference — Oregon State, Washington State, Cal, and Stanford — playing under a historic banner that refuses to die, anchored by two programs (the Beavers and Cougars) who literally sued to keep the name alive.

## Voice
Fans inside the rebuilt Pac-12 talk with a chip-on-shoulder pride that's equal parts defiance and dark humor — "we're still here" is the whole vibe. Outsiders either laugh or genuinely respect the audacity; there's no middle ground when you watch Oregon State and Washington State refuse to fold while their former conference-mates are fighting for bowl eligibility in the SEC and Big Ten.

## Current Context
Spring 2026 has the Pac-12 operating as a legitimate four-program league with Cal and Stanford fully committed after their brief ACC experiment, and both Oregon State and Washington State coming off seasons where they outperformed most expectations. The conference is in active expansion conversations, with San Jose State, Boise State, and a handful of Mountain West programs circling as potential additions that could push the Pac back toward relevance as a mid-major with real teeth.

## Calendar Moment
Spring practices are wrapping up with Oregon State's defense looking nastier than it has any right to be, and every Pac-12 fan is watching the expansion news cycle with white knuckles — the next school announced as a member could completely change the trajectory of whether this thing becomes a real league again or stays a glorified scheduling agreement.
