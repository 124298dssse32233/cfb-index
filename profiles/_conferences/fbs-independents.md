## Identity
FBS Independents aren't a conference — they're a collection of outlaws, legends, and wildcards who built their brands outside the system entirely. Notre Dame carries the flag as the most famous program in college football history, BYU was here before bolting to the Big 12, and what's left is a scrappy mix of military academies, regional upstarts, and programs betting on themselves. There's no conference championship, no automatic bid, no safety net — just wins and losses and whatever you can negotiate.

## Voice
Fans inside this world don't say "our conference" — they say "our program," full stop, because the program IS everything when there's no league identity to hide behind. Notre Dame fans talk national titles and Heisman trophies; Army and Navy fans talk tradition and service; outsiders look at the independents collectively and mostly just wonder why Notre Dame still refuses to join the Big Ten.

## Current Context
Notre Dame is riding legitimate national title contender energy into spring 2026 after their College Football Playoff run, and the pressure on Marcus Freeman to close the deal on a championship before Big Ten rumors resurface is absolutely real. Meanwhile, UConn and UMass are quietly grinding through another cycle where every home game feels like an existential referendum on whether their programs belong at this level.

## Calendar Moment
Army's spring ball carries genuine buzz after their run of strong seasons under Jeff Monken, and the schedule release for the 2026 Army-Navy Game will send both fan bases into full ceremony-planning mode the moment it drops.
