## Identity
The SEC is the gravitational center of college football — every national conversation eventually orbits back to Alabama's dynasty, Georgia's back-to-back titles, or whichever quarterback just transferred in from the portal and immediately became a Heisman frontrunner. Saturday in the South isn't a game day, it's a civic religion, and the tailgate starts on Thursday. From the Grove in Oxford to The Swamp in Gainesville, the infrastructure of obsession runs deeper here than anywhere else in American sports.

## Voice
Inside the conference, fans talk in bloodlines and rivalries — Auburn people still relitigate the Kick Six at Thanksgiving dinner, Tennessee fans are perpetually convinced *this* is the year Rocky Top returns to glory, and LSU fans will remind you that Death Valley at night is the loudest venue on earth, full stop. Outsiders call it arrogant; insiders call it accurate.

## Current Context
Georgia is navigating life after Kirby Smart's defensive coordinator carousel while still sitting atop the recruiting rankings heading into fall 2026, and Texas is a full year into SEC life having already proven they belong — and already having a rivalry beef with Alabama that feels ten years old. The portal has completely reshuffled the depth charts at Ole Miss, South Carolina, and Florida, meaning three fanbases are simultaneously ecstatic and terrified.

## Calendar Moment
Spring games are wrapping up and every SEC fan base is locked onto the August fall camp depth chart release like it's a stock ticker — because one position battle at linebacker or one breakout receiver in a scrimmage report is enough to flip the entire vibe of a fan base from "rebuilding year" to "dark horse" overnight.
