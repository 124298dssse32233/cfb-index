## Identity
The AAC is the conference that Power programs treat like a talent buffet — UCF, Cincinnati, and Houston bolt for the Big 12, and the league just shrugs and reloads. Tulane, Memphis, UTSA, and SMU's descendants keep the fire burning. This is a conference built on hungry programs with something to prove and fanbases that have never once been handed anything.

## Voice
Inside the AAC, fans talk in terms of disrespect receipts — every slight from a Power program, every snubbed bowl ranking, every "who?" from a SEC talking head gets catalogued and cashed in when the wins pile up. Outsiders still call it a "Group of Five also-ran." Insiders call that fuel.

## Current Context
Spring 2026 finds the AAC in a genuine identity reset, with programs like Tulane and Memphis building legitimate recruiting pipelines and UTSA cementing itself as a program that actually belongs. The conference is quietly watching the expanded College Football Playoff the way a street fighter watches an open bracket — one hot run and everything changes.

## Calendar Moment
Spring practices are the whole conversation right now — Memphis fans want to see whether the offensive line can protect a quarterback long enough to make a CFP run legitimate, and Tulane's fanbase is already circling the schedule looking for a statement win to hang the season on.
