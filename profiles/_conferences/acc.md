## Identity
The ACC is a conference that carries genuine blue-blood weight — Clemson's dynasty, Miami's dynasty before that, Florida State's Bowden era — and spends enormous energy trying to remind everyone of it. It is a conference of coastline campuses, basketball-first fan bases, and just enough football chaos to keep things interesting. The gap between Clemson and literally everyone else has defined the last fifteen years more than any rivalry, any championship, any moment.

## Voice
Fans inside the ACC talk in cycles of resurrection — someone is always "back," some program is always "turning the corner," some coordinator hire is always "the one." Outsiders talk about the ACC the way you talk about a restaurant that used to be great: respectful nostalgia, quiet skepticism, occasional genuine surprise. The conference has a chip on its shoulder the size of the Orange Bowl.

## Current Context
The Grant James era at Miami is generating legitimate buzz heading into 2026, and Clemson is reloading after another double-digit win season under Dabo with a retooled offensive line that scouts are watching closely. Florida State's slow rebuild under a new staff is the most-watched storyline in the conference — the talent is there, the urgency is real, and the fan base has zero patience left.

## Calendar Moment
Spring games across the conference wrap in late April, and every ACC fan base is watching Miami's spring depth chart like it's breaking news — the Hurricanes haven't entered a season with this much legitimate expectation in fifteen years, and the fan base knows exactly how badly it needs to hold.
