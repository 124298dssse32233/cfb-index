## Identity
The Mountain West is the blue-collar conference of the Group of Five — built on Boise State's Fiesta Bowl legacy, UNLV's sudden rise from laughingstock to contender, and San Diego State's perpetual ability to ruin someone's season with ugly, effective football. This is a conference where facilities are modest, recruiting battles are uphill, and wins over Power Four programs hit different because nobody handed you anything. The altitude isn't just a geographic fact — it's a personality.

## Voice
Fans inside the Mountain West talk like people who've been doubted their whole lives and stopped caring whether you agree with them. They don't ask for respect anymore — they point at the scoreboard. Outsiders treat the MWC like a footnote until Boise State goes 10-2 and a New Year's Six bid is on the table, then suddenly everyone rediscovers how "underrated" the conference is.

## Current Context
Spring 2026 finds the Mountain West in a genuine identity crisis — and a genuine opportunity. UNLV is riding momentum from back-to-back bowl appearances and Barry Odom has that program believing, while Boise State is reloading after another frustrating CFP near-miss that felt more like a snub than a loss. The conference is watching its coaching carousel closely, knowing one bad hire can send a program sideways for three years.

## Calendar Moment
Spring games are wrapping up and the real conversation is already about who emerges from fall camp as the legitimate MWC title favorite — Boise State, UNLV, and a Fresno State squad with something to prove are all circling each other like they know August can't come fast enough.
