## program_history

Air Force football's all-time peak was Fisher DeBerry's 1985 12-1 + Bluebonnet Bowl championship + #5 final AP rank. Modern peak is Troy Calhoun's 2007-present tenure — multiple bowl appearances, Mountain West championship contention, sustained Commander-in-Chief's Trophy competition (20+ wins). The triple-option offense identity (continuous since the Ben Martin era 1958-1977) is permanent program scheme. Falcon Stadium (1962) sits at 6,621 ft.

## fanbase_summary

Falcon fans are Colorado-Springs-Falcon-loyalists. The Cadet Wing + alumni network + military-family geographic dispersion + Colorado Rockies fanbase overlap create a fanbase that values service-academy mission alignment over conventional wins-only loyalty. The Commander-in-Chief's Trophy annual competition + Falcon Stadium altitude advantage + Troy Calhoun longevity anchor the program identity.

## rivalry_context

Army (the Commander-in-Chief's Trophy triangle partner, since 1972) is the structural service-academy rival. Navy (the Commander-in-Chief's Trophy triangle partner, since 1972) is the structural service-academy rival. The Mountain West conference rotation brings San Diego State + Boise State + Fresno State as annual partners. The service-academy axis (Army-Navy-Air Force) is permanent.
