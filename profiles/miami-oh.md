## program_history

Miami (OH) football's defining historical legacy is the 'Cradle of Coaches' coaching lineage — Paul Brown (1928-1929 player) → Woody Hayes (1949-1950 HC) → Ara Parseghian (1951-1955 HC, 1955 9-0 Tangerine Bowl championship) → Bo Schembechler (1963-1968 HC) — four CFB Hall of Fame head coaches who developed at Miami before leading larger programs. The four-coach lineage shaped modern CFB at Ohio State + Michigan + Notre Dame. Modern peak is the 2024 9-4 + Sun Bowl appearance under Chuck Martin.

## fanbase_summary

RedHawk fans are Oxford-Love-and-Honor-RedHawks. The Cradle of Coaches institutional memory + Victory Bell rivalry + Love and Honor post-game alma mater + Yager Stadium MAC venue create a fanbase that values heritage equal to wins. The In-State Ohio identity vs Ohio State / Cincinnati / Ohio (the other MAC Ohio program) makes the program's positioning unique.

## rivalry_context

Cincinnati (the Victory Bell, since 1888 — the oldest non-Ivy rivalry in CFB) is the structural Ohio fixture across MAC era + Cincinnati's Big 12 entry. Ohio (the Battle of the Bricks, since 1908) is the In-State MAC fixture. Bowling Green / Toledo / NIU / Akron / Buffalo are the modern MAC cycle annual partners.
