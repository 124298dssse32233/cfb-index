## program_history

Kansas State football's transformation under Bill Snyder is one of CFB's most-cited rebuild stories. Pre-1989 K-State was widely cited as "the worst program in FBS history" — 27 winning seasons in the program's first 89 years. Snyder's two tenures (1989-2005 and 2009-2018) produced two Big 12 titles (2003 + 2012), the stadium named in his honor, and a culture-anchor that survives him. Chris Klieman's 2019-present era continues the Snyder foundation.

## fanbase_summary

Wildcat fans are Manhattan-purple-patient-loyalists. EMAW is the universal identity marker; Wabash Cannonball is the auditory signature. K-State's location in Manhattan, Kansas (population 55K) concentrates the regional fanbase in a way no Big 12 peer matches.

## rivalry_context

Kansas (the Sunflower Showdown, "Dillons Sunflower Showdown") is the in-state structural rival — basketball Jayhawks dominate the broader narrative, but football K-State has the upper hand. Iowa State (Big 12 partner since 1996) is the secondary fixture. Realignment continues to reshape Big 12 dynamics.
