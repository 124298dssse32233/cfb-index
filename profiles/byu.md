## program_history

BYU football's 1984 LaVell Edwards national championship is the program's only major peak and one of the most improbable titles in college football history. Edwards' 257-101 career record built the program from a Mountain West underdog into a Quarterback U identity (Ty Detmer Heisman 1990, Steve Young 1983, Jim McMahon 1981). The 2023 Big 12 move was the structural pivot — escape independent scheduling, embrace P5 weight-class.

## fanbase_summary

BYU fans are LDS-faithful-patient. The Honor Code defines who plays for the program; the religious identity shapes recruiting, transfer market participation, and weekly content. LaVell Edwards Stadium has consistent sellouts despite being in the smallest market of any P5 program. National brand strength via LDS diaspora is unique in CFB.

## rivalry_context

Utah (Holy War) is the structural rivalry — same state, opposite religious-cultural identity, Big 12 conference partners now. Utah State (Beehive Boot) is the secondary rivalry. The 2023 Big 12 move reshaped every meaningful BYU rivalry context.
