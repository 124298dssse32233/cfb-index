## 1. The North Star (the seat we claim)

> **CFB Index is the repository of college football's story — across day, week, month, season, and era — with editorial conviction, data calibration, and a fanbase-aware voice that no competitor has built.**

The competitive map as it stands May 2026:

- **ESPN / CBS / Fox** — real-time, no synthesis, no historical depth
- **The Athletic** — world-class writing, no data layer or longitudinal memory
- **Sports Reference / CFB Reference** — definitive history, zero current pulse, zero voice
- **247Sports / On3 / Rivals** — recruiting-only, season-only horizon
- **Action Network / VSiN** — betting-only
- **SP+ / FEI / Football Outsiders** — brilliant single-metric depth, no breadth
- **Bill Connelly / Bud Elliott / Spencer Hall** — closest analog, but each is one person, not a system
- **r/CFB** — collective intelligence, totally ephemeral

**Nobody owns the seat of:** data + voice + fanbase memory + structural truth + market belief + calibration ledger, across all time horizons, with the editorial conviction of an opinion column.

That's the seat. The product's one-sentence pitch: *"Where every team stands, what every fanbase thinks, why it matters, and what the last hundred years told us would happen."*

## 2. Why CFB 2026 Specifically (the bespoke framing)

This is not "a sports data product." It is a CFB product, anchored to the specific conditions of mid-2026. Enforcement criteria for every PR:

- **Realignment is the era's main character.** Every page references the conference a team is *in now* and *just left* if applicable. The Pac-12 has a dignified archive, not a 404. The B1G's western expansion gets first-class storyline treatment.
- **The 13 CFP-era seasons are the editorial substrate.** Every team's era page tells its CFP-era story in three acts (Founding 2014–2020 / Transition 2021–2023 / Expansion 2024–). Pre-2014 is honored as inheritance, not first-party.
- **NIL is mature, not novel.** Five years in. Treated as a roster-construction input like scholarships.
- **The 12-team CFP path is the projection currency.** Every team's outlook is "your path to the bracket," not "your bowl projection."
- **The portal is roster strategy, not catastrophe.** Top portal classes are first-class news, equal to recruiting classes.
- **House settlement era acknowledged.** Revenue-sharing is real. Walk-on culture is changing. The voice acknowledges with adult honesty.
- **No NFL voice contamination.** CFB has its own rhythms (signing day, spring game, fall camp, media days, Iron Bowl week). The voice honors them on their own terms.
- **Fanbase voice is the moat.** Every team's editorial register matches how *that fanbase* actually talks. `never_use` lists per team. Alabama copy doesn't sound like Stanford copy.
- **G5 is fully covered, not patronized.** Boise's expectations, Memphis's recruiting ceiling, Liberty's quiet dominance get the same depth as P4.
- **HBCU programs first-class.** Jackson State, Howard, Grambling — full coverage. The Classic is the real season for these programs.

## 3. Posture Decision (locked)

**Decision: Posture A — Compound-ship through 2026, perfect launch 2027.**

Two postures were considered:

- **A — Compound-ship:** Every Saturday of the 2026 season, the live site visibly improves. Public momentum compounds. Editorial team gets reps under real readership. Calibration ledger accumulates real prediction outcomes from 2026 games — making the 2027 launch dramatically more credible. **The 2026 season IS the public beta.**
- **B — Quiet build:** Site stays at current capability publicly. All work goes into a private version. Single coordinated launch when ready. Forfeits 2026 season coverage as content.

Posture A wins on three grounds:
1. **The calibration ledger is the product's trust spine, and it requires public predictions to earn credibility.** If we don't publish projections for the 2026 season, the 2027 launch can't say "we called Texas A&M as a Fragile Contender on August 15; here's how it played out."
2. **CFB 2026 is unmissable content** — Year 2 of 12-team CFP, realignment cohering or fracturing, Saban-to-DeBoer year 2 verdict, Vandy's renaissance test, coaching carousel for 2027.
3. **Iterative shipping makes the perfect launch better, not worse.** Every shipped surface is one fewer surface to debug in the 2027 launch window.

## 4. The CFP Era Frame (data + editorial anchor)

**Owned-data horizon starts 2014** — first CFP season, 13 completed seasons through 2026. This is what we have first-party data for.

Three editorially-distinct sub-eras within the CFP era, each with its own narrative weight:

| Act | Span | Defining forces | Stories told |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Founding** | 2014–2020 | 4-team CFP, Bama-Clemson duopoly, pre-NIL, pre-portal, conferences mostly stable | Cardale Jones / Ohio State 2014; Bama's third dynasty; Clemson's two titles; LSU '19; COVID '20 |
| **Transition** | 2021–2023 | NIL legal (Jul 2021), portal explodes, Georgia ascends, ACC fractures | Georgia back-to-back; Caleb Williams Heisman; Texas A&M's #1 class fizzle; Michigan title; Harbaugh-to-NFL |
| **Expansion** | 2024–present | 12-team CFP, mass realignment (USC/UCLA/Oregon/Washington to B1G; Texas/OU to SEC), House settlement era | Inaugural 12-team field; the death of the Pac-12; CFP-as-revenue-machine |

Every team has a *position* in each act. Era pages tell the team's story across all three acts.

**Pre-2014 framing:**
- **Borrowed (1936–2013)** — AP poll era. Game results, polls, major awards from public sources (Sports Reference, CFB Reference). Backfilled in Phase 4.
- **Inherited (pre-1936)** — bowl results, major champions. Treated as canon-historical. Pre-1869 doesn't exist (game wasn't invented yet).

The visual aesthetic distinguishes the three: sharper colors and full chart density for Owned, lighter ink for Borrowed, archive-style typography for Inherited. The aesthetic signals what kind of claim is being made.

## 5. The Five-Horizon Time Model

Every entity on the site (team, player, coach, rivalry, conference, era) is queryable at five time zooms. Each zoom has its own voice register:

| Horizon | Window | Primary question | Refresh | Voice |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Pulse** | Last 72 hours | "What just happened, what's about to" | Hourly in-season; daily off | Reporter |
| **Beat** | 7-day window | "What changed this week" | Daily | Beat writer |
| **Arc** | 30-day window | "Where is this team's story bending" | Weekly | Columnist |
| **Season** | 1 cycle (Aug → Jan + offseason chapters) | "What was the story of this year" | Monthly | Essayist |
| **Era** | 5-, 10-, 25-, all-time | "How does this fit a century of football" | Annually | Historian |

A team page exposes the horizon as a control — a horizontal time-zoom selector. Same canvas, sliding time, different evidence window, different voice. UX implementation: cross-archetype navigation strip (not redesigned Dashboard).

## 6. The Five-Layer Signal Model

Every claim on the site comes from one or more of these five layers. Each layer has clear sources, clear destinations, clear update cadence:

### Layer 1 — Attention (numeric, identity-free, cheap)

What it measures: how much oxygen this entity is taking. No NLP required.

Sources: Wikipedia pageviews + edits, GDELT article counts, Google News volume, YouTube view-velocity on tracked channels, Spotify podcast chart position, Reddit submission counts, Bluesky post counts, SeatGeek get-in price + listing counts.

Destination table: `numeric_observations` (new, unified). Today the 7 numeric adapters silently fail because their target tables don't exist; the unification fixes this.

### Layer 2 — Conversation (text, requires NLP)

What it measures: what fans + media + recruits are *saying* and how they feel.

Six audience buckets (replacing the single `'national'` default that all 8,005 current rows carry):
- **fan** — per-team subreddits, alumni subs, city subs, team message boards
- **rival** — opposing-team-sub posts where the primary mentioned team is the home team
- **national** — r/CFB, national pods, national news, national Substacks
- **media** — beat writers, substacks, podcasts (metadata), local radio
- **recruit** — recruit's social + 247/On3/Rivals free-tier threads
- **insider** — verified-credibility paid-tier sources (Cowork-observed)

### Layer 3 — Markets (prediction markets + tickets)

What people pay money to express. Highest signal-to-noise.

Sources: Polymarket CFB contracts (running, 160 rows), Kalshi CFB (adapter exists), SeatGeek ticket demand (adapter exists), StubHub (not wired).

Markets are the gold-standard "respect" baseline. Respect Gap becomes much stronger if it's `fan_belief − market_implied_probability` instead of `fan_belief − conversation_sentiment_national`.

### Layer 4 — Structural (already populated, world-class)

Power ratings (32,618 rows), resume ratings (26,367), returning production, talent composite, recruiting class rank, transfer portal class, depth chart projections, NFL Draft pipeline (2,316), player game stats (1.3M).

This layer is the only one currently complete. Reality Gap reads against it and works fine.

### Layer 5 — Outcomes & Calibration (the trust spine)

What it measures: were past beliefs right?

`confidence_calibration` table exists with 5 rows. Grows into "for every player whose chip we ever published, did our belief match what happened" — and feeds back into the confidence band shown on every chip.

This is the killer feature. No competitor has it. Every published prediction logs to a `prediction_ledger`; outcome resolver runs weekly. Methodology page renders the live calibration history.

## 7. The Cohesion Principle

**For every published fact on the site, there is exactly one populator that writes it, exactly one table that stores it, exactly one renderer that reads it, and exactly one freshness check that gates it.**

If a fact renders in two places, both renderers read the same row. If a fact has two sources (e.g., archetype from classifier + archetype from profile YAML), one is canonical and the other validates against it. CI fails if they disagree without an explicit override.

This is the governance discipline that prevents the codebase from developing the "five parallel classification systems" pathology it already partially has. See `DECISIONS.md` for the consolidation plan.

## 11. Local LLM Architecture (the hardware-aware stack)

**Hardware:** Dell Alienware Aurora A1250 — Intel Core Ultra 7 265KF (20 cores), NVIDIA RTX 5070 (12 GB GDDR7, Blackwell, Gen 5 Tensor Cores, native FP4/FP8), 32 GB DDR5, 1 TB NVMe.

**Two-runtime architecture (May 2026 state of the art):**

- **vLLM** (primary, batch + production) — FP8 on Blackwell, EAGLE-3 speculative decoding. Serves Chronicle regeneration, mass classification, mass embedding. Continuous batching → ~19× throughput vs Ollama at 8 concurrent requests.
- **Ollama** (secondary, interactive + hot-swap) — Marquee model (Mistral Small 24B or Qwen3-30B-A3B MoE) hot-swapped in for profile drafting and era ledes. Small classifier (Qwen 2.5 1.5B) always warm.

**Model tier ladder:**

| Tier | Model | Format | VRAM | Speed | Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Marquee | Mistral Small 3.2 24B | Q3_K_M GGUF | ~11 GB | ~18 tok/s | Era pages, profile drafts |
| Marquee (alt) | Qwen3-30B-A3B (MoE) | Q4 + CPU expert offload | ~9 GB GPU + ~14 GB RAM | ~24 tok/s | 30B-class quality, faster |
| Standard | Qwen 2.5 14B | Q4_K_M or FP8 via vLLM | ~9 GB | 25–90 tok/s | Daily Chronicle workhorse |
| Reasoning | Qwen 3 8B | FP8 via vLLM | ~8 GB | ~80 tok/s | Voice validator, fact critic |
| Mass classification | Qwen 2.5 1.5B | Q5_K_M | ~1.5 GB | 200+ tok/s | Sentiment, banlist checking |
| Embeddings | Nomic Embed Text V2 | F16 CPU | ~0.3 GB RAM | CPU only | Semantic search index |
| Reranker | BGE-Reranker-v2-m3 | F16 GPU | ~1.3 GB | 50–100ms/query | RAG quality |
| Audio | distil-large-v3 via faster-whisper | — | ~4 GB | 6–8× real-time | Podcast ingest |
| Voice LoRA | Unsloth + Mistral-Nemo 12B base | QLoRA 4-bit | ~7 GB peak | 90 min for 5k examples | Per-archetype voice adapters |

**Voice LoRA is the killer feature for "bespoke to CFB."** Expand voice corpus from 329 → 1,500 passages (fair-use snippets from named CFB writers + podcasts). Train per-archetype LoRA adapters. Chronicle output routes through the matching adapter based on team archetype + season phase.

**Total stack cost:** ~$0 marginal beyond electricity. Cloud LLM fallback drops to $0–10/mo.

## 12. Chronicle Cards — Suppress, Heal, Regenerate

**Current state (May 2026):** 692 cards, 189 LKG (live on team pages). Every card is some variation of "Polymarket says team X has Y% to win 2027 CFP." Voice reads like Bloomberg. Repetitive. Editorially worthless in offseason.

**Root cause:** The pipeline is correctly working — it generates evidence-anchored narrative from available structured evidence. In offseason the *only* structured evidence flowing is 160 rows of Polymarket data. The problem isn't the pipeline; it's the evidence vacuum.

**Plan:**

1. **Suppress all current LKG cards immediately.** The team page is better with zero Chronicle cards than with 5 Polymarket variations.
2. **Diversify offseason evidence sources** — add depth chart deltas, transfer events, archetype transitions, recruiting class movement, coaching events to Chronicle's retriever.
3. **Expand voice corpus + train offseason-voice LoRA** to kill the "financial analyst" register.
4. **Add banlist entries** — "market sentiment," "remarkably stable," "wisdom of crowds," "trading volume," "consistent high volume," "betting volume."
5. **Reduce offseason card count** — 1–2 cards per team in offseason, 5–8 in-season, 10+ for marquee teams in November.
6. **Re-evaluate after Week 4 of the 2026 season.** That's when evidence diversity is real and Chronicle has a fair test.

## 13. What We Explicitly Refuse to Build

To prevent scope drift over a year. Every item on this list has been considered and rejected:

- **NFL Draft content beyond the CFB-to-NFL pipeline.** Don't become a draft site.
- **High school recruiting at top-500 depth.** Don't try to be 247Sports.
- **Live game tracking, win-probability animations, scoring updates.** ESPN owns this. Embed if needed; don't compete.
- **Highlight clips / video.** Rights are messy; editorial voice is text-and-chart-first. Stay text-and-chart-first.
- **Native app.** Web is fine.
- **Comments, user accounts, social features.** The product is a publication, not a community. Reddit and X are the community.
- **AI chat with the data.** Tempting, but the voice is the moat. A chatbot dilutes it.
- **Multi-sport expansion.** CFB is the bottomless well. Don't dilute. (Possible extension: NFL Draft as a CFB-pipeline projection — but not basketball, baseball, etc.)
- **Twitter/X integration.** Post-2024 API regime is hostile and expensive. Bluesky covers the CFB community sufficiently.
- **Discord scraping.** Audience unrepresentative + ToS murky.
- **Pop-up newsletter solicitation, ads, paywalls (for now).** Earn the audience first.
- **TTS / podcast generation.** Text-and-chart-first.

## 14. The Cohesion CI Gates

Automated checks that catch drift across systems. Each one fails the build if violated:

1. **Profile-classifier consistency** — every profile YAML's archetype matches classifier output, or has explicit override.
2. **Cohort-list singleness** — no Python file imports from old cohort sets (`PROFILED_SLUGS`, `BLUEBLOOD_PROGRAMS`, `priority_teams.yaml` direct read) after migration.
3. **Chart vocabulary lock** — no `def render_*_chart` outside `src/cfb_rankings/charts/`. Lint catches.
4. **Receipt-pattern enforcement** — every Chronicle output card has ≥1 receipt per 200 words. Validator catches at write time.
5. **Freshness gates** — every published chip has a max-staleness threshold. Smoke test fails if any tier-0 chip is stale beyond threshold.
6. **Voice consistency** — every team's rendered output passes `voice_validator` against that team's `never_use` list.
7. **Adapter freshness** — every adapter has been successful within its expected cadence window. CI alerts.

## 17. Editorial Rhythm (Day-of-Week + Season-Phase)

The codebase has `_DOW_LABEL` and `_MONTH_TO_PHASE` enums in `state_resolver.py`. They drive `PageState` at render time. But the *editorial intent* for each day/phase has lived in conversations and informal docs. This section locks the rhythm.

**Source of truth:** [docs/editorial-rhythm.md](docs/editorial-rhythm.md) (detailed spec, to be created in Phase 1).

### 17.1 In-Season Day-of-Week Rhythm (Aug–Jan)

Each day has a distinct editorial archetype. Bespoke content per day.

| Day | Phase label | Editorial focus | Auto-published surfaces | Editor review |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | licking-wounds-or-basking | Game autopsy — what we learned, what changed in beliefs | Wire (Monday autopsy), per-team mood-swing chips, archetype-transition events from the weekend | Mailbag prep |
| **Tue** | depth-chart-injuries | Injury reports + depth chart deltas | Wire (Tuesday depth), updated `player_depth_chart_2026` deltas, NFL-pipeline chips refreshed | None |
| **Wed** | matchup-sharpens | Upcoming-game matchup analysis | Wire (Wednesday matchup), Chronicle `matchup_echo` cards for upcoming-game rivalries | None |
| **Thu** | hype-peaks | Thursday-night games + Friday-game previews | Wire (Thursday hype), Chronicle pre-game cards for Thursday games | None |
| **Fri** | anticipation | Saturday-morning preview, Mailbag drops | Wire (Friday set), **Mailbag publishes 9am ET** | Mailbag final review (Thursday night) |
| **Sat** | gameday | Live game-day coverage (minimal — ESPN owns this) | Live attention chips, win-probability spark updates if a model is plugged in; **does NOT do play-by-play** | None |
| **Sun** | autopsy | Post-game recap + week-wide synthesis | Daily (Sunday recap), updated mood data, market-belief delta chips, **storyline candidate queue surfaces for editor review** | Sunday editor review window |

**Why this rhythm:** Mirrors how fans experience the week — Monday is for reckoning with Saturday's result, Tuesday/Wednesday is for processing the depth chart and the upcoming opponent, Thursday/Friday is anticipation, Saturday is the game, Sunday is the week-wide view. Every day's surface answers the question fans are actually asking that day.

**Offseason editorial cadence:**
- Wire: M/W/F (not daily — there's not enough news to justify daily)
- Mailbag: Weekly (Friday 9am ET)
- Storyline chapters: bi-weekly

- Era pages: rolling Phase 2-3 publishes

### 17.4 The transition windows

Two transitions matter most:

**Offseason → in-season transition (Aug 23 ± 2 weeks):**
- Phase swap: "Fanbase Awareness" chips → "Fan Belief" chips (per D-009)
- Wire cadence: M/W/F → daily
- Chronicle generation cadence: weekly → daily during game weeks
- Calibration ledger: zeroed for new season

**In-season → offseason transition (mid-Jan):**
- Phase swap reverses
- Wire cadence: daily → M/W/F
- Chronicle: focus shifts from game-evidence cards to portal/recruiting/heritage cards
- Annual calibration report published ("Here's how we did in 2026")

## 19. Autonomous Operation Model

The site runs largely autonomously each quarter. User can override anything. Different flow for in-season vs offseason. This is the operational discipline that makes the 12-month vision tractable for one editor + one engineer (effectively the user + Claude).

### 19.1 What runs autonomously

**Without human review (auto-publish):**
- Ingest workflows (hourly, daily, weekly crons)
- Classifier outputs (fanbase archetypes, narrative-arc transitions, player archetypes)
- Numeric observations (Wikipedia, GDELT, markets, etc.)
- Chip-level renders (attention chip, archetype chip, Reality Gap, etc.) when confidence_band ≥ medium
- Calibration ledger writes (every prediction logged automatically)
- Chronicle cards that pass voice_validator + fact_critic + receipt-density gate at confidence_band ≥ medium
- Wire daily auto-summary (built from Chronicle cards + numeric_observations deltas)
- Era page lede regenerations on quarterly cron

**Goes to review queue (default-publish if not flagged in 24h):**
- Chronicle cards at confidence_band = low or fact_critic < 0.75
- Storyline chapter drafts (Sunday editor review)
- Profile drafts (always reviewed before merge per D-011)
- Calibration anomalies (model accuracy drops >10pp week-over-week)

**Requires human approval (does NOT auto-publish):**
- Profile creation/replacement (every profile reviewed at merge)
- New page archetype templates
- DECISIONS.md additions
- Refuse-list changes
- Any chart-vocabulary additions
- Anything that touches the voice_validator banlist

### 19.2 Override mechanism

The user sees auto-published content via:
- **Daily digest email** (Sunday + Friday at minimum, ideally daily in-season): "Auto-published yesterday: X Wire posts, Y Chronicle cards, Z archetype transitions. Anything to flag?"
- **`/retract <url>` one-shot:** flags the content, removes from live site, opens an issue to investigate. One command.
- **`/amend <url> "fix description"`:** marks content for human-revised reissue.
- **`/freeze <workstream>`:** pauses auto-publish for that workstream until explicitly unfrozen.

### 19.3 Quarterly autonomous-operations review

Once a quarter (Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec): structured review of what auto-published over the past 90 days.

**The review answers:**
- Calibration accuracy per model — within tolerance?
- Voice drift — any unflagged off-voice content slipped through?
- Receipt density — any editorial pages drifted below 1/200?
- Adapter freshness — any silent failures?
- Cost — within $25-65/mo envelope?
- New DECISIONS to log based on patterns seen?

**The review output:** an entry appended to DECISIONS.md if anything meta-decisional changed, plus a STATUS.md "quarterly review" note. Auto-publish thresholds get retuned if needed (a tighter `fact_critic` gate, looser `confidence_band` requirement, etc.).

### 19.4 In-season vs offseason cadence (different flows)

**In-season autonomous flow (Aug 23 – mid-Jan):**
- Hourly: ingest + sentiment classification + numeric observations
- 4am ET daily: Wire publish + freshness page update + Chronicle T0 cards for top games
- 9am ET Friday: Mailbag publish
- Sunday evening: Daily synthesis + storyline candidate queue + weekly calibration report
- Saturday during games: minute-by-minute attention chip updates (no play-by-play — ESPN owns)

**Offseason autonomous flow (mid-Jan – Aug 22):**
- 6h: ingest cadence relaxes from hourly (saves Actions minutes)
- M/W/F: Wire publishes (not daily)
- Weekly Friday: Mailbag publishes
- Bi-weekly: storyline chapters
- Monthly: era page lede regenerations + archetype backtest review
- Quarterly: full autonomous-operations review

### 19.5 The trust threshold

Auto-publish is only as trustworthy as the gates. Three pre-conditions for going fully autonomous:

1. **Calibration ledger has ≥3 months of resolved predictions** showing the model is within stated confidence bands. Until then, all Chronicle output stays in review queue.
2. **Voice_validator + chronicle_banlist coverage** at every editorial surface (not just Chronicle). Phase 1 work.
3. **Override workflow tested end-to-end** at least once for each retract/amend/freeze command.

Until those three hold, auto-publish only covers chip-level renders (low-risk numeric facts), not editorial cards.

### 19.6 What this means for the user

When fully operational:
- **In-season:** User reviews a daily digest (~5 min). Flags 1-2 items per week typically. Otherwise the site runs.
- **Offseason:** User reviews weekly digest (~10 min). Flags ~1 item per month typically.
- **Quarterly:** User joins the 90-day autonomous-operations review (~1-2 hours). Adjusts thresholds. Approves new DECISIONS entries.

Effort budget: **~30 min/week in-season, ~15 min/week offseason, ~2 hours/quarter for the review.** Compatible with the 9-14 month timeline running alongside other commitments.

## 23. The One Honest Caveat

This doc is opinionated and detailed. It is also May 2026's best understanding of a 12-month plan. Reality will move; some decisions in this doc will turn out wrong. Two disciplines keep it useful:

- **`DECISIONS.md` is append-only.** Don't edit past decisions. Add new entries that say "previously decided X for reason Y; reality showed Z; new decision is W."
- **Quarterly master plan revision is allowed and expected.** Don't keep this doc static. Re-version it (`VISION_2026_2027_v2.md`) and archive prior. Carry forward what's still right.

Plans that try to predict everything fail. Plans that capture current best understanding + an update cadence survive.
