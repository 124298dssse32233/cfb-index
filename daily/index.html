1Take #1 — Top Story

Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is the Recruiting Story of the Week, But Oregon Just Made the Quietly Terrifying Move

Five-star WR Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State is going to get all the headlines — and it should, the kid is a problem — but don't sleep on Immanuel Iheanacho flipping to Oregon. 247Sports' Brandon Huffman flagged Iheanacho as one of the most physically dominant interior linemen in this cycle, and he's now sitting in Dan Lanning's room. An elite five-star IOL commit doesn't move the casual-fan needle the way a receiver with a famous last name does, but ask any offensive line coach in the country what controls a Big Ten season in November and they'll tell you it's the trenches, every single time.

Meanwhile, On3's Jamie Shaw noted that Ohio State's backend additions this offseason already had the defense stabilized — adding Henry Jr. to that roster is the kind of thing that makes rival coordinators genuinely miserable in their film sessions.

Notre Dame landing Ian Premer at TE keeps Marcus Freeman's class rolling, too.

2Take #2 — Two Reads

Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is the Stat Nerds vs. Die-Hards Battle Nobody Saw Coming

The recruiting composite crowd is treating Chris Henry Jr.'s commitment to Ohio State like a spreadsheet win — another five-star checkbox, another Rivals/On3 bump that inches the Buckeyes toward a top-three class. That read isn't wrong. It's just incomplete.

The die-hards watching Ryan Day's actual roster construction see something weirder and more interesting: Ohio State just added a receiver with generational bloodlines and a highlight reel that makes defensive coordinators rethink their career choices, onto a depth chart that already had a receiver room most programs would trade their facilities budget for. Pete Nakos at On3 noted the commitment "immediately reshapes the Big Ten's 2026 recruiting landscape." Meanwhile, Bill Bender at The Sporting News flagged that Day is clearly building around a spread-and-shred identity heading into the CFP era.

The absurd part? Iowa just landed a quarterback commit the same week. Tradon Bessinger to Iowa. Both matter. Neither feels real in the same universe.

Watch whether Alabama — fresh off landing five-star safety Jireh Edwards — makes a counter-move at receiver this week.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

Ohio State Landing Chris Henry Jr. Is the Big Ten's Buried Story This Week

Everyone's talking about Oregon's Iheanacho commit locking down the trenches for the Ducks, and fair enough — as Stephen Means at The Athletic noted in his Big Ten recruiting breakdown, interior line depth is the quiet separator in this conference. But the move that actually reshapes the landscape is Chris Henry Jr. choosing Columbus.

Here's the buried lede: Ohio State already addressed the back end through the portal — two top-12 DB transfers, documented right there in the fan sentiment data — and now they're stacking elite receiving talent on top of that defensive foundation. That's not rebuilding. That's reloading in real time while everyone else is still arguing about schemes.

Shehan Jeyarajah at CBS Sports has been tracking how Ryan Day's staff is threading the needle between portal urgency and prep recruiting. Henry Jr. is Exhibit A that they haven't lost the room on either front.

Meanwhile, Alabama quietly grabbing five-star safety Jireh Edwards while the buyout math circulates in group chats is a very on-brand DeBoer move — keep signing, don't blink.

Watch the 247Sports team rankings update Thursday. The gap between Ohio State and everyone else in the Big Ten is about to get visually uncomfortable.
