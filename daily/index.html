1Take #1 — Top Story

Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is the Headline, But the Real Story Is What's Happening to the Big Ten Recruiting Map All at Once

Oregon landed five-star IOL Immanuel Iheanacho. Ohio State grabbed five-star WR Chris Henry Jr. — yes, the son of the late Chris Henry Sr. — and Notre Dame locked up five-star TE Ian Premer. All in the same overnight window. The Big Ten and its allied programs are just hoovering up the 2026 class in real time, and the rest of college football is watching it happen.

On Henry specifically, 247Sports' Brandon Huffman noted the pick was a major blow to several SEC programs that had invested heavily in his recruitment. Meanwhile, The Athletic's Ari Wasserman flagged that Ohio State's receiver room now has the kind of five-star density that makes spring depth charts feel like formalities.

The absurd part? Oregon also won the interior line battle with Iheanacho, which means Dan Lanning is building the 2026 class like he's playing Dynasty mode with the sliders maxed.

Watch the Alabama and Georgia offer boards today — both programs will respond.

2Take #2 — Two Reads

Ohio State's Chris Henry Jr. Commit Breaks Differently Depending on Which Tab You Have Open

The stat crowd and the die-hards are genuinely living in parallel universes on this one. For the 247Sports composite obsessives, Chris Henry Jr. arriving in Columbus is a clean algorithmic win — five stars, elite separation metrics, the kind of profile that moves team rankings before he touches a practice field. On247, he's a number that improves Ohio State's class ceiling measurably overnight.

But the message-board faithful aren't looking at composites. They're asking why now, and what it means for the receivers already in Ryan Day's room. Stewart Mandel noted at The Athletic that Ohio State's offensive rebuild is leaning heavily on transfer continuity, which makes a high-profile true freshman WR commitment feel either redundant or visionary depending on your mood. Pete Nakos at On3 has tracked Henry Jr.'s recruitment as one of the more deliberately paced processes of this cycle — this wasn't an impulse flip.

College football is absurd enough that both reads can be correct simultaneously. Watch how OSU's existing receiver depth chart shifts over the next week — that movement will tell you which version of this commit actually matters.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

Ohio State Landing Chris Henry Jr. Is the Big Ten Story Everyone's Sleeping On

The flashiest commit of the last 24 hours isn't the one getting the most airtime. Yes, Ian Premer to Notre Dame is a massive tight end haul, and Oregon snagging five-star IOL Immanuel Iheanacho is a genuinely program-altering get — Bill Connelly noted on X that elite interior line recruiting is "the quietest predictor of sustained Big Ten competitiveness." But Ohio State landing five-star WR Chris Henry Jr. is the buried lede here. Chris Henry Jr. — son of the late Chris Henry — is one of the most emotionally charged recruiting stories in recent memory, and the Buckeyes won it. That's not just a 247Sports ranking bump. That's Ryan Day reloading a receiver room that just sent Emeka Egbuka to the NFL, with a kid who carries genuine symbolic weight for the program. Greg Biggins at 247Sports called him "arguably the best receiver in the 2026 class." Ohio State fans should be losing their minds right now, and somehow the discourse is calm. Watch whether this flips momentum in the Big Ten class rankings by end of week — Oregon and Michigan aren't standing still.
