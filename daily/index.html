1Take #1 — Top Story

Oregon Is Running a Recruiting Heist in Broad Daylight

Dan Lanning didn't just have a good Saturday on the trail — he had the kind of day that makes rival recruiting coordinators stare at the ceiling at 2 a.m. Five-star EDGE Anthony Jones is a Duck, and when you stack that on top of four-star RB Tradarian Ball, four-star IOL Tommy Tofi, and four-star S Devin Jackson all landing in the same window, you're not looking at a recruiting class anymore. You're looking at a statement. On247Sports, the Jones commitment was flagged immediately as a class-reshaping get for the Big Ten, and Brandon Huffman noted the Ducks' ability to pull Jones over a loaded SEC offer sheet as evidence that Oregon's brand ceiling is "genuinely undefined right now." Meanwhile, Pete Nakos at On3 pointed out that this class is already threatening top-five nationally, which — for a program that was playing in the Pac-12 two years ago — remains objectively absurd.

Notre Dame quietly landed five-star TE Ian Premer in the same 24-hour stretch and barely got the airtime it deserved.

Watch Oregon's class ranking on the composite through Tuesday — it's moving.

2Take #2 — Two Reads

Oregon's Recruiting Haul Looks Different Depending on Whether You Trust the Stars

The stat crowd and the die-hards are staring at the same Oregon weekend and reaching completely different conclusions. Recruiting analytics people are going wild — five-star EDGE Anthony Jones plus three additional commits in a 48-hour window is the kind of class-building velocity that moves composite rankings in real time. 247Sports has Oregon's 2026 class jumping into the national conversation with this cluster, and that's before Dan Lanning presumably makes another phone call.

The die-hards, though? They're doing the math on retention. As Brandon Huffman at 247Sports has documented repeatedly, Dan Lanning's program cycles portal entrants through Eugene at a pace that makes roster continuity genuinely hard to project. Meanwhile, Pete Nakos at On3 noted earlier this cycle that high-floor recruiting classes mean nothing if your portal door swings both directions equally hard.

Both reads are correct, which is exactly the absurdity of modern CFB roster management — you're essentially recruiting a two-year window, not a program.

Watch whether Jones's commitment triggers any decommitments from Big Ten programs chasing the same EDGE archetype this week.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

Oregon Is Quietly Building the Big Ten's Most Dangerous Roster and Nobody's Panicking About It Yet

The headline was Anthony Jones — five-star EDGE, the kind of commit that moves the needle in any conference — but the buried lede from Oregon's last 24 hours is the volume. Jones plus four-star RB Tradarian Ball, four-star IOL Tommy Tofi, and four-star S Devin Jackson landed in the same news cycle. That's not recruiting, that's restocking a hardware store. 247Sports' composite board will update shortly, and Oregon's class is going to look uncomfortably complete for a program that already went to the CFP.

The Big Ten talent consolidation story keeps accelerating. As The Athletic's Ari Wasserman has noted in tracking the conference's recruiting geography shifts, the footprint programs can now pull from has fundamentally changed post-expansion. Oregon is the proof of concept.

Meanwhile Notre Dame reeled in five-star TE Ian Premer the same day, which — fine, yes, absurd — this sport just does this now.

Watch Oregon's composite class ranking when the services refresh Monday. If they crack top three nationally, the "who stops the Ducks" conversation gets a lot louder in Indy.
