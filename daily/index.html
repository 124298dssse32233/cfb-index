1Take #1 — Top Story

No Wire, No Problem — The Offseason Is Doing Its Thing Anyway

Here's the honest truth about a Wednesday in mid-May: the sport is running on fumes and vibes right now, and anyone telling you otherwise is selling something. The portal window has cooled to a simmer, spring games are in the rearview, and the depth charts that matter won't drop until August camps crack open.

That said, the offseason never actually stops — it just goes underground. Stewart Mandel noted in The Athletic recently that this particular stretch, mid-May through Memorial Day, is historically when the quietest roster decisions get made, the ones that don't generate press releases but absolutely show up in fall two-deeps. Meanwhile, Pete Nakos at On3 has been tracking a handful of unsigned transfers still sitting in the portal who represent real leverage for programs with open scholarships.

The absurdity is real: we are analyzing the absence of news and calling it content. Welcome to college football in 2026.

What to watch: Portal activity from programs that closed spring with obvious positional holes — those decisions tend to cluster in the back half of May. Check the offer trackers Thursday morning.

2Take #2 — Two Reads

The Stat Guys and the Die-Hards Are Looking at the Same Offseason and Seeing Two Completely Different Sports

The gap between analytics Twitter and message-board nation has never felt wider heading into summer, and honestly, that tension is half the fun of following this sport. Stat-first folks are leaning on SP+ projections and returning production numbers to sort out who's actually positioned to compete — the kind of work Bill Connelly has been doing at The Athletic that turns preseason chaos into something resembling signal. Meanwhile, the die-hards are watching practice reports, reading body language into every coordinator quote, and deciding games based on vibes that no model is capturing.

Neither camp is wrong, which is the most college football sentence possible. Stewart Mandel noted recently that the disconnect between advanced metrics and fan perception is "wider than it's been in years" — and that friction is going to define how fanbases process every roster move between now and August camp.

The number to watch this week: which programs are adding portal pieces that satisfy both crowds simultaneously. Those are the rosters quietly building something real.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

The Offseason Silence Is Louder Than It Looks

No wire entries. No pulse spikes. No surprise receipts. In mid-May, that quiet isn't boring — it's actually the signal. Every program that matters right now is doing its real work behind closed doors, and the gaps in the news cycle are where roster construction either gets locked in or falls apart. This is the week coaches finalize their summer depth chart logic before fall camp planning kicks into gear, and the schools that are slow-playing their portal decisions right now are going to feel that crunch hard in August.

Stewart Mandel wrote in The Athletic earlier this spring that the programs consistently winning the offseason "information war" are the ones operating with zero public urgency. That tracks. Meanwhile, Bruce Feldman has repeatedly flagged that late-May is when transfer portal commitments that didn't get announced quietly disappear — guys decommit, re-enter, and nobody notices until fall camp rosters drop.

Watch portal tracker activity through Friday. The quiet programs are either very good or very bad at this. You won't know which until it's too late to care.
