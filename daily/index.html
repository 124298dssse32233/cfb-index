1Take #1 — Top Story

Oregon Is Quietly Building a Monster and the Big Ten Should Be Paying Attention

Dan Lanning didn't just land a recruit overnight — he landed a statement. Five-star EDGE Anthony Jones committed to Oregon, and if you've been watching this class stack up, you already know what's happening in Eugene. On3's Charles Power called Jones "the most complete edge prospect in the 2026 cycle," and that's not recruiting-service inflation talking — that's a player who could start Day 1 in a Power Four conference. Stack that on top of four-star RB Tradarian Ball, four-star IOL Tommy Tofi, and four-star safety Devin Jackson all dropping commitments in the same window, and Lanning is running a conveyor belt, not a recruiting board.

247Sports' Brandon Huffman noted Oregon's class is already "trending toward a top-five national finish," which would be absurd to say out loud three years ago and is completely unsurprising now.

The Big Ten arms race is real and Oregon keeps bringing ammunition.

Watch whether a defensive back or pass rusher flips off a rival board this week — this class has room and momentum to steal somebody.

2Take #2 — Two Reads

Oregon's Recruiting Haul Looks Different Depending on Which Lens You're Using

The stat crowd and the die-hards are not watching the same film on Oregon's week. The analytics guys are running composite scores and screaming about class ranking jumps — five-star EDGE Anthony Jones plus the four-star depth at RB, IOL, and safety in a single news cycle is genuinely absurd roster construction. On 247Sports, On3, and every composite aggregator, Oregon just moved needles. But the die-hards on the boards are asking the harder question: can Dan Lanning hold these guys when December roster chaos hits?

That tension is real. As Pete Nakos at On3 has documented repeatedly, elite recruiting classes and portal attrition are not mutually exclusive anymore — they feed each other. And Stewart Mandel noted in The Athletic that Big Ten programs are increasingly judged not on signing day but on who's still there in August.

Jones at EDGE is the headliner, full stop. Four commits around him signal program momentum, not a fluke.

Watch this week whether any of Oregon's current roster depth at the affected positions starts generating transfer portal noise. That's the read-through that actually matters.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

Oregon Is Quietly Building a Monster, and the Big Ten Should Be Uncomfortable

Four commits in one cycle — five-star EDGE Anthony Jones, four-star RB Tradarian Ball, four-star IOL Tommy Tofi, four-star S Devin Jackson — and the casual fan sees a good recruiting weekend. What they miss is the architecture. Jones isn't just a pass rusher; he's the kind of edge piece that makes an entire defense's alignment decisions easier. Pair that with interior line depth via Tofi and a ball-carrier in Ball, and Dan Lanning is stacking a roster that can bully you in November when the Willamette Valley turns into a swamp. Brandon Huffman at 247Sports noted Oregon's class is ascending toward top-five nationally, and Pete Nakos at On3 flagged Jones specifically as the headliner who changes Oregon's ceiling for 2027 and beyond. The absurd part? The Ducks are doing this while everyone still debates whether the Big Ten is their real home. It is now. Watch whether Ohio State or Penn State responds on the trail this week — because someone in that conference just blinked.
