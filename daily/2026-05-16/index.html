Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is the Recruiting Story of the Week, But Don't Sleep on What Oregon and Notre Dame Just Did

Five-star WR Chris Henry Jr. committing to Ohio State is the loudest bell ringing this morning, and rightfully so — the Buckeyes just added a name that carries weight before he's taken a college snap. On3's recruiting analysts flagged the commitment as an immediate Big Ten shakeup, and that tracks. Ryan Day has been building a receiving room that doesn't ask you to squint to see the ceiling.

But here's the thing: Oregon landing five-star IOL Immanuel Iheanacho in the same 24-hour window is the quiet monster of this cycle. The Athletic's Ari Wasserman has noted repeatedly that the trenches decide Big Ten titles, full stop. Iheanacho is exactly the kind of get that makes a room. Meanwhile, Notre Dame locked up five-star TE Ian Premer, which — yes, Marcus Freeman is doing that thing where he just keeps stacking skill positions and acting like it's normal.

CFB recruiting in May is objectively unhinged. Watch how Ohio State's WR portal activity adjusts now that Henry Jr. is in the building. That ripple hits fast.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

Ohio State Just Solved Its WR Room and Nobody's Talking About It

The buried lede from this 24-hour recruiting window isn't the Notre Dame tight end or Oregon's interior line add — it's Chris Henry Jr. committing to Ohio State, and what that means for a program that already raided the portal for defensive backs. The Buckeyes quietly built a complete offseason roster renovation while everyone was watching the NIL drama elsewhere.

Henry Jr. is the name here. His father's legacy carries weight, but his tape carries more — he's a legitimate separator at the catch point, exactly the profile Ohio State needs behind Jeremiah Smith when defenses decide to bracket the best receiver in college football. As Jake Moody noted on The Athletic's recruiting tracker, the Buckeyes' '26 class is shaping into one that addresses "positional redundancy at premium spots," which is dynasty-speak for sustainable depth.

Meanwhile, Stewart Mandel flagged Ohio State's defensive back portal movement as the off-the-radar story of the spring. Now add Henry Jr. on offense. This program is constructing a two-deep that looks like an NFL roster sheet.

Watch how Alabama responds — Edwards commits to Tuscaloosa, but their wide receiver board is still incomplete heading into the summer evaluation period.
