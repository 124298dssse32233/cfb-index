1Take #1 — Top Story

Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is the Recruiting Story of the Week, But Oregon's IOL Grab Is the One That Actually Moves the Board

Five-star WR Chris Henry Jr. committing to Ohio State will get the clicks — the name carries weight for obvious, painful reasons, and the Buckeyes adding another elite skill piece to an already loaded offense is the kind of news that makes Big Ten defensive coordinators stare at the ceiling. But the move that deserves more conversation overnight is five-star IOL Immanuel Iheanacho choosing Oregon. Dan Lanning is quietly building the trenches into something genuinely scary, and 247Sports' Brandon Huffman has tracked Oregon's offensive line class trajectory all cycle — this one slots as the crown jewel. Meanwhile, Alabama landing five-star S Jireh Edwards keeps DeBoer's class afloat while the buyout math circulates on group chats, as On3's Jeremy Crabtree noted the secondary was the program's most urgent need entering this evaluation period. Three elite commits in one news cycle is peak May absurdity, and we are absolutely here for it.

2Take #2 — Two Reads

Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is Either the Exclamation Point or the Decoy — Depends Who You Ask

The stat crowd and the die-hards are reading this commit in completely opposite directions, and honestly, both arguments hold up.

Analytics types see a program that already leads the Big Ten in returning receiver production and now adds a five-star name on top of it — potential redundancy dressed up as a trophy. On the boards, though, it's pure oxygen. This is Chris Henry Jr., legacy recruit, five-star wideout, and Ohio State just locked him down over everyone.

Steve Wiltfong at 247Sports noted the crystal ball consensus swung hard toward Columbus in the final 48 hours — that kind of momentum doesn't just happen. And Pete Nakos at On3 flagged the Henry commitment as the headliner in what's shaping up to be Ohio State's most receiver-heavy class in the NIL era.

The absurd part? The Buckeyes might have too many weapons, which is genuinely a sentence you can write in 2026 without laughing. Watch whether any current commits quietly reopen their recruitment now that the room got even more crowded.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

Ohio State's Chris Henry Jr. Commit Is the Headliner, But Oregon's Iheanacho Is the Buried Lede

Five-star WR Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State is the move everyone's talking about — and it deserves the noise. But scroll past it on the recruiting tracker and you'll find the commit that actually reshapes Big Ten power dynamics for the next four years: five-star IOL Immanuel Iheanacho to Oregon.

Here's why that matters. Oregon's offensive line was the one thing keeping Dan Lanning's program from being a genuine national title threat, not just a College Football Playoff participant. Pete Nakos at On3 has been tracking Iheanacho as arguably the top interior lineman in the 2026 class. Brandon Huffman at 247Sports flagged Oregon's offensive front as the program's most critical roster need heading into the cycle. Iheanacho doesn't just fill that hole — he anchors it.

Meanwhile, Alabama landing five-star S Jireh Edwards is exactly the kind of quiet infrastructure move that makes DeBoer's rebuilding argument more credible than the booster group chats will admit.

Watch Oregon's next three offensive line offers this week. If Lanning doubles down on the trenches, the Ducks are building something scarier than their record suggests.
