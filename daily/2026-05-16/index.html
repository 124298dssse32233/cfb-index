1Take #1 — Top Story

The Offseason Vacuum Is Real, and That's Actually the Story

Here's the thing about a Friday morning in mid-May with zero wire movement and no pulse spikes: the silence is the news. We're in the dead center of the dead zone — post-spring ball, pre-camp, the stretch where the sport runs entirely on portal breadcrumbs and coaching staff Venmo activity.

That doesn't mean nothing is happening. It means everything happening is underground. Stewart Mandel noted in The Athletic earlier this month that the second wave of portal movement — the quiet, no-fanfare kind — consistently peaks around Memorial Day weekend, when kids finishing finals suddenly realize their depth chart situation isn't what spring practice made it feel like. Separately, Bud Elliott at 247Sports has been tracking how several blue-blood programs still carry obvious positional voids that haven't been addressed publicly.

This is the part of the offseason that separates the fans doing homework from the fans just waiting for fall. The boards are your friend right now. Watch portal activity at receiver and edge rusher across the SEC — that's where the next wave is breaking.

2Take #2 — Two Reads

The Offseason Stats Argument Nobody Is Actually Winning Yet

There's a split happening in CFB discourse right now that's worth naming: the analytics crowd and the die-hards are looking at the same spring rosters and arriving at completely different conclusions, and both sides are kind of right in the most annoying way possible. Stat-forward readers are leaning hard on returning production metrics and transfer portal grades — the kind of number-crunching that Bill Connelly has built a career around at ESPN, where his SP+ projections reward teams that quietly restock interior lines over teams that land the flashy skill-position transfer. Meanwhile, the message-board die-hards are doing what they do, which is trusting their eyes on spring game tape and deciding the vibes are real data. Stewart Mandel noted at The Athletic that spring evaluations remain "historically poor predictors" of September performance, which is correct and also completely irrelevant to anyone who has watched their team's new quarterback throw three touchdowns in a glorified scrimmage. Both readings are operating in good faith. Both will be hilariously wrong about at least one team. Watch depth chart releases and portal commitment trackers through the weekend — that's where the real signal hides.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

The Offseason Quiet Is Lying to You

We're in that weird mid-May dead zone where nothing seems to be happening, which is exactly when everything actually happens. No wire entries, no pulse spikes, no surprise receipts — just the calm before the roster chaos. This is the part of the calendar where programs are quietly finalizing their portal boards before the summer window cracks open, and the schools that do this work in silence are the ones who look brilliant in August.

Here's what the stillness is actually telling you: the gap between programs that treat roster management as a 365-day operation and those that scramble reactively is wider than it has ever been. Stewart Mandel has written repeatedly in The Athletic about how the elite staffs now run portal cycles the way NFL front offices run free agency — tiered boards, contingency lists, the whole infrastructure. Pete Thamel at ESPN flagged earlier this spring that several Power Four programs already have verbal commitments from June portal targets sitting in their back pocket right now.

Watch who makes a "surprise" move in the next seven days. It won't actually be a surprise.
