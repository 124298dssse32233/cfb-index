1Take #1 — Top Story

Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is the Recruiting Story of the Weekend, But Don't Sleep on What Notre Dame Just Did

Five-star WR Chris Henry Jr. committing to Ohio State was always the headline — the name alone carries weight, and Buckeye Nation treated it accordingly. On3's recruiting analysts called it "one of the most emotionally significant commits of the 2026 cycle," and that framing isn't wrong. But while Columbus celebrated, South Bend quietly landed five-star TE Ian Premer, and that one deserves more noise than it's getting.

Notre Dame's tight end pipeline under Marcus Freeman has become genuinely elite, and Premer fits the mold — a move-the-chains, red-zone weapon type that Marcus Freeman's staff has been hunting for two cycles. 247Sports recruiting insider Brian Dohn noted that Premer's commitment "stabilizes Notre Dame's offensive identity at a position that defines their offense."

Meanwhile, Oregon grabbed five-star IOL Immanuel Iheanacho, which is the kind of quiet, line-building commit that wins you Big Ten titles in November rather than Twitter arguments in May.

Watch the Ohio State–Notre Dame battle for remaining 2026 top-100 targets this week. The board is about to get competitive fast.

2Take #2 — Two Reads

The Recruiting Class Scoreboard Looks Different Depending on Whether You're Holding a Spreadsheet or a Fight Song

The stat crowd and the message-board lifers are genuinely watching this recruiting cycle through different lenses, and both camps have a point. On paper, Ohio State landing five-star WR Chris Henry Jr. is the kind of composite-score surge that makes 247 team rankings grids go haywire — Steve Wiltfong has been tracking that recruitment as a national bellwether for months. But the die-hards aren't talking about composite averages; they're talking about legacy and grief. Henry Jr. is the son of the late Chris Henry Sr., and that weight matters in ways no recruiting algorithm captures.

Meanwhile, Oregon pulling five-star IOL Immanuel Iheanacho is the move that has the advanced analytics crowd salivating. As The Athletic's Chris Vannini has noted repeatedly, interior line recruiting wins championships in the Big Ten before October even starts. Oregon's offensive infrastructure just got a serious foundation brick.

Notre Dame securing Ian Premer rounds out a three-commit day that genuinely reshapes three different conference narratives simultaneously — which is completely absurd and also completely normal now.

Watch all three programs' remaining board targets through the weekend. Momentum is contagious and rivals are scrambling.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

Ohio State Landing Chris Henry Jr. Is the Big Ten's Buried Story This Week

Oregon gets Immanuel Iheanacho, a five-star interior lineman who immediately shores up what was already a terrifying offensive front, and that's legitimately significant. But the commit that should be rattling rival staff rooms hardest? Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State.

Here's the part casual fans will scroll past: Henry isn't just a name. He's a legacy carry — son of the late Chris Henry — and that kind of recruitment carries emotional weight that changes locker room culture before a single snap. 247Sports' Brandon Huffman has tracked this recruitment as one of the most closely watched at the position nationally. Meanwhile, Pete Sampson at The Athletic noted this week that Notre Dame's board is "starting to read like a hostage list" as blue-chips keep finding other homes — and Ohio State just took another one off the table.

Oregon builds the trench. Ohio State builds the perimeter. Both moves in the same 24-hour window is genuinely chaotic Big Ten roster construction happening in real time.

Watch whether Georgia or Penn State responds with a portal move this week. Someone's getting squeezed.
