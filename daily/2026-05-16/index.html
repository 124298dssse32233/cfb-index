1Take #1 — Top Story

Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is the Recruiting Moment Everyone Saw Coming and Still Can't Believe

Five-star WR Chris Henry Jr. committed to Ohio State overnight, and Columbus is doing that thing where it collectively tries to act calm while absolutely losing its mind. This is the son of the late Chris Henry — NFL bloodline, elite talent, maximum storyline weight — and Ryan Day landed him. On247, Steve Wiltfong had flagged Ohio State's momentum building for weeks, and the commitment validated every whispered prediction on the boards. Meanwhile, Oregon didn't sleep: five-star IOL Immanuel Iheanacho committed to the Ducks, which, per The Athletic's Brunch Bucket crew, further cements Dan Lanning's ability to recruit the trenches at an elite level inside the Big Ten. Notre Dame also grabbed five-star TE Ian Premer, because apparently Marcus Freeman decided May was a good time to remind everyone the Irish can still cook. Alabama landed five-star S Jireh Edwards, keeping the DeBoer era from completely losing its stranglehold on the SEC secondary pipeline.

Watch the Ohio State pulse board this week — Arthur Smith's offense just got a whole lot more interesting to recruit around.

2Take #2 — Two Reads

Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is the Recruiting Story Stat Heads and Film Junkies Are Reading Completely Differently

The analytics crowd is stacking this one on top of the Buckeyes' current 247Sports composite ranking and declaring Columbus back in the national title conversation before a single snap gets played. The film junkies, meanwhile, are watching Henry's route tree on loop and seeing something rarer: a kid who plays bigger than any number you can attach to him. Both camps are right, which is what makes this commitment genuinely weird to process.

What's actually getting slept on is the NFL bloodline angle. As Dan Wetzel noted on Yahoo Sports, generational pedigree in recruiting cuts both ways — expectation becomes a weather system that follows a prospect everywhere. Ohio State's fan sentiment pulse is already oscillating between champagne and anxiety, which, per the Solid Verbal guys, is basically Columbus's resting state.

Pair this with Oregon landing five-star IOL Immanuel Iheanacho, and the Big Ten's 2026 recruiting arms race just escalated faster than anyone projected in January.

Watch whether Alabama's Jireh Edwards commitment triggers a secondary wave of safety offers across the SEC this week. That board is about to get loud.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

Ohio State Lands Chris Henry Jr., But Oregon's Iheanacho Is the Commit That Actually Reshapes the Big Ten

Everyone's hitting refresh on the Chris Henry Jr. news — and fair, an NFL bloodline five-star at receiver is legitimately must-watch stuff in Columbus. But the quieter earthquake yesterday was Immanuel Iheanacho choosing Oregon, and the recruiting boards haven't fully caught up to what that means yet.

Interior offensive line wins championships in the Big Ten. Full stop. Dan Lanning just landed a five-star IOL who shores up the one area where the Ducks got exposed when games got physical late in the season. Brandon Huffman at 247Sports has tracked Iheanacho as one of the ten most physically projectable linemen in this class. Meanwhile, Pete Nakos at On3 noted Oregon's offensive line recruiting has quietly become elite-tier, not just competitive.

Ohio State fans celebrating Henry Jr. — rightfully — should also be clocking that Oregon is building the machinery to make their own lives miserable for the next three seasons.

Watch whether any of Ohio State's offensive line commitments waver this week. That's the actual tell.
