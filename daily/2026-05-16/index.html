1Take #1 — Top Story

Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is the Recruiting Story You Actually Care About This Morning

The Buckeyes landed five-star WR Chris Henry Jr. overnight, and combined with the two top-12 DB transfers already banked, Ohio State's 2026 class is starting to look less like recruiting and more like a hostile takeover. This is the NFL legacy kid — son of the late Chris Henry — and he was trending scarlet over Athens and Austin, which makes this a genuine statement win for Ryan Day. On247, Steve Wiltfong had been tracking Ohio State as the team to beat here for weeks, and that read paid out.

Meanwhile Oregon scooped five-star IOL Immanuel Iheanacho, which is a quietly enormous get for a line that needs to reload after sending bodies to the NFL. On3's Chad Simmons called Iheanacho "a foundational piece type," and that tracks — you don't build a Big Ten offensive identity without winning the trenches in recruiting first.

Notre Dame adding five-star TE Ian Premer is the third headliner from one overnight cycle. Three five-stars, one night. CFB is genuinely unhinged.

Watch whether Georgia or Texas makes a counter-move on Henry Jr.'s crystal ball in the next 48 hours — flips happen fast in May.

2Take #2 — Two Reads

Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is the Recruiting Story Stat Heads and Die-Hards Are Reading Completely Differently

The five-star WR commitment lands and immediately splits the room. Stat fans are pulling up historical comp models, noting that wide receiver recruiting rankings correlate with offensive production at a rate that makes this addition genuinely program-altering. Die-hards, meanwhile, are just thinking about Chris Henry Sr. — the legacy angle hits different when you grew up watching that number in Cincinnati.

Both reads are valid, and that tension is half the fun of a cycle this absurd. On3's recruiting analysts flagged Henry Jr. as a top-five player nationally, while The Athletic's Ari Wasserman has consistently tracked Ohio State's aggressive pursuit of skill-position talent as a direct response to last January's playoff stumbles. These aren't separate stories — they're the same bet on Ryan Day finally surrounding his quarterback with an embarrassment of weapons.

Ohio State's pulse is unambiguously up. The Buckeyes now have an NFL legacy kid, two elite DB transfers, and a stadium renovation incoming.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is the Headline. The Oregon Offensive Line Move Is the Story.

Everyone's going to talk about Chris Henry Jr. committing to Ohio State — and they should, the legacy element alone is worth a column — but the commit that actually reshapes the Big Ten landscape this week is five-star IOL Immanuel Iheanacho landing in Eugene. The Buckeyes adding Henry gives Will Howard's eventual successor a weapon. Oregon adding a five-star interior lineman gives Dan Lanning the infrastructure to run his program through the trenches for the next four years. Those are different categories of important.

The Athletic's recruiting desk has tracked Oregon's offensive line room as a quiet priority all cycle, and Iheanacho's commitment validates that the Ducks are building — not reloading. Meanwhile, on3's Chad Simmons noted Ohio State's class momentum as "the best confluence of portal and high school talent in the country right now," which feels right when you stack Henry against the two DB transfers already in Columbus.

Alabama pulling Jireh Edwards keeps DeBoer's class respectable, but watch whether Oregon's line depth becomes the sleeper class story of the summer.
