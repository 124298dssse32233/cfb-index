1Take #1 — Top Story

Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State Is the Recruiting Story of the Week, But Oregon Just Made the Big Ten Race Messier

Five-star WR Chris Henry Jr. committing to Ohio State is the headline that'll dominate your timeline today, and it deserves to. The Buckeyes landing the son of the late Chris Henry is a story with genuine weight — On3's Chad Simmons called it "one of the most emotionally significant recruitments in recent memory," and that's not hyperbole for once. Ryan Day keeps stockpiling skill-position talent like the portal doesn't exist.

But don't sleep on Immanuel Iheanacho flipping to Oregon. A five-star interior offensive lineman choosing the Ducks signals that Dan Lanning isn't just recruiting to compete — he's recruiting to dismantle. 247Sports' Brandon Huffman noted Iheanacho "could anchor an offensive line for four years," which in a conference where Iowa just grabbed a QB and Penn State's momentum is real, matters enormously.

Notre Dame's Ian Premer landing at tight end adds another layer to an already absurdly talented '26 cycle.

Watch the composite rankings shuffle by Sunday. The Big Ten's recruiting ceiling just got a lot more crowded.

Ohio State Lands Chris Henry Jr. — But the Stat Guys and the Die-Hards Are Watching Completely Different Numbers

The recruiting composite crowd is loading up the team rankings pages right now, celebrating Ohio State absorbing five-star WR Chris Henry Jr. into what is quietly becoming a terrifying 2026 class. Makes sense. The algorithmic view says this is a straightforward elite-program flex.

Die-hards are reading it differently. Chris Henry Jr. carries weight that numbers can't fully hold — son of the late Chris Henry Sr., a story 247Sports has tracked carefully through every visit and decision. For that contingent, this commit lands harder than any composite bump. It's not a slot on a depth chart yet, it's a legacy playing out in real time, and that's a different kind of investment.

Meanwhile, Steve Wiltfong at 247Sports noted the recruitment came down to a genuinely tight battle, and The Athletic's Ari Wasserman has flagged Ohio State's receiver room as the unit most likely to reshape the Big Ten's offensive identity in 2027 and beyond.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

Ohio State Landing Chris Henry Jr. Is the Headliner — But Oregon's Trenches Move Is the One That Actually Changes the Big Ten

Five-star receiver Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State is going to eat every headline today, and fairly so — Ryan Day adding a name that carries that kind of weight and backstory is legitimately significant. But scroll past it for a second, because Oregon quietly did something nastier: five-star IOL Immanuel Iheanacho committed to the Ducks, and interior offensive line in the Big Ten is not a glamour recruitment, it's a war recruitment.

As Pete Nakos at On3 has tracked all cycle, Oregon's roster-building logic under Dan Lanning prioritizes the point of attack first, skill positions second. Iheanacho fits that blueprint perfectly. Meanwhile, Steve Wiltfong at 247Sports noted earlier this week that the Big Ten's physical identity increasingly runs through whoever can dominate the interior — and Oregon just answered that question loudly.

Notre Dame's Ian Premer at tight end is also no joke. Marcus Freeman is stacking this class with methodical patience.

Watch this week: How Ohio State's receiver room reacts internally to Henry Jr.'s commitment — portal activity in the next 72 hours will tell you everything about the locker room temperature in Columbus.
