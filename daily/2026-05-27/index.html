1Take #1 — Top Story

Vanderbilt Just Landed a Five-Star QB and the SEC Should Be Mildly Terrified

Jared Curtis committing to Vanderbilt isn't a feel-good story — it's a program legitimately threatening to become annoying. Clark Lea has turned Nashville into a place where recruits actually consider visiting, and now he's got a five-star signal-caller to build around. Nobody's laughing at the Commodores anymore, and Curtis is the clearest evidence yet that Diego Pavia's 2024 run wasn't a fluke — it was a recruiting pitch.

Meanwhile, the rest of the SEC wasn't sitting still. Alabama landed five-stars on both sides of the secondary — CB Jorden Edmonds and S Jireh Edwards — in what Steve Wiltfong at 247Sports called movement that "solidifies the Tide's 2026 class as a top-three unit nationally." This is college football being its most absurd self: Vanderbilt is now a threat, Alabama is still Alabama, and the recruiting cycle never sleeps.

Vanderbilt Just Landed a Five-Star QB and the SEC Still Isn't Taking This Seriously Enough

The buried lede from yesterday's recruiting cycle isn't Chris Henry Jr. to Ohio State (spectacular, yes) or Alabama stacking the secondary with Jorden Edmonds and Jireh Edwards (expected, almost boring). It's Jared Curtis — five-star quarterback — choosing Vanderbilt. Read that sentence again slowly.

This isn't a fluke or a charity commitment. This is the direct downstream consequence of Diego Pavia making Nashville a legitimate football address. As 247Sports tracked Curtis's recruitment, Vanderbilt was never supposed to be in this conversation. They were there anyway, and they won it.

The fan pulse has been screaming this for weeks — nobody's laughing at Clark Lea's program anymore.

The Athletic's national recruiting desk hasn't fully connected these dots yet, but they will.
