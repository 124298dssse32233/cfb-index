1Take #1 — Top Story

Oregon Is Quietly Building Something Terrifying in the Big Ten

Dan Lanning didn't just have a good recruiting weekend — he had the kind of weekend that makes rival fan bases refresh 247Sports at midnight for no good reason. Five-star EDGE Anthony Jones commits to Oregon, and suddenly the Ducks' 2026 class has a centerpiece that makes every Big Ten defensive coordinator nervous. Throw in four-star RB Tradarian Ball, IOL Tommy Tofi, and S Devin Jackson, and Oregon dropped four commits in what felt like a single news cycle. That's not recruiting — that's a statement.

Brandon Huffman at 247Sports noted Jones as one of the premier pass-rush prospects in the entire 2026 cycle, and On3's Chad Simmons flagged Oregon's recent momentum as a legitimate threat to Ohio State's stranglehold on Big Ten recruiting geography. Both of them are right.

Notre Dame landing five-star TE Ian Premer the same weekend is the universe's way of reminding everyone that two programs can go crazy simultaneously. Absurd weekend, honestly.

Watch whether Jones's commitment triggers any decommitments elsewhere — the ripple effect on the EDGE market this week will tell you everything about where the class ranks are heading.

2Take #2 — Two Reads

Oregon's Haul Is a Class, Not Just a Commit — But the Stat Crowd and the Die-Hards Are Arguing Past Each Other

Five-star EDGE Anthony Jones is the headline, but zoom out and Dan Lanning just stacked four commits in what feels like a 48-hour window — Jones, RB Tradarian Ball, IOL Tommy Tofi, S Devin Jackson. The composite-score crowd on 247 is doing the math and watching Oregon climb toward a top-three national class in real time. That's the stat-fan read: aggregate rankings, position-group balance, Big Ten championship roster construction.

The die-hards aren't wrong to pump the brakes, though. As Pete Nakos noted at On3, depth-chart fit and scheme alignment matter as much as the star next to the name — and Oregon's portal dependency the last two cycles complicates how you weight any early signing period haul. Meanwhile, Brandon Marcello flagged at 247Sports that the Ducks' attrition rate from high-profile classes hasn't always matched the hype.

Both reads can be true simultaneously. CFB recruiting is chaos dressed in a polo shirt.

Watch whether Notre Dame's Ian Premer commit reshuffles Big Ten TE board targets this week — dominoes are already wobbling.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

Oregon Is Quietly Building the Most Dangerous Big Ten Roster Nobody Is Panicking About Yet

The Anthony Jones commitment gets the headlines — five-star EDGE, program-defining type of talent — but zoom out and Oregon just did something quietly terrifying over the last 24 hours. Jones plus RB Tradarian Ball, IOL Tommy Tofi, and safety Devin Jackson in the same news cycle isn't a recruiting day, it's a statement. Dan Lanning is constructing a roster that addresses every structural vulnerability from last season simultaneously, and most people are still talking about Notre Dame's Ian Premer commit like that's the only big swing of the week.

The buried lede: Oregon is the only program in the Big Ten right now assembling elite depth on both lines and the back end in the same class window.247Sports' composite rankings have tracked this surge, and Pete Nakos at On3 noted earlier this cycle that Lanning's staff has "flipped the recruiting geography" of the Pacific Northwest entirely in two years. That's not a fluke — that's infrastructure.

Watch whether any Big Ten programs make counter-moves on the offensive line portal this week. Oregon just raised the floor.
