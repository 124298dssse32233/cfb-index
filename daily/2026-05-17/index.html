1Take #1 — Top Story

Oregon Is Quietly Building a Monster, and the Big Ten Should Be Nervous

Dan Lanning didn't just land a commit overnight — he landed a class statement. Five-star EDGE Anthony Jones choosing Oregon is the kind of move that reshapes a recruiting board at the conference level. On3's Charles Power noted Jones was "one of the most disruptive pass-rushers in the 2026 cycle," and when a prospect like that picks the Ducks over programs with deeper legacy, it means something real. Pair Jones with four-star RB Tradarian Ball, four-star IOL Tommy Tofi, and four-star S Devin Jackson — all committing in the same 24-hour window — and you're not reading a recruiting class, you're reading a manifesto. 247Sports' Brandon Ciresi flagged Oregon's board as "one of the most efficiently constructed in the portal-and-recruiting era," and honestly, that framing holds up. Lanning is doing this with a methodical consistency that Ohio State and Michigan probably aren't losing sleep over yet, but probably should be.

Meanwhile, Notre Dame landing five-star TE Ian Premer keeps the Irish right in the national conversation.

Watch whether Jones's commitment triggers a domino on any unsigned five-star EDGE targets still floating. The next 72 hours on the offer tracker matter.

2Take #2 — Two Reads

Oregon's Recruiting Haul This Week Isn't a Class — It's a Statement, and the Stat Crowd Sees It Differently Than the Die-Hards

The die-hards look at Oregon's last 24 hours — five-star EDGE Anthony Jones, four-star RB Tradarian Ball, IOL Tommy Tofi, safety Devin Jackson — and see a program announcing itself as the Big Ten's next monster. The stat crowd looks at the same board and immediately starts querying composite rankings, positional scarcity, and whether Jones profiles as a Day 1 pass rusher or a project. Both reads are correct and that's what makes this absurd and fun simultaneously.

247Sports national recruiting editor Brandon Huffman has tracked Jones as one of the premier EDGE prospects in the 2026 cycle. Meanwhile, The Athletic's Bruce Feldman has consistently framed Oregon's Big Ten transition as contingent on whether Dan Lanning can stockpile defensive line talent — which is exactly what this week addresses.

The disagreement is real: die-hards want the vibes, stat fans want the three-year production curve. Neither camp is wrong, they're just watching different movies.

Watch whether Anthony Jones takes an official visit anywhere else before the calendar flips to June. That visit window will tell you everything about how firm this commitment actually is.

3Take #3 — Buried Lede

Oregon Is Quietly Building a Monster, and the Big Ten Should Be Uncomfortable

The headline was Anthony Jones — five-star EDGE, instant-impact pass rusher, the kind of commit that moves a class from good to genuinely scary. Fine. Log it. But zoom out and the buried lede is that Dan Lanning added Jones on top of four-star RB Tradarian Ball, IOL Tommy Tofi, and safety Devin Jackson in what looks like a coordinated, single-cycle roster construction project, not a recruiting class. That's infrastructure. That's not vibes, that's architecture.

247Sports' Brandon Huffman has tracked Lanning's Pacific Rim pipeline for two cycles now, and the IOL depth quietly being assembled is the part that doesn't get clicks but absolutely gets wins in January. Meanwhile, On3's Chad Simmons noted Jones' recruitment came down to Oregon over Georgia — which means Lanning beat Kirby Smart on the edge rusher's home turf. That's a sentence worth reading twice.

USC adding Jaimeon Winfield the same week feels like a footnote by comparison, honestly.

Watch whether Notre Dame's Ian Premer commitment reshapes Oregon's TE board — Lanning still needs a receiving weapon in the seams, and that position now has a big name off the market.
