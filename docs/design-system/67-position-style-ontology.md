The play-style blurb keeps a constant **shape** (signature → grounded trait → texture/what-to-watch → honest growth edge → forward stake) and a tier-calibrated **register** (cinematic / descriptive / plain). What changes per position is the **vocabulary**, the **eval dimensions**, the **grounding source** (box-rich vs box-thin), and the **sub-archetype**. This doc supplies those.

## Cross-cutting CFB-2026 lenses (available as beats on ANY position when live)
- **Revenue-share / NIL / portal era** *(verified 2026-06-13).* Rosters are salary-managed assets; every player carries a *homegrown-development vs portal-acquisition vs draft-clock-rental* frame. The portal poaches QB / OT / EDGE hardest. (House settlement: a **$20.5M direct-pay cap per school**, live since the 2025-26 cycle, rising ~4%/yr; the cap is direct-pay ONLY — top rosters reach **$40–50M** counting collective/booster NIL on top; the cap is being challenged in court.) Live eval question: *did the system make him, or does his game travel?*
- **12-team Playoff** *(verified).* Locked at 12 through 2026-27 (SEC-wanted-16 vs Big-Ten-wanted-24 stalemate). **2026 moves to STRAIGHT seeding** by ranking — no more auto-top-4 seeds for conference champs; the Group-of-Six auto-bid goes to the highest-ranked non-Power-Four team (need not be a champ); Notre Dame is in if it finishes top-12. Depth + "Playoff-tested" reps carry weight; rotation players have real arcs.
- **Two-high shells (quarters / Tampa-2) as the base** *(verified).* The answer to explosive passing — squeezes deep shots, opens the middle/underneath. Raises the value of QB patience/accuracy, the slot, the move-TE, and the do-it-all safety. **And, crucially, two-high leaves LIGHT BOXES — which has sparked a run-game renaissance** (see RB).
- **Tempo / spread / RPO.** Conditioning + processing-at-speed + run-pass conflict; the dual-threat QB is the default; committee backfields; motion/bunch WR usage.
- **Sim-pressures / creepers.** Defenses rush four (drop a lineman, blitz a DB) — so DL must win 1-on-1 and the front-7 must be positionless.
- **Scheme-fit-after-transfer.** A portal player's style must re-fit a new system — a live forward question, especially QB/OL.

Box-thin positions (OT, IOL, in-line TE blocking, run-defense) lean on PFF grade + frame + attributed scouting. Box-rich positions (QB, RB, WR, DL disruption, DB ball-production) lean on the proprietary PBP percentiles.

## OFFENSE

### Quarterback
- **Sub-archetypes:** pocket general · gunslinger · dual-threat creator · efficient distributor/game-manager · improviser (off-script).
- **Dimensions:** arm talent (velocity, layering, deep touch) · processing (pre/post-snap reads, anticipation) · pocket (movement, poise, sack-avoidance) · accuracy by level (short/intermediate/deep, on- vs off-platform) · mobility (designed runs, scramble, escapability) · decision/turnover-worthiness.
- **Grounding:** aDOT, completion %, EPA/dropback, success rate, CPOE, explosive-pass rate, sack rate, INT/TD rate; rushing EPA/explosive for dual-threats.
- **2026 lens:** must beat two-high (patience, take the underneath/check-down); the QB run is baseline, not a bonus; portal QB1s are often one-year rentals → *masters a new system fast* is a real 2026 trait.
- **Common edge:** off-platform creation · deep-ball touch · processing disguised coverage · turnover discipline.

### Running back
- **Sub-archetypes:** bell-cow (rare now) · between-the-tackles grinder · one-cut zone back · scat/satellite (receiving) · committee thunder/lightning.
- **Dimensions:** vision/patience · burst/long speed · contact balance/broken tackles · receiving (routes, hands) · pass-protection (the snap that keeps you on the field) · short-yardage/red-zone finish.
- **Grounding:** ypc, explosive-run rate, success rate, EPA/carry, yards-after-contact / forced-missed-tackles, receiving usage, TD rate (finish).
- **2026 lens** *(CORRECTED — verified):* two-high umbrella coverage leaves LIGHT BOXES, so the run game has **re-emerged as pivotal** — the RB's value is back UP, not down (my earlier "workhorse extinct" was wrong). Workloads are often split, but receiving + pass-pro still keep you on the field in spread, and the do-it-all back is a genuine weapon; NIL keeps elite backs in school longer.
- **Common edge:** the "complete back" jump (receiving + pass-pro + finish) · TD efficiency · durability for volume.

### Wide receiver
- **Sub-archetypes:** X / boundary (contested, beats press) · Z / flanker (motion, off the line) · slot (option routes, nickel-beater, YAC) · gadget/jet.
- **Dimensions:** release (vs press/off) · separation (route nuance, breaks) · hands/contested-catch · YAC · route-tree breadth · ball-tracking/deep.
- **Grounding:** catch rate, YPR, explosive-target rate, EPA/target, TD rate, aDOT (deep vs possession); separation/contested via charting/PFF.
- **2026 lens:** two-high tightens deep windows → slot, YAC, and contested-catch rise; motion/bunch usage; the alpha X who wins single coverage vs press is gold against quarters; portal-churned WR rooms.
- **Common edge:** route-tree expansion · separation vs man · contested-catch consistency · the deep connection.

### Tight end
- **Sub-archetypes:** in-line Y (blocker-first) · move / "joker" (mismatch receiver, flex/slot) · H-back/fullback hybrid.
- **Dimensions:** in-line/down blocking · seam-stretch/receiving · YAC for size · alignment versatility (in-line/flex/backfield) · blocking in space.
- **Grounding:** receiving (catch/YPR/TD) is box-measured; blocking is box-thin → PFF grade + scouting; alignment/usage data.
- **2026 lens** *(verified — the "TE revolution"):* TE usage is RISING in both roles — 11-personnel (one TE) up to ~23% of snaps and 12/13 heavy-TE personnel growing too; the move-TE beats two-high up the seam and is a scheme cheat code. The position is *expanding*, not the in-line blocker fading.
- **Common edge:** the dual-threat jump (block AND stretch the seam) · separation as a flex receiver.

### Offensive tackle (LT/RT)
- **Sub-archetypes:** pass-set protector (LT prototype) · road-grader (run-first RT) · athletic zone-blocker.
- **Dimensions:** pass-set/kick-slide · anchor vs power · hand placement/reset · length · lateral agility/mirror · run-blocking (drive/reach/climb) · finish.
- **Grounding:** BOX-THIN → PFF grade, sacks/pressures allowed, snap count, frame/length; scouting consensus + draft stock.
- **2026 lens:** the portal made OL continuity a luxury — the tackle who *stays* is rare and valuable; pass-pro vs tempo conditioning; protecting a high-NIL QB is a roster priority.
- **Common edge:** run-game consistency to match the pass-pro · anchor vs elite power · the jump to "first off the board."

### Interior OL (Guard / Center)
- **Sub-archetypes:** power/mauler guard · athletic pulling guard (zone/screen) · center as the line's QB.
- **Dimensions:** power/leverage/pad level · lateral quickness (pull/reach/climb to the 2nd level) · anchor vs interior power · hand use · (C) snap + protection calls/IQ · finish/nastiness.
- **Grounding:** PFF grade, pressures/sacks allowed, scouting; the center's calls/IQ are cerebral and box-invisible.
- **2026 lens:** interior protection vs sim-pressures/creepers — the slot-DB blitz tests the center's IDs; tempo conditioning; the center who diagnoses pressure is a 2026 premium.
- **Common edge:** anchor vs bull-rush · reaching the 2nd level in space · (C) pre-snap pressure IDs.

## DEFENSE

### EDGE
- **Sub-archetypes:** speed-rush/bend · power/bull-rush · hand-technician/counter-rusher · strong-side setter (run-first).
- **Dimensions:** get-off/first-step · bend/ankle flex · power conversion (speed-to-power) · hand usage/counters/move-stringing · motor/finish · run-defense (set the edge, spill/contain).
- **Grounding:** sacks, TFL, pressures (charting/PFF), QB hits, run-stop %; the pressure>sack distinction matters.
- **2026 lens:** sim-pressures mean the edge often rushes four and must WIN 1-on-1; the hybrid stand-up edge/LB who can drop is prized; the portal poaches edges hard.
- **Common edge:** a pass-rush PLAN (counters, not one move) · run-game on early downs · drop/coverage for the hybrid.

### Interior DL (Nose / 3-tech)
- **Sub-archetypes:** penetrating 3-tech · two-gap nose/anchor · interior pass-rush specialist.
- **Dimensions:** get-off/first-step · gap penetration vs two-gap anchor · hand use/leverage · pocket-push/interior rush · motor · double-team-eating.
- **Grounding:** TFL, sacks (modest inside), pressures/pocket-collapse, run-stuff %, snaps; value is disruption that hides from the box score.
- **2026 lens:** creepers/sim-pressures need an interior who collapses the pocket so the blitzing DB gets home; the nose who eats doubles frees the LBs; 4-man-rush wins are gold.
- **Common edge:** penetration → finishes (pressures into sacks) · anchor vs double-teams · conditioning for snap volume.

### Off-ball LB (Mike / Will / hybrid)
- **Sub-archetypes:** thumper run-stopper · sideline-to-sideline WILL · coverage/hybrid LB · blitzing green-dot.
- **Dimensions:** run-fit/diagnosis (read-and-fill) · range/closing speed · coverage (hook/curl, man on backs/TEs) · blitz/pressure · tackling in space · communication (green-dot).
- **Grounding:** tackles, TFL, run-stop, coverage grade (PFF), blitz pressures, missed-tackle rate.
- **2026 lens:** spread/RPO demands LBs who cover — the box-only thumper is exposed; the hybrid LB/safety is the modern prototype; sim-pressures use LBs as creepers.
- **Common edge:** coverage range (the spread-era requirement) · tackling in space · processing RPO conflict.

### Cornerback
- **Sub-archetypes:** boundary press-man (island corner) · field/zone-match · ball-hawk · physical run-support corner.
- **Dimensions:** press technique/jam · hips/transition · recovery speed · ball skills (PBU/INT, ball-tracking) · zone-match IQ · tackling/run-support.
- **Grounding:** PBU, INT, completion %/passer-rating-against (charting/PFF), targets, missed-tackle rate.
- **2026 lens:** two-high / man-match needs corners who play zone-match AND press; a boundary lockdown corner lets a defense roll coverage to the slot; portal-heavy secondaries.
- **Common edge:** ball production (PBUs → INTs) · tackling · man-coverage vs elite separators.

### Safety (Free / Strong-box)
- **Sub-archetypes:** single-high rangy free (deep eraser) · box/strong (run support) · do-it-all positionless.
- **Dimensions:** range/deep coverage · ball skills · run support/box trigger · tackling in space · communication (defense's QB) · versatility (deep/box/slot).
- **Grounding:** tackles (box = high), PBU/INT, TFL, missed-tackle rate, alignment snaps (deep vs box vs slot).
- **2026 lens:** the two-high era made SAFETY the most important growth position — defenses live in two-high, so range + tackling-in-space + slot-coverage versatility is the prototype; the 3-safety/big-nickel look.
- **Common edge:** ball production catching up to range · slot-coverage reps · the read-and-trigger.

### Nickel / STAR (slot defender)
- **Sub-archetypes:** cover-slot (matchup eraser) · blitzing STAR (run-fit + pressure) · big-nickel (third safety).
- **Dimensions:** slot coverage (option routes, quickness) · run-fit/edge-setting from the slot · blitz/pressure timing · tackling in space · versatility (CB/S/LB tweener).
- **Grounding:** coverage grade, slot-blitz pressures, tackles/TFL, missed-tackle rate, alignment versatility.
- **2026 lens:** the STAR/nickel is now a near-permanent starter (defenses are in nickel/dime vs spread) — arguably the position the two-high/spread era *created*; the positionless DB is the most valuable developmental win.
- **Common edge:** covering elite slots · run-fit physicality · blitz timing.

## SPECIAL TEAMS
- **Kicker:** leg/range (50+), accuracy by distance, clutch (game-winners), kickoff touchback rate. Ground in FG %, long, pressure kicks.
- **Punter:** hang time, directional/pooch, net average, inside-20 pin rate — the flip-field weapon (the portal/NIL reaches specialists too).
- **Returner:** explosiveness, vision/decision, ball security — a field-position weapon.
