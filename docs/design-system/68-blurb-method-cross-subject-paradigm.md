# 68 — Blurb Engine: Methods & Cross-Subject Paradigm

_Status: METHODS SPEC, 2026-06-13. The durable, subject-agnostic methodology behind the CFB Index blurbs — documented in full and abstracted so the same paradigm applies to **players** (built/live: 66, 67, 41, 42, 60), **teams** (narrative crown spec'd on this paradigm: 50, 51, 62), and future subjects (**coaches, games/matchups, rivalries**). Read this for the *method*; read the per-subject docs for the *instantiation*. Memory: `prospective-player-blurb-engine-2026-06-13`._

> One line: every CFB Index "lead" — player, team, coach, game — is a **matched pair** (a *why-it-matters* narrative + a *how-it-plays* identity blurb), written to a **fixed function with free form**, **sized to the story** (not the data), **grounded in sourced evidence**, **calibrated by register**, and acting as **the page's spine, not another module**. The subject changes; the method does not.

---

## PART A — The universal paradigm (holds for every subject)

These principles are already shared, explicitly, across the player docs (41/60/66/67) and the team docs (50/62). Stated here as the binding cross-subject contract.

1. **The matched pair.** Each subject gets two complementary leads in different registers:
   - a **narrative blurb** — *why it matters* (the conversation, the stakes, forward-looking);
   - a **"how-it-plays" / character blurb** — *what it IS* (the on-field/operational identity).
   Player = story + play-style. Team = program-state + how-the-team-plays. Coach = career/fit + coaching-philosophy. *(The "how-it-plays" half is the newer method, §B.6.)*
2. **Spine, not sibling.** The blurb is the page's **lead** — it asserts a thesis and points down at the modules as the **evidence locker**. It is the only element allowed to take a position. It never summarizes the modules.
3. **Fix function, free form** (the anti-formula law). A **contract floor** is invariant; the lead, order, and emphasis are chosen per subject from a **combinatorial grammar**, never a fixed template. *Contract floor:* orient a stranger · plant one forward stake · deliver one non-obvious payoff · stay honest · calibrate drama to real (durable) stakes.
4. **Best product, not moat.** Source-agnostic. Proprietary data competes on merit (it earns the lead via a *surprise bonus* when genuinely non-obvious, and loses to a better outside fact otherwise).
5. **Length + register follow STORY SIZE, not data density or raw attention** (§B.2). The **register ladder** — cinematic / descriptive / plain / minimal — scales prose ambition to stature. **Restraint is a feature:** no story → short and honest; *never manufacture a saga* ("the Quiet State" is still a confident card). Over-waxing a minor subject is inflation.
6. **The grounding law.** Every claim is sourced — a **metric**, a **verified fact**, or an **attributed voice**. The house **compiles, never adjudicates**: takes are attributed ("fans say…", "the market prices…", "scouts call it…"), and the only thing asserted as fact is "this is what's being said / what the numbers show." Receipt density ≥1 marker / 200 words ([[32-receipt-pattern]]).
7. **Ethics gate on evidentiary status, not topic.** Verified/official consequences (suspension, charge of record, portal entry, injury, firing) are reportable and often *required* for accuracy; rumors/allegations are suppressed regardless of volume. Gate via source-trust tiers.
8. **Temporal integrity.** Present-day accurate; the forward frame is **calendar-phase-aware** (offseason projects, in-season reports); carries an **as-of stamp**; **regenerates on trigger events**; a forward claim has a **half-life**.
9. **The BAN / one honest number.** A single welded *stat object* (record, percentile, count, margin), gated and never bare — the honesty anchor.
10. **No hero NIL number** ([[nil-valuation-not-a-hero-number]]). NIL-as-story is allowed (from discourse); NIL valuation is never a hero/BAN claim.

## PART B — The world-class method in full (the machinery)

The subject-agnostic pipeline: **assemble evidence → normalize → detect → plan → write → gate → cache → regenerate.**

### B.1 The matched pair, defined
- **Narrative** mines *the conversation* (attention, discourse, stakes, the calendar) → *why it matters now, forward.*
- **How-it-plays** mines *the tape/operation* (metrics + scheme + identity descriptors) → *what it is, how it works.* §B.6.
They share evidence (scheme/coach context, the BAN) but never duplicate; narrative sets up the page, how-it-plays sets up the analytics modules.

### B.2 The sizing dial — divergence-and-durability story score
`story_score` = **production · attention · their MISMATCH** (over/under-rated divergence), **× durability** (sustained vs spike), **amplified by situation/stakes** (Playoff math, role, schedule), **framed by calendar phase**, **capped by the ethics gate**. It sets the **rung → {length band, register, investigative depth (web on/off)}**. Length bands are CEILINGS, not targets (the padding gate enforces). Effort scales with attention — a high-interest subject with thin output means *search harder*, not ship short.

### B.3 The live-signal detector (turns the grammar into "what's live NOW")
- Per candidate angle, a **liveness vector**: `{present, live, forward, strength, durability, confidence}`. The killer field is `live` (active vs settled/dormant).
- **Normalize everything** — cohort percentile (multi-altitude: local + national, the gap is a story), delta-vs-own-baseline (trend beats level), de-seasonalized; **thin cohort → hierarchical backoff + shrinkage + lower confidence** (never break — confidence == the restraint dial).
- **Calendar-phase gating** turns whole element families on/off by date and sets register + forward horizon.
- **Relational/collision detectors** (subject × opponent × history) catch the best hooks (e.g., the rematch).
- The detector **is** the freshness engine — a flipped detector is a regeneration trigger.

### B.4 The planner (angle-selection — where anti-formula lives)
Consumes the ranked live cluster + evidence → emits a structured **PLAN** (spine · supporting blend · the payoff · forward stake · register · horizon · length band · drop-list · evidence bindings). The writer writes TO the plan; gates verify prose AGAINST it. Scoring = `reader_value = (live × forward × strength × durability × confidence) + surprise_bonus − staleness_penalty` — **source is not a term.** Anti-formula mechanisms: selection-over-template · merit-blind-to-source · blend-and-name (coin new shapes) · coherence-check (one story, not two stapled) · lead-diversity backstop (penalize structural-opener repetition) · restraint as an explicit output.

### B.5 The writer (to the plan)
Free within the spec; CFB-native voice + the §A rules. **Web/outside facts are vetted in evidence-assembly, not wielded live by the writer** (so freshness is enforceable). **Length is a ceiling + a stop instruction, never a word target.** Register/horizon tokens condition tense and scope. Writer quality matters most for the cinematic register → use the best model there.

### B.6 The "how-it-plays" method — 9 world-class pillars
1. **Subject-native ontology.** Lead in the subject's own vocabulary. For players this is the **position ontology** ([[67-position-style-ontology]]); for teams it is a *team-identity/scheme* ontology; for coaches a *coaching-philosophy* ontology (both to be built, §C).
2. **Signature-first, specificity over completeness** — center the one distinctive thing; three vivid true things, not ten generic.
3. **Seamless quant↔qual braid** — the metric becomes prose (invisible scaffolding), never a stat line.
4. **Three-lens synthesis (numbers / analysts / the crowd), tension-aware** — disagreement across lenses *is* a story (the style aura-gap). E.g., "the film crowd frets about his arm; the production says it never mattered."
5. **The sourced "tell"** — one small, cinematic, *sourced* recognizable detail.
6. **Honest edge in proportion** — the limitation framed as part of the identity, one, grounded/attributed.
7. **Scheme / system-fit** — how the identity fits or fights the coach's system + the role; a scheme change is a forward variable. *(This is exactly where forthcoming team/coach profiles plug in — §C.)*
8. **Anti-cliché gate with teeth** — a scout-speak/ buzzword banlist; every trait anchored-or-cut; a cliché-density hard fail.
9. **Cinematic, sensory prose** at the top register; **clean descriptive** at the feature register; **plain** below.
   **The qualitative-descriptor LAW** (anti-hallucination): a descriptor is allowed ONLY if (a) metric-computed, (b) a physical/verified fact, or (c) attributed to a real cited source — the house never free-asserts character. **Grounding ladder:** metric → attributed-analytical (analyst/beat) → attributed-crowd (fan discourse) → drop. **Descriptor types:** identity/perception (the *home-vs-rival split* — a signature delight), behavioral tendency (metric-proxy-first), physical/frame (measurables).

### B.7 The gate stack
- **HARD** (correctness/safety — pass or reject+retry): attribution · ethics (evidentiary) · grounding/factscore · freshness/staleness · **eligibility/active-status** (no forward blurb for a departed subject).
- **SOFT** (quality — degrade or *re-tier*): padding · contract-floor completeness · plan-conformance · lead-diversity.
- Order **cheap/deterministic before expensive/semantic**; resolve HARD-vs-SOFT conflicts by **re-tiering** (smaller rung), never arm-wrestling; bounded retries → **Last-Known-Good** floor. Never ship a gate-failing blurb.

### B.8 Data + grounding discipline (lessons, hard-won)
- Ground in **present-day-verified facts** (web verification mandatory — training data is stale on the latest draft/portal).
- Ground the proprietary half in **real metrics** (PBP percentiles), woven as prose; box-thin subjects lean on grade/frame/attributed scouting.
- **Eligibility hard-gate:** a drafted/graduated/transferred-out subject never gets a returning-season forward blurb.
- **Identity/linkrot caution:** match curated content to its subject by a **stable key** (slug/name+context), not an unstable id (orphan-row linkrot).
- The **discourse-descriptor pipeline** needs cleanup (proper-noun/news filtering, descriptor-type classification, coverage) before the fan-vocabulary layer is usable.

## PART C — Cross-subject adaptation guide

Apply the same method; swap the **subject ontology**, the **"how-it-plays" companion**, and the **grounding sources**. The narrative half ports almost unchanged; the how-it-plays half needs a per-subject ontology (the analog of doc 67).

| | Narrative ("why it matters") | How-it-plays ("what it is") | Subject ontology needed | Grounding sources |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Player** *(built/live)* | the story, stakes, forward | play-style | **position ontology — [[67-position-style-ontology]]** ✓ | PBP percentiles + scouting + fan discourse |
| **Team** *(narrative spec'd: [[50-team-story-card]],[[51-team-narrative-engine]],[[62-team-page-master]])* | program state / season arc, in the tribe's voice | **how the team plays** — scheme identity, tempo, run/pass tilt, front/coverage identity, line-of-scrimmage character, special-teams | **team-identity / scheme ontology — TO BUILD** (analog of 67: offensive identity axes, defensive identity axes, tempo, physicality, variance) | team advanced/savant stats + scheme & coach profiles + fanbase discourse |
| **Coach** *(future)* | career arc, program fit, seat temperature, the bargain | **how he coaches** — offensive/defensive philosophy, scheme, tempo, roster-building (portal vs HS), game-management, culture | **coaching-philosophy ontology — TO BUILD** | record/tendencies + scheme charting + portal/recruiting approach + discourse |
| **Game / matchup** *(future)* | the storyline, stakes, what's on the line | **the stylistic clash** — strength-vs-strength, tempo battle, scheme matchup (e.g. their two-high vs his deep ball), trench edge | reuse team + coach ontologies (the *collision* of two identities) | both teams' profiles + the relational/collision detector (§B.3) |
| **Rivalry** *(future)* | the history, the grudge, the stakes-of-this-edition | **the cultural/stylistic contrast** — the identities that define the feud | reuse team ontologies + rivalry corpus | rivalry history + both fanbases' discourse |

**To extend to a new subject, you build exactly two things** (the method is otherwise reused wholesale): (1) the **subject ontology** (its style dimensions + native vocabulary + sub-archetypes + grounding, as doc 67 does for positions), and (2) the **how-it-plays companion** wired into that subject's page spine. Everything else — the sizing dial, the detector, the planner, the writer, the gate stack, the register ladder, the grounding law, temporal integrity — ports unchanged.

**The forthcoming team + coach profiles ([owner, in progress])** are the missing grounding input for the **scheme/system-fit pillar (§B.6 #7)** across all subjects: they let the engine ground *"the player's role within the team and the coach's system"* from canonical internal data instead of leaning on the open web. Wire them as a first-class evidence source for both blurb halves.
