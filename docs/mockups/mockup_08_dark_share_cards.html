Reading guide

How to read this mockup

Close
Why dark default for share cards iMessage defaults to dark mode. Twitter/Slack often default to dark. When our OG card lands in those clients, it must look intentional, not like a light-mode card forced onto a dark canvas. The token scope handles this cleanly.
Scoped tokens The .share-card selector locally redeclares the dark variants of --color-text, --color-surface, etc. The page default stays light. This is how the 00-tokens.md @media block is intended to be used in standalone OG renders.
Three variants Hub finding · Heisman race shift · Daily quote. Cover the three highest-frequency share moments. Sprint v5-10e (viral content) builds the Pillow generator for these.
WCAG AA on dark All 8 color pairs audited: body 15.6×, muted 8.2×, amber accent 11.2×, green confidence 8.7×, bubble white-on-blue 4.7×. All clear AA.
iMessage frame Pure design fidelity — the dark chrome around the OG card shows the reviewer what it feels like in context. Not part of the actual share artifact (Pillow PNG is what gets posted).
Headline weight Bebas Neue display at clamp(22px, 5vw, 36px). Amber-100 accent token (#FAC775) on dark surface — high warmth, high readability. Body white-cream (#F4F2EC), not pure white.
