Michigan Lands Five-Star RB Savion Hiter, and the Fan Base Immediately Splits Three Ways

The commit landed Tuesday afternoon. By evening, the stat folks were hedging, regular fans were celebrating, and the boards were already checking scheme fit. Classic.

Five-star running back Savion Hiter committed to Michigan on Tuesday, and before the ink was dry on whatever digital surface these things get signed on now, the fan base had already fractured into three distinct conversations. That's not a criticism — that's just what happens when a program with Big Ten implications adds a player at a position group everyone's been watching. The commit matters. What's more interesting is why it matters differently depending on who you ask.

The stat folks are intrigued but hitting pause. The advanced tape crowd isn't popping champagne yet, and honestly, that's their job. "Saw the film," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter. "Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment." That's the caveat that will live rent-free in recruiting debates for the next 18 months. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky was a touch warmer: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Michigan before judging." AdvancedScout over on r/cfb split the difference — "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit" — which is the statistical equivalent of saying we'll see, but we like what we see. Sentiment is cautiously positive, not euphoric. These folks want a fall camp before they'll commit to a grade.

Regular fans, meanwhile, are just happy. And why wouldn't they be? A five-star commit is a five-star commit, and Michigan's staff has been grinding. "Welcome to Michigan! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," came one fan comment from the program subreddit, capturing roughly half the internet's mood. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," added a fan tweet that probably got 200 likes before dinner. The Discord crowd was equally straightforward: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." College football has always been about managed chaos. The portal just made the chaos visible — and regular fans have fully embraced watching the staff work in real time.

The boards, though, have been waiting for this specific move. The die-hards who track position group depth by depth chart slot were tracking this one. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message board, the kind of sentence that only makes sense if you've been obsessing over the RB room since February. PositionCoachFan on Rivals did the homework: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." And Diehard_Fan on the message board closed the loop on the process itself: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." That last line is the one that'll age best — or worst.

What we're watching: Whether any verified film drops in the next 72 hours that moves the stat folks off their scheme-adjustment hedges — because if the tape wins that crowd over, the sentiment gap between these three groups closes fast. Also watching whether any competing Big Ten programs respond with a counter-move at the same position. Nothing clarifies a commit's value like a rival program suddenly getting active at the same spot on the roster.
