Michigan Lands Five-Star RB Savion Hiter — and the Fan Base Can't Quite Agree on What They Got

The commit is in. The celebrations are happening. The tape, however, is still loading.

Savion Hiter picked Michigan on Tuesday, and the Wolverines now have their five-star running back for 2026. That part is settled. What isn't settled — and what's actually interesting — is that three completely different versions of this story are being told simultaneously, and they all think they're right.

The stat folks are pumping the brakes, gently. This isn't a pessimistic crowd, just a measured one, and right now they're asking the question that always gets asked too late: against whom, and in what scheme? "Saw the film," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter. "Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment." That's a polite way of saying the box score doesn't travel without context. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky offered the most balanced read available right now: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Michigan before judging." AdvancedScout over on r/cfb landed in the same neighborhood — "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit" — which is essentially the stat-minded version of 'we like what we see, ask us again in August.'

Regular fans, meanwhile, are simply enjoying the moment, as they should. College football has always been about managed chaos, and the portal just made the chaos visible. In that environment, every commit feels like a lifeline, and Wolverines fans in the program subreddit and beyond were not bothering with nuance Tuesday afternoon. "Welcome to Michigan! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," came one fan comment from the program subreddit. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," added a fan tweet. A Discord commenter put the vibe best: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." Sentiment score of 0.72 doesn't lie — this group is happy, and they're allowed to be.

The boards, though, are where this commit gets genuinely interesting. The die-hards have been watching the running back room with the focus of a position coach, and they're treating this less like a celebration and more like a solved equation. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message board. PositionCoachFan on Rivals had clearly done the film work: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." Diehard_Fan on the message board cut straight to the institutional confidence angle: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want."

The split matters because it tells you what kind of commit this actually is. Not a ceiling-raiser. Not a panic move. A schematic solve that the stat folks want to verify, regular fans are celebrating, and the boards already signed off on.

What we're watching: Whether Hiter's previous program tape surfaces widely enough for the film community to move off the fence — and whether Michigan's staff drops any public comment about how he fits the offense. Seventy-two hours of that context will tell you which version of this story was closest to right.
