Michigan Lands Five-Star Savion Hiter — And Nobody Agrees on What It Means

The commitment dropped Tuesday afternoon and the fan base immediately split three ways. That split is the story.

Michigan picked up a five-star commitment from 2026 running back Savion Hiter on Tuesday, and the program's fan base did what it always does: splintered into factions before the ink was dry. That's not a knock — it's actually the healthiest sign a program can show. Different people watching the same player through different lenses, all caring enough to argue about it. The divergence here, though, is sharper than usual, and it tells you something real about where Michigan sits at this position heading into next cycle.

The stat folks are playing it close to the vest. The tape exists, the physical profile checks out, but the context around those numbers is still fuzzy. "Saw the film," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter. "Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment." That's the polite way of saying: we need more data. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky landed in the same place: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Michigan before judging." AdvancedScout over on r/cfb put it plainly — "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." Measured. Curious. Not sold, not skeptical. Waiting.

Regular fans, who make up the loudest and most immediate wave of any reaction, are simply happy. And honestly, that's a legitimate read too — a five-star commitment is a five-star commitment, and Michigan's staff has earned some goodwill. "Welcome to Michigan! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," came one fan comment from the program subreddit. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," a fan tweeted. "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it," added another voice from Discord. College football has always rewarded optimism more than caution at this stage. There's a reason signing day feels like Christmas even when half the presents are mystery boxes.

The boards, meanwhile, are doing what the boards do — connecting dots everyone else missed. These are the folks who noticed the position group thinning out three months ago and have been watching the staff's board ever since. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message board. PositionCoachFan on Rivals had actually done the film work: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." Diehard_Fan tied it together: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." That last line carries weight. Trust in process is a different animal than hype.

What we're watching: The next 72 hours will tell us whether other programs make a counter-move that reframes this commitment, whether Hiter's film circulates widely enough to shift the stat folks off their neutral read, and whether Michigan's staff signals any additional movement at the position — which would confirm exactly what the boards have been saying all along about the depth chart math.
