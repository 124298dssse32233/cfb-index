Five-Star RB Savion Hiter Is a Wolverine — and Three Groups of Fans Are Reacting to Completely Different Commits

Michigan landed one of the marquee names in the 2026 cycle. Whether that means 'generational talent,' 'necessary depth,' or 'wait for the scheme-adjusted tape' depends entirely on who you ask.

Savion Hiter committed to Michigan on Tuesday afternoon, and the fanbase did what college football fanbases always do: splintered into three completely separate reactions that, taken together, paint a more honest picture than any single one of them could alone. Five-star. Running back. Michigan. That's the headline. What happens underneath it is the actual story.

The stat folks are cautiously intrigued — emphasis on cautiously. The tape exists, the physical profile is real, but the evaluators who live in spreadsheets and scheme adjustments aren't ready to pop champagne yet. "Saw the another program film. Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter, which is the polite way of saying we need more data. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky was warmer but still hedged: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Michigan before judging." AdvancedScout over on r/cfb landed somewhere in the middle: "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." Translation: the ceiling is real, the floor is unknown, and anyone pretending otherwise is selling something.

Regular fans? They are simply, purely happy. And honestly, good for them. Not every moment in college football needs to be a film session. "Welcome to Michigan! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," came one comment from the program subreddit, and it carried the energy of someone who has been waiting all spring for something to cheer about. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," added a fan on Twitter, which is accurate if not particularly granular. "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it," said a fan on Discord, capturing the vibe of a fanbase that remembers what it felt like to lose ground in recruiting cycles and is relieved to be on the right side of one.

The boards, though — the boards have context. The people who watched Hiter during bowl season, who've been tracking this position group's needs since February, are reacting with something closer to relief than euphoria. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message boards. PositionCoachFan on Rivals had clearly done the homework: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." Diehard_Fan summed up the process itself: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." That last line matters. Michigan's staff didn't stumble into a five-star — they built toward this one.

The split here isn't about disagreement so much as depth of information. College football has always rewarded the people paying closest attention, and the portal just made those attention levels visible in real time. Hiter is genuinely exciting. He is also genuinely unproven at this level. Both things are true, and which one feels most urgent to you says more about how you watch the sport than it does about the recruit.

What we're watching: The next 72 hours will tell us whether any of Hiter's previous program's staff or remaining portal targets react publicly — a decommit chain or a rival's pivot in this cycle would reframe the significance of this commitment considerably. We're also watching whether Michigan's coaching staff schedules any early visibility moments (spring visits, social content) that might give the stat crowd something more to evaluate. If the physical profile the evaluators are flagging shows up on camera, the cautious optimism upgrades fast.
