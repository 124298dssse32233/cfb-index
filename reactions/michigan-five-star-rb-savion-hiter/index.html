Michigan Lands Five-Star RB Savion Hiter, and Everybody Immediately Disagreed About What It Means

The commit dropped Tuesday afternoon and the fanbase split three ways before the notification banners even cleared.

Savion Hiter chose Michigan on Tuesday, and the five-star tag did exactly what five-star tags always do: gave everyone a different thing to argue about. The recruiting profile is real, the need at the position was real, and the split in how Michigan fans processed this was — predictably, wonderfully — real.

The stat folks hit pause before they hit celebrate. The film-room crowd isn't dismissing Hiter, but they're doing what they always do: asking for more context before they'll let themselves feel good. "Saw the another program film. Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter, and that pretty much set the tone for the whole wing of the fanbase that thinks in adjusted splits. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky was cautiously intrigued — "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Michigan before judging" — which is responsible and correct and also slightly exhausting when you're just trying to enjoy a commitment. AdvancedScout over on r/cfb landed somewhere in the middle: "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." Translation: the ceiling exists, they just want to see it under Wolverine conditions.

Regular fans, meanwhile, didn't need the caveats. The program subreddit and Discord were warm from the jump. "Welcome to Michigan! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," came one fan comment, and it stood in pretty stark contrast to the film-room hedging happening two tabs over. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," added a fan on Twitter — short, accurate, satisfied. The vibe in that half of the fanbase is that the coaching staff has earned some trust, the position group needed reinforcement, and a five-star is a five-star. "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it," a Discord commenter added. College football has always been about managed chaos. The portal just made the chaos visible, and this part of the fanbase has made peace with that.

The boards were somewhere in the middle, and arguably the most interesting. The message-board faithful had actually been watching this position group for weeks, which means they brought receipts. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message board — not hype, just confirmation that the piece fits. PositionCoachFan on Rivals went deeper: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." That's the die-hard's version of enthusiasm — grounded, specific, quietly excited. "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want," Diehard_Fan added, which is really the through-line: trust in the process, not just the rating.

What we're watching: Whether any film surfaces from Hiter's previous program that moves the stat folks off their wait-and-see position — because if the tape clears their adjustments, the sentiment across all three groups converges fast. We're also watching the position depth chart to see if this commit signals another departure is coming, which would reframe the whole conversation.
