Michigan Lands Five-Star RB Savion Hiter, and the Fan Base Immediately Splits Three Ways

The commitment is real and it moves the needle in the Big Ten. What people think it means depends entirely on who you ask.

Savion Hiter picked Michigan on Tuesday afternoon, and the five-star tag on his recruiting profile did exactly what five-star tags always do: it made everyone feel something different at the same time.

The stat folks are pump-faking the celebration.

The film-and-numbers crowd is willing to be excited, but they're not there yet. The tape exists; the opponent quality around that tape is the open question. "Saw the film. Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter, which is the politest possible way of saying we don't fully know yet. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky added, "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Michigan before judging" — a reasonable hedge that will age either very well or very poorly by November. AdvancedScout on r/cfb put it plainly: "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." Translated: the ceiling is real, the floor is genuinely unclear, and Michigan's staff is betting on the physical tools surviving the context shift.

Regular fans are simply happy, and honestly, fair enough.

For the majority of Michigan's fan base tuned in on a Tuesday in May, a five-star commit is a five-star commit and the math is flattering. "Welcome to Michigan! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," read one comment on the program subreddit, which captured the mood cleanly. Another fan on Twitter kept it surgical: "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get." And from Discord, where nuance goes to be either perfected or ignored: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." College football has always been about managed chaos. The portal just made the chaos visible — and right now, Michigan's chaos looks organized.

The boards watched this position group all spring and they're nodding slowly.

The serious film-watchers who track Michigan's depth chart like a livestock auction were ahead of this one. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message boards — and that framing matters, because it reframes Hiter not as a splash add but as a schematic solution. PositionCoachFan on Rivals went further: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." That's not a standing ovation, but in message board terms it's a warm handshake. Diehard_Fan closed the loop: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want."

What we're watching: Whether any program-affiliated film surfaces in the next 72 hours that either validates the physical profile the stat folks are hedging on or complicates it. The boards will have a verdict before the beat reporters do. Watch the Rivals and 247 comment threads — if the tone shifts from cautious approval to genuine buzz, Michigan may have landed something that looks bigger by Friday than it does today.
