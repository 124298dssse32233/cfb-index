Savion Hiter Is a Michigan Man Now — But Nobody Agrees on What That Means

The five-star RB's 2026 commitment landed with a thud on Tuesday afternoon. The reaction split almost immediately along three very different fault lines.

Savion Hiter picked Michigan on Tuesday, and the five-star designation did exactly what it always does: it made everyone feel something, and nobody feel the same thing. The commitment is real, the ranking is real, and the disagreement about what it actually means is — also very real. Welcome to May recruiting season, where the tape is thin and the takes are thick.

What the stat folks are saying

The film-and-numbers crowd landed somewhere between intrigued and cautious, which is about where they live on every prospect until a full camp's worth of evidence comes in. "Saw the another program film. Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter, which is as measured as you get when a five-star drops. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky was similarly hedged but not dismissive: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Michigan before judging." AdvancedScout over on r/cfb threaded the needle: "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." The consensus here isn't skepticism so much as earned patience — they've seen big rankings not survive the jump, and they've seen unheralded prospects thrive in Ann Arbor. They're just not ready to call it yet.

What regular fans are saying

The general Michigan fan base, meanwhile, skipped the nuance entirely and went straight to joy — which is the correct and healthy response to a five-star commit in mid-May. "Welcome to Michigan! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," came one fan comment off the program subreddit, and it set the tone. A fan tweet put it plainly: "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get." And a Discord voice summed up the broader feeling: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." This cohort isn't wrong, exactly. Staff activity is a signal. A five-star is a five-star. Sometimes the right reaction is just to feel good about it.

What the boards are saying

The die-hards, characteristically, had been watching this position group for weeks before Tuesday's news broke. They saw the need before the staff announced the fill. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message boards. PositionCoachFan over on Rivals added actual tape context: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." And Diehard_Fan kept it institutional: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." The boards aren't celebrating blindly — they're nodding. There's a difference.

What we're watching

The next 72 hours will tell us whether Hiter's commitment triggers any downstream movement — either dominoes falling in Michigan's direction or competing programs pivoting their own boards in response. Big Ten rivals will be watching his positional fit tape closely now that the commitment is public. And if any additional film surfaces from his previous program's spring workouts, the stat folks will be all over it by Thursday morning. The real question isn't whether Michigan got a good player. It's whether the rest of the Big Ten just got a reason to worry.
