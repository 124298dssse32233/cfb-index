Vanderbilt Lands a Five-Star QB and Nobody Can Agree on What It Means

Jared Curtis is a Commodore. The film people want to wait, the message boards are cautiously pleased, and regular fans are already buying in. All three are kind of right.

Vanderbilt did something it almost never gets to do on a Thursday afternoon in May: it made the recruiting wire move. Five-star quarterback Jared Curtis committed to the Commodores for 2026, and within hours the reaction split cleanly into three directions that tell you almost everything about where Vanderbilt football is right now — and almost nothing about Jared Curtis specifically.

The stat folks want to slow down. And honestly, that's fair. Film study is a longitudinal sport, and five-star ratings on prep quarterbacks have a way of being revised by reality. "Saw the film," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter. "Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment." That's not a diss — it's just the discipline. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky was willing to lean in slightly further: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Vanderbilt before judging." AdvancedScout over on r/cfb landed in the same ZIP code: "The numbers need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." The throughline is patience. These folks are not popping champagne, and they're not wrong to hold.

Regular fans, though? They are absolutely popping champagne. Or at least a very enthusiastic sparkling water. The program subreddit was warm within minutes — "Welcome to Vanderbilt! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," read one fan comment that collected replies faster than most Vanderbilt football content manages. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," said a fan tweet that captured the general temperature. And from Discord: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." College football has always been about managed chaos. The portal just made the chaos visible — and Vanderbilt fans are thrilled to finally be in the chaos.

The boards are doing what the boards do: measured enthusiasm with receipts. These are the people who watched the bowl game tape voluntarily, and they're not going to pretend they didn't. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message board — and that word hunting is doing a lot of work. PositionCoachFan on Rivals clocked the film personally: "Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." Diehard_Fan brought it back to process: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." That's the Vanderbilt die-hard operating in their natural habitat: trust the process, acknowledge the ceiling, don't embarrass yourself by overselling.

The split matters because Vanderbilt isn't supposed to be here. A five-star commit is an event — not just a roster move. How the program develops Curtis will answer questions that go well beyond one quarterback.

What we're watching: The next 72 hours will show whether national recruiting analysts revise Curtis's composite rating in either direction now that the commitment is official, whether Vanderbilt's staff follows with any secondary announcements that suggest this is part of a coordinated offseason push, and whether the film community starts surfacing extended cut tape that sharpens the grade-ability debate. If the physical profile holds up under wider scrutiny, the cautious folks may have to move their number.
