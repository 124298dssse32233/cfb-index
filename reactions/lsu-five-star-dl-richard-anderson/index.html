Five-Star DL Richard Anderson Picks LSU — and Everybody Reacted Differently

The commit dropped, the notifications fired, and three completely different conversations broke out. That split is the actual story.

Richard Anderson made it official Friday, flipping his commitment to LSU and giving the Tigers a five-star defensive lineman for the 2026 class. The announcement hit, the purple-and-gold internet did its thing, and within hours you could watch three distinct fan universes reach three distinct conclusions about what it actually means. College football has always been about managed chaos. The portal just made the chaos visible — and commits like this one put it under a microscope.

The stat folks pumped the brakes. Not negatively, exactly — more like carefully. The film-and-numbers crowd spent the day flagging the obvious caveat: we haven't seen Anderson against elite competition, and five-star grades at the high school level are a starting point, not a verdict. "Saw the film," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter. "Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment." DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky echoed that patience: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at LSU before judging." AdvancedScout over on r/cfb landed somewhere similar: "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." Collectively, that's not a cold take — it's a measured one. The physical tools check out. The proof of concept comes later.

Regular fans, meanwhile, did not need a disclaimer. The program subreddit and Discord lit up with the kind of uncomplicated joy that makes college football worth following in the first place. "Welcome to LSU! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," came one fan comment from the program subreddit. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," added a fan on Twitter. "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it," wrote another fan on Discord. This is the majority of the volume — more than half the conversation — and honestly, it's not wrong. Roster-building enthusiasm is real enthusiasm.

The boards, though, brought the receipts. The people who've been watching this position group for months knew exactly why this mattered schematically. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message board. PositionCoachFan on Rivals went further: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." And Diehard_Fan tied it together neatly: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." That's the distinction — not just excitement, but contextual excitement. These are fans who can tell you what snaps the Tigers gave up inside last November.

The gap between those three reactions isn't a flaw in the fanbase. It's a feature of a program operating at SEC level, where every commit gets processed through a dozen different lenses simultaneously.

What we're watching: The next 72 hours will tell us whether Anderson's commitment triggers any downstream recruiting movement — either prospects who follow him in or opponents who now recalibrate their own DL boards. We're also watching whether any film deep-dives surface that sharpen the opponent-quality conversation the stat crowd started. If early camp reports begin circulating by weekend, that's when the measured takes either get validated or quietly shelved.
