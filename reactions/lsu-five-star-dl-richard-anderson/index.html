Richard Anderson Is an LSU Tiger, and Nobody Agrees on What That Means

The five-star DL commitment landed Friday night and immediately split the fanbase three ways. That split is the story.

Five-star defensive lineman Richard Anderson committed to LSU Friday night, and by Saturday morning the reaction had fractured in exactly the way you'd expect from a program that runs hot on everything. The commitment is real. The consensus on what it means is not.

The stat folks are holding their scorecards. The film watchers came in measured, almost deliberately so. "Saw the film," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter. "Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment." That's not a knock — it's a flag. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky offered a similar read: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at LSU before judging." The throughline from this corner of the conversation is patience — the physical profile draws real interest, but AdvancedScout on r/cfb put it plainly: "The numbers need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." Translation: the ceiling is visible, the floor is still being measured.

Regular fans didn't need a second opinion. The broader LSU base greeted the news the way they greet most good recruiting news — with immediate, uncomplicated enthusiasm. "Welcome to LSU! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," came one comment from the program subreddit. A fan tweet called it simply: "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get." Over on Discord, the sentiment matched: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." College football has always been about managed chaos. The portal just made the chaos visible, and most LSU fans have decided to enjoy the visibility.

The boards, though, came in with receipts. The message board crowd had been watching this position group for weeks, and they weren't surprised — they were satisfied. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster. PositionCoachFan on Rivals had actually done the film homework: "Watched him during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." The nuance here is real — this group sees a schematic need being addressed deliberately, not just a trophy commit. As Diehard_Fan put it on the message boards: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want."

The divergence matters because it maps directly onto what Anderson's first season will get judged against. The numbers crowd will be watching his snaps against quality competition. The regulars will celebrate the jersey. The boards will be tracking whether he fits the scheme. Three different scorecards, one player.

What we're watching: The next 72 hours will tell us whether Anderson's commitment triggers any movement in LSU's remaining DL targets — if other names start surfacing on the boards, this was the domino. We're also watching for any reaction from programs that were in his final group. Sometimes a commit closes a door. Sometimes it opens a conversation.
