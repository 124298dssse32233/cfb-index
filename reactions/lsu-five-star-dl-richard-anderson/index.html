Richard Anderson Is a Tiger, and Nobody Agrees on What That Means

Five-star DL Richard Anderson committed to LSU on Friday. The celebration was universal. The interpretation was anything but.

Richard Anderson picked LSU on Friday, and Baton Rouge did what Baton Rouge does — it welcomed him loud and immediately. Five-star defensive linemen don't grow on trees, and the Tigers' staff clearly worked to land this one. The commit is real, the need was real, and the disagreement over what it actually means started about forty-five minutes after the announcement.

The stat folks are hedging their enthusiasm. Not because Anderson looks bad — he doesn't — but because the tape only tells you so much without context. "Saw the film," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter. "Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment." That's not a knock, it's a disclaimer, and it's an important one. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky put it plainly: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at LSU before judging." AdvancedScout over on r/cfb landed in roughly the same place: "The numbers from his previous program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." The throughline is patience. The measurables are there. The verdict waits for fall camp.

Regular fans are not waiting for fall camp. They are already welcoming Anderson to the family, and honestly, can you blame them? LSU needed bodies up front, the staff went and got one, and that's a complete sentence for most of the fan base. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," came one fan tweet that landed like a consensus opinion. Over in the program Discord, someone put it even simpler: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." College football has always run on this energy — the belief that addition is always good, and that questions are for other fan bases. It's not wrong, exactly. It's just incomplete.

The boards, meanwhile, are doing their homework. These are the folks who watched Anderson play in person, who tracked the position group all offseason, and who know exactly which schematic holes needed filling. BoardPoster on the message boards: "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for." PositionCoachFan on Rivals went further: "Watched him during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." Diehard_Fan added the institutional faith element: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." The boards aren't euphoric. They're satisfied, which in their language is practically a standing ovation.

What we're watching: The next 72 hours will show whether any additional film analysis shifts the stat folks off their cautious read, whether opposing SEC programs react with any counter-recruiting moves that signal how seriously they're taking Anderson's ceiling, and whether LSU's staff follows this commit with another move on the defensive front — which would tell us a lot about whether they see Anderson as the answer or just the first piece.
