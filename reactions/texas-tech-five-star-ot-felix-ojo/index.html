Felix Ojo Is a Red Raider, and Nobody Agrees on What That Means

Texas Tech picked up a five-star offensive tackle commitment from Felix Ojo for the 2026 class, and the program's fan base did what fan bases do: immediately fractured into three very different reactions. The commit dropped, the notifications fired, and within hours you had three conversations happening in parallel that barely acknowledged each other. That gap is the actual story.

The stat folks pumped the brakes — politely. The tape-first crowd isn't skeptical so much as measured, and they're being upfront about the limits of what they've seen. "Saw the film. Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter, a pretty clean summary of the position: the raw tools are there, but the competition context matters. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky echoed it: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Texas Tech before judging." AdvancedScout over on r/cfb added, "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." Taken together, the read is cautiously optimistic — not a red flag, not a celebration, just a bookmark for fall camp.

Regular fans? They are absolutely here for it, no asterisks required. This is the majority of the conversation, and honestly, good for them. "Welcome to Texas Tech! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," came one program subreddit comment, which is exactly the energy a coaching staff wants circulating in May. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," added a fan on Twitter, and a Discord comment summed up the vibe neatly: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." College football recruiting has always been about optimism as a renewable resource, and nobody is generating more of it right now than the Red Raider faithful.

The boards, though, are the ones who've actually been watching the offensive line. The deeply-online die-hards who track position group depth charts the way normal people track weather — they've had this need circled for months. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message board. PositionCoachFan on Rivals went further: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." Diehard_Fan put a bow on it: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." That last line matters — it's trust in process, not just trust in the player.

The split here isn't dysfunction. It's just three different lenses on the same player, and all three are reasonable. College football has always been about managed chaos. A five-star commit just makes the hope visible.

---

What we're watching: The next 72 hours will tell us whether any competing programs make a late push — flip risk is real at this stage for five-stars, and a site visit offer from a blue blood would change the temperature fast. We're also watching whether Ojo's film surfaces more widely and whether the tape-review crowd lands on a harder take once opponent-adjusted grades circulate. If the boards stay bullish and the metrics folks get comfortable with what they see, this one settles into clean momentum. If another program starts showing up in the mentions, the mood shifts quickly.
