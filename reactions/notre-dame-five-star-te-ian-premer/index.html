Notre Dame Lands Five-Star TE Ian Premer, and Nobody Quite Agrees on What That Means

The commitment surprised most of the recruiting world — Surprise Index 76.3 qualifies as a genuine upset. What happened next was three completely different reactions from three completely different kinds of fans.

Surprise Index 76 ← unlikely

Nobody had Premer to Notre Dame circled on their board. The five-star tight end's commitment landed Friday morning with a Surprise Index north of 76, which in recruiting terms means the staff pulled something off that the consensus said probably wasn't happening. Marcus Freeman's crew got their guy anyway. Now comes the part that's actually interesting: depending on who you asked in the hours after the news broke, you got three entirely different reads on what Notre Dame just did.

The stat folks hit the brakes first. The film-and-numbers crowd isn't popping champagne yet, and they're not apologizing for it. "Saw the film," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter. "Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment." That's not a dismissal — it's a request for more data. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky put it simply: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Notre Dame before judging." AdvancedScout over on r/cfb added, "The numbers from his previous program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." The throughline: real promise, real questions, real patience required. College football has always rewarded programs willing to develop rather than just collect, and Notre Dame's track record at tight end gives the cautious crowd a reason to trust the process even while withholding the grade.

Regular fans didn't need more information. The program subreddit and timeline were warm and immediate. "Welcome to Notre Dame! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," read one fan comment on the program subreddit. A fan tweet cut straight to the point: "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get." And from Discord: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." This is not naivety — it's the rational response of a fan base that watched its staff identify and close a five-star prospect most programs had already written off. The enthusiasm is the appropriate emotion. The skepticism can come later, at fall camp, when it's earned.

The boards knew this one mattered before anyone else admitted it. The message board contingent had been watching the tight end room with specific anxiety all spring, and they received this news with the measured satisfaction of people who had done the homework. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message boards. PositionCoachFan on Rivals went deeper: "Watched him during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." Diehard_Fan kept it structural: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." That last line is the one that matters. Notre Dame didn't stumble into Premer. They hunted him.

What we're watching: The next 72 hours will tell us whether any other programs respond with a counter-offer — surprising commits at this level occasionally get revisited inside the first week. We're also watching whether Premer's previous program drops any reaction that signals this was more of a mutual parting than a pure steal, which would shift the evaluation conversation considerably. And quietly, we're watching whether Notre Dame makes another move at a skill position before the weekend ends. One unlikely get can sometimes signal a staff that's on a run.
