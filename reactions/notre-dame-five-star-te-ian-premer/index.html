Ian Premer Said Yes, and Notre Dame's Fan Base Said About Three Different Things

The five-star TE's surprising commitment to the Irish landed differently depending on who you asked — and the gap between those answers is the actual story.

Surprise Index 76 ← unlikely

Five-star tight end Ian Premer committed to Notre Dame on Friday morning, and nobody fully saw it coming. A 76.3 on the surprise index isn't "where did this come from" territory, but it's firmly in "wait, really?" range — the kind of commit that sends everyone scrambling to their preferred corner of the internet at the same time, for very different reasons.

The stat folks pumped the brakes.

This group wasn't cold on Premer, exactly — more like professionally cautious. The tape exists, the athleticism markers exist, but the sample size question is real. "Saw the film," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter. "Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment." DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky echoed the measured tone: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Notre Dame before judging." AdvancedScout over on r/cfb landed in the same zip code: "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." Translation: promising, unproven against elite competition, and Notre Dame's coaching staff is now the variable that matters most.

Regular fans skipped straight to celebration.

Which, honestly, is college football working exactly as intended. The program subreddit was warm immediately — "Welcome to Notre Dame! Love seeing the staff active in the portal" — and the energy held across platforms. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," read one fan tweet, which captured the mood in eight words. Over on Discord: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." This cohort isn't wrong to celebrate. A five-star commitment is a five-star commitment, and optimism is not a character flaw.

The boards, though, came with receipts.

The most informed corners of Notre Dame fandom have been watching this position group for months, and they landed somewhere between the other two: genuinely encouraged, specifically so. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message boards. PositionCoachFan on Rivals had clearly done homework: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." And Diehard_Fan cut to what they think actually matters: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." That last sentence is either reassuring or a warning, depending on how much trust you've banked with Marcus Freeman's staff.

What we're watching: The next 72 hours will reveal whether any competing programs push back with a late offer to test Premer's commitment, and whether Notre Dame's staff moves to close out any remaining 2026 targets who may have been watching this domino fall. The tape review cycle will also sharpen — if film accounts start posting actual clips with opponent-adjusted context, the sentiment among stat folks could move in either direction fast.
