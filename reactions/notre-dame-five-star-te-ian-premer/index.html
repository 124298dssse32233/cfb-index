Ian Premer Picks Notre Dame, and Nobody Quite Agrees on What That Means

The five-star TE's surprise commitment landed Friday morning and immediately sent three very different fan bases to three very different conclusions.

Surprise Index 76 ← unlikely

Five-star tight end Ian Premer committed to Notre Dame on Friday, and if you slept through the news cycle, the reaction alone would have clued you in that something unexpected happened. Surprise Index of 76.3 — that's not a mild upset, that's a genuine jaw-dropper — and the Irish fanbase did what Irish fans do: splintered immediately into factions, each convinced they have the correct read. College football has always been about managed chaos. The portal just made the chaos visible. Premer's commitment is a case study in all three flavors of it.

The stat folks pumped the brakes. The film-and-numbers crowd isn't popping champagne yet, and they'll tell you exactly why. "Saw the film. Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter, flagging the context problem that shadows every five-star not playing in a Power Four grind. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky kept it measured: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Notre Dame before judging." AdvancedScout on r/cfb landed somewhere in the middle — "The numbers from his previous program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit" — which is essentially the stat-world version of cautious optimism. None of these folks are saying no. They're saying not yet.

Regular fans said yes, immediately, and loudly. The program subreddit lit up within minutes, and the vibe was uncomplicated joy. "Welcome to Notre Dame! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," one fan comment on the program subreddit read, blissfully unbothered by opponent quality adjustment. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," a fan tweet declared, which, fair. "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it," came another fan comment on Discord, and honestly that energy carried most of the morning. This cohort represents the majority of the reaction volume, and they are simply happy. Sometimes that's the right instinct.

The boards, though, saw something specific. The people who have been charting this position group for months — tracking snap counts, watching bowl tape, cataloging which bodies were leaving — they understood immediately why this mattered. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message board, with the quiet satisfaction of someone who saw it coming. PositionCoachFan on Rivals had done the homework: "Watched him during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." And Diehard_Fan on the message board put the credit where the boards always put it: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." That last line is the one that threads all three reactions together — if the staff knew, somebody was surprised.

What we're watching: The next 72 hours will tell us whether any competing programs make a late push to flip Premer's commitment — surprise commits at this star level have a history of staying fluid into early summer. We're also watching for Notre Dame's depth chart to quietly update, which will tell the boards everything the press release won't.
