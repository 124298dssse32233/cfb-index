Notre Dame Lands Five-Star TE Ian Premer in a Commit Nobody Quite Saw Coming

The Irish pulled off a recruiting surprise that had a Surprise Index of 82.3 — and depending on who you ask, it means everything, something, or 'let's wait for camp.'

Surprise Index 82 ← unlikely

Notre Dame landed five-star tight end Ian Premer for the 2026 class in a commitment that, by most measures, came out of nowhere. The Irish weren't widely considered the frontrunner, and yet here we are — which is kind of how the best recruiting stories always go. But the more interesting thing isn't the commitment itself. It's the three completely different conversations that broke out the moment Premer made it official.

The stat folks are pumping the brakes — gently

For the film-and-numbers crowd, Premer is a prospect with a compelling physical profile and a question mark where the context should be. The tape is real, but so is the need to adjust for competition level before drawing any big conclusions. "Saw the film. Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter, voicing the measured skepticism that defines this corner of the fanbase. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky echoed the patience: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Notre Dame before judging." And AdvancedScout over on r/cfb put it plainly: "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." The stat folks aren't pessimistic — they're just not ready to crown anyone before a single Irish practice rep.

Regular fans? Already in love

The broader Notre Dame fanbase received this news approximately the way you'd expect — with open arms and zero asterisks. Five-star. Commits. Done. "Welcome to Notre Dame! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," came one fan comment on the program subreddit. Another fan tweet got right to the need: "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get." Over on Discord, the vibe was pure appreciation for the coaching staff's hustle: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." College football has always rewarded optimism, even when optimism slightly outruns the evidence. The regular fans are fine with that trade.

The boards saw this position group coming from miles away

The die-hards — the ones who track depth charts like spreadsheets and remember every bowl game snap — are reacting with something closer to quiet satisfaction than surprise. They've been watching the tight end room and knew a move was inevitable. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message board. PositionCoachFan on Rivals did the film homework: "Watched him during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." And Diehard_Fan on the message board credited the process: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." The boards aren't celebrating recklessly. They're nodding, which for them is basically a standing ovation.

What we're watching: The next 72 hours will tell us whether any other programs that were quietly in on Premer surface with a competing narrative — or whether his previous program's fanbase has opinions about how this ended. We'll also be watching for any Irish depth chart movement that signals where exactly the staff plans to use him schematically. If a coach drops a hint in a presser, the boards will have already charted it.
