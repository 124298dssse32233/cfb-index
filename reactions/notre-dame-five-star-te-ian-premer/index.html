Ian Premer Picks Notre Dame, and Nobody's Reading It the Same Way

A surprising five-star TE commitment has the stat folks pumping the brakes, regular fans throwing a parade, and the boards quietly nodding like they saw this coming.

Surprise Index 76 ← unlikely

Nobody had this one penciled in as a lock. Five-star tight end Ian Premer's commitment to Notre Dame on Friday morning carried a surprise index that cleared 76 — which, in recruiting terms, means the people who get paid to track this stuff were caught mostly flat-footed. Marcus Freeman's staff landed a name, and now the fun part begins: three very different groups of people are trying to figure out what it actually means.

What the stat folks are saying

The tape-and-numbers crowd is intrigued but not ready to pop anything yet. The dominant read here is patience — see how Premer's previous production holds up once you strip away scheme and opponent quality. "Saw the another program film. Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter, capturing the general mood of a group that's learned the hard way not to trust raw recruiting rankings. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky added that "the athleticism markers are there — give him a full camp at Notre Dame before judging," and AdvancedScout over on r/cfb landed in the same zip code: "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." Translated: good bones, prove it in South Bend.

What regular fans are saying

Regular fans did not get that memo, and honestly, good for them. The program subreddit and Discord lit up with the kind of uncomplicated joy that reminds you why any of this matters in the first place. "Welcome to Notre Dame! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," said one fan comment on the program subreddit. A fan tweet cut straight to need: "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get." And a Discord commenter summed up the broader vibe cleanly: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." This cohort isn't wrong, exactly — they're just skipping about four steps of evaluation.

What the boards are saying

The message-board diehards, who've been tracking this position group like it's a thing they have actual feelings about (they do), landed somewhere in the middle — genuinely pleased but measured about it. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," posted BoardPoster on the message boards. PositionCoachFan over on Rivals went further: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." And Diehard_Fan tied it back to process: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." That last part matters. Trust in the process covers a lot of uncertainty.

What we're watching

The next 72 hours will tell us whether Premer's commitment triggers a ripple — does it shake loose any other targets who were waiting to see which way Notre Dame was building at TE, or does the position group settle here? We're also watching whether any tape surfaces that shifts the stat crowd's measured skepticism into something warmer. One good film session can move a lot of opinion very fast.
