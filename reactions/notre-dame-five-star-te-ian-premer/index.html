Ian Premer Picks Notre Dame, and Nobody Saw It Coming — Which Is Why Everyone Has Opinions

Five-star tight end Ian Premer committed to Notre Dame on Saturday morning, and if you had this one circled on your board, you were ahead of pretty much everyone — his Surprise Index clocked in at 82.3, well into the nobody-saw-this-coming territory. The Irish landed him anyway. Now the interesting part: depending on which corner of CFB fandom you inhabit, this commit means something completely different.

The stat folks are tapping the brakes — gently. The data-forward crowd isn't skeptical so much as measured, and they're waving the flag they always wave when a big name lands without a deep film file: wait for context. "Saw the film. Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment," wrote CFBFilmRoom on Twitter, and that's basically the thesis. "The numbers from his previous program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit," added AdvancedScout on r/cfb. The throughline is optimism held at arm's length. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky put it cleanest: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Notre Dame before judging." That's not a red flag. That's just how this crowd operates — they'll tell you what they think once they've actually seen the reps.

Regular fans? Already in. The program subreddit and timeline lit up fast, and the vibe was uncomplicated joy. "Welcome to Notre Dame! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," came one fan comment on the program subreddit, which is basically the recruiting-season equivalent of a standing ovation. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," said a fan tweet that aged about thirty seconds before it was buried under similar ones. A fan comment on Discord captured the room: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." There's something genuinely refreshing about the part of the fanbase that just wants the team to be good and trusts that a five-star commit is a step in that direction. They're not wrong, either.

The boards had been watching this position group for weeks. The die-hards brought receipts. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message board, and you get the sense he'd been typing that sentence in his head since March. PositionCoachFan on Rivals actually did the homework: "Watched him during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." That's a specific, useful take, and it's why this group matters. Diehard_Fan on the message board credited the process itself: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want."

The split matters because these three reads aren't in conflict — they're complementary. One wants data, one wants wins, one wants proof the staff has a plan. Premer walks into Notre Dame carrying all three expectations at once. College football has always been about managed chaos. The portal just made the chaos visible — and occasionally, surprisingly, it lands a five-star.
