Washington Lands Five-Star OT Kodi Greene — and Nobody Agrees on What It Means

The commit dropped Sunday morning and by noon, three completely different versions of this story were already being written.

Washington picked up a five-star offensive tackle commitment from Kodi Greene for the 2026 class, and the Huskies fan base did what it always does: immediately split into factions. The announcement is the easy part. What comes next — the arguing about what the announcement means — that's where it gets interesting.

The stat folks are pumping the brakes, gently.

Not pessimistic, exactly, but measured. The film hasn't been fully digested through a Washington scheme lens yet, and that matters to this crowd before they'll assign a verdict. "Saw the another program film. Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment," noted CFBFilmRoom on Twitter, capturing the general posture. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky added: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Washington before judging." And AdvancedScout over on r/cfb put it plainly: "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit." The throughline is consistent — the tools are there, but the context is everything, and Washington's system will tell us more than the recruiting profile ever could.

Regular fans, meanwhile, are simply happy.

Which is correct, actually. A five-star offensive lineman just chose Washington. The vibes are good and the vibes are allowed to be good. "Welcome to Washington! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," posted one fan in the program subreddit, speaking for roughly half the internet in that moment. Another fan tweet cut straight to the point: "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get." A Discord comment summed it up: "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it." This crowd isn't wrong to celebrate. They're just celebrating a different thing — the recruitment, not the projection.

The boards, though, have context the other two groups don't.

The serious Washington watchers have been watching this position group with real concern for weeks. They know why this commit landed where it did on the staff's board, and they're reading this as a targeted fill, not a splash. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message board. PositionCoachFan on Rivals added: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." And Diehard_Fan on the message board offered the trust-the-process read: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." College football has always been about managed chaos. This is the staff managing it.

What we're watching: Whether Greene's film drops into wider circulation in the next 72 hours and moves the stat-folks' sentiment off neutral — and whether the Washington coaching staff follows this commit with any signal about where the OL depth chart actually sits heading into fall camp. One more move in this position group would tell us everything about how the staff sees the current room.
