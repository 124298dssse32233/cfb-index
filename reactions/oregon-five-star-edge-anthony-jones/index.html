Anthony Jones Is a Duck, and Nobody Agrees on What That Means

Oregon lands a five-star EDGE for 2026, and the reaction split tells you everything about how differently people watch recruiting tape.

Five-star EDGE Anthony Jones committed to Oregon on Saturday, and within hours the fan base had fractured into three distinct conversations — none of them wrong, exactly, but none of them quite talking to each other either. That's college football in May. Someone commits, the timeline lights up, and you realize everyone's watching a different movie.

The stat folks pumped the brakes — gently. The five-star label is real, but the evaluation crowd wants to see the tape in context before they hand out trophies. "Saw the another program film. Grade-ability at next level depends heavily on opponent quality adjustment," noted CFBFilmRoom on Twitter, threading the needle between skepticism and optimism. DraftMetrics_CFB on Bluesky was measured: "Low sample size but the athleticism markers are there. Give him a full camp at Oregon before judging." AdvancedScout over on r/cfb echoed the theme — "The numbers from another program need scheme-adjustment but the physical profile is legit" — which is film-room speak for: we like what we see, we just need more of it. The sentiment here isn't cold water, it's patience. There's a difference.

Regular fans, though? They're not waiting on scheme-adjusted numbers. The program subreddit and Discord were functionally a celebration the moment the news dropped. "Welcome to Oregon! Love seeing the staff active in the portal," read one comment that collected enough likes to suggest it was saying what most people felt. "Been needing depth there all spring. Great get," came a fan tweet, no hedging, no caveats. "The staff is working overtime in the portal. I'm here for it," added a Discord commenter who was clearly just happy someone showed up. This is the majority of the fan base, volume-wise, and honestly — their optimism isn't irrational. A five-star commit is a five-star commit.

The boards, as usual, were somewhere in between — but specific. The people who've been watching the EDGE position group all spring know this wasn't a vanity add. "This position group needed a body with real experience. This is exactly the fill the staff was hunting for," wrote BoardPoster on the message board, with the energy of someone who's been saying this for three months. PositionCoachFan on Rivals offered the most grounded take: "Watched him at another program during their bowl game. Solid technique, good instincts. Low risk, decent ceiling." Diehard_Fan on the message board gave the staff their credit: "The staff's due diligence is what made this happen. They know who they want." That last part matters — Oregon isn't swinging blind here.

The split isn't a crisis. It's the natural tension between the people who count stars, the people who count snaps, and the people who count scheme fits. College football has always been about managed chaos. The portal just made the chaos visible — and the recruiting calendar made everyone an analyst.

What we're watching: The next 72 hours will tell us whether Jones's commitment triggers any position-group reshuffling — if another EDGE target quietly decommits or goes quiet, the staff's board becomes clearer. We're also watching for film drops or 7-on-7 footage that gives the stat crowd something to actually work with. If the physical profile grades out the way the metrics folks expect, the cautious 0.15 sentiment score moves fast.
