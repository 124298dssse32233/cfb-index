# Author Portrait — The CFB Index's fictional editor-in-chief

## One-line brief
An original character — a leprechaun reimagined as a Gilded-Age sporting editor — rendered as a halftone hedcut portrait. The byline face of the publication.

## Visual family
Halftone hedcut engraving, warm-ink with amber accent on bone paper. Third illustration family in the system (alongside ligne-claire totems and risograph covers).

## Selection rationale
Character reads instantly — the leprechaun identity dominates (ears, beard, grin, stocky build, clover on hatband), the plutocrat styling (top hat, tailcoat, pinstripes, watch chain) is the costume rather than the identity. Pugnacious boxer's stance reinforces editorial voice: this is a writer who punches at conventional wisdom. The amber accents — hat clover, buckles, pocket watch — form a small diagonal path across the figure that guides the eye without dominating. Off-center right composition preserves left-side negative space where the byline/masthead would set.

## IP posture
Original character. Leprechaun is public-domain folklore (not the Notre Dame mascot). Plutocrat caricature references Thomas Nast 1870s engravings (public domain, not Rich Uncle Pennybags / Monopoly Man trademark). Both reference lineages sidestep existing trademarks.
