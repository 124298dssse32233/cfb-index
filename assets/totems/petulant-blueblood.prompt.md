# Petulant Blueblood — Archetype Totem 16

## One-line brief
An ornate gilded throne knocked onto its side. Visual shorthand for the blueblood program throwing a fit in public.

## Visual family
Ligne-claire totem, warm-ink + amber on bone paper. Matches locked master plate.

## Full prompt used
```
Single standalone 1:1 square illustration — not a grid, not a contact sheet, not panels. One image only.

Match the uploaded totem_master.png style exactly: flat ligne-claire on bone paper #F3EEE4, uniform 2px warm-ink contour, flat fills only, no hatching, no shadow, no gradients. Clean mid-century European cartooning discipline.

SUBJECT: an ornate gilded throne knocked onto its side on a plain ground line. High back, carved armrests, cushioned seat visible. The throne lies at roughly 30 degrees off vertical. No humans, no team logos.

AMBER: the throne's frame and ornamental carving are amber #E0A300. The cushion is warm ink. Exactly one amber element — the frame.

COMPOSITION: throne off-center right, lying toward lower-right, negative space upper-left, ~20% paper margin.
```

## Reference images
- `assets/masters/totem_master.png`

## Model + settings
- ChatGPT Pro, Art Director Custom GPT (gpt-image-1 backend), 1:1, single image.

## Selection rationale
The throne came through upright rather than tipped on its side (prompt said "lying at roughly 30 degrees off vertical"). Accepted because the uprightness actually strengthens the "petulant" read more than a toppled throne would — this is a throne preening, not a throne defeated. The rococo scrollwork ornamentation in amber with the warm-ink cushion is the strongest single-object ornamental read in the set; the throne is so elaborate it reads as comically self-important, which is precisely the archetype. Light ground shadow is the one composition rule broken (master plate bans shadows); minor, acceptable deviation.

## Rejection notes
None — single-image workflow, committed on first generation. Worth noting: prompt intent was "toppled" but the upright variant the model returned is a better fit for the archetype. If later totem-family consistency demands a tipped throne, regenerate. For now, keep.
