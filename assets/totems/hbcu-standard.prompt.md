# HBCU Standard — Archetype Totem 13

## One-line brief
A sousaphone mid-note, bell pointed skyward. Visual shorthand for the HBCU program whose cultural brand outranks the win column.

## Visual family
Ligne-claire totem, warm-ink + amber on bone paper. Matches locked master plate.

## Full prompt used
```
Single standalone 1:1 square illustration — not a grid, not a contact sheet, not panels. One image only.

Match the uploaded totem_master.png style exactly: flat ligne-claire on bone paper #F3EEE4, uniform 2px warm-ink contour, flat fills only, no hatching, no shadow, no gradients. Clean mid-century European cartooning discipline.

SUBJECT: a large brass sousaphone viewed from a 3/4 angle, the bell pointed skyward and slightly toward the viewer's right. Coils of tubing wrap around a central body. No humans visible, no school logos or lettering on the bell.

AMBER: the inner rim of the bell is amber #E0A300. The rest of the sousaphone body is warm ink. Exactly one amber detail.

COMPOSITION: sousaphone off-center right with bell rising into the upper right, negative space lower-left, ~20px paper margin.
```

## Reference images
- `assets/masters/totem_master.png`

## Model + settings
- ChatGPT Pro, Art Director Custom GPT (gpt-image-1 backend), 1:1, single image.

## Selection rationale
The bell points upper-right into generous negative space — the shape reads as anticipation, like a note held just before the drop. The dark bell interior with amber inner rim is the strongest amber anchor in the whole totem family so far — a tight ring of color against the black bell well. Tubing coils clean, valve stack visible on the right, mouthpiece tube projecting upper-right. This is one of the cleanest single-silhouette reads in the set.

## Rejection notes
None — single-image workflow, committed on first generation.
