# Service Academy — Archetype Totem 11

## One-line brief
A triangularly folded ceremonial flag on a wooden bench. Visual shorthand for the service-academy program whose identity is ritual, not ranking.

## Visual family
Ligne-claire totem, warm-ink + amber on bone paper. Matches locked master plate.

## Full prompt used
```
Single standalone 1:1 square illustration — not a grid, not a contact sheet, not panels. One image only.

Match the uploaded totem_master.png style exactly: flat ligne-claire on bone paper #F3EEE4, uniform 2px warm-ink contour, flat fills only, no hatching, no shadow, no gradients. Clean mid-century European cartooning discipline.

SUBJECT: a triangularly folded ceremonial flag resting on the slats of a simple wooden bench. Tight folds, crisp corners. Stars visible on the top-facing triangle face but no specific national insignia beyond generic five-point stars. No humans, no team logos.

AMBER: the top-facing triangle's star field is amber #E0A300 instead of warm ink. Exactly one amber detail.

COMPOSITION: bench horizontal across lower-right, flag off-center right on the bench, negative space at upper left, ~20% paper margin.
```

## Reference images

## Model + settings
- ChatGPT Pro, Art Director Custom GPT (gpt-image-1 backend), 1:1, single image.

## Selection rationale
The folded-flag triangle sits on the bench with a second fold visible underneath — the stacked folds read as "this has been folded many times, ceremonially." The amber star field across the top face is the bold anchor; stars themselves are warm-ink-on-amber which keeps the "one amber detail" rule honest (the field is one contiguous amber, stars are negative space within it). Clean bench slats, no background clutter.

## Rejection notes
None — single-image workflow, committed on first generation.
