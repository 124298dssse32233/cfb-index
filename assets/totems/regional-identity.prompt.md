# Regional Identity — Archetype Totem 17

## One-line brief
A vintage dark triangular pennant on a wooden dowel. Visual shorthand for the program whose whole identity is its place.

## Visual family
Ligne-claire totem, warm-ink + amber on bone paper. Matches locked master plate.

## Full prompt used
```
Single standalone 1:1 square illustration — not a grid, not a contact sheet, not panels. One image only.

Match the uploaded totem_master.png style exactly: flat ligne-claire on bone paper #F3EEE4, uniform 2px warm-ink contour, flat fills only, no hatching, no shadow, no gradients. Clean mid-century European cartooning discipline.

SUBJECT: a vintage felt pennant cut in the outline shape of a generic, non-specific US-state silhouette (don't use any real state outline — use a plausible invented shape that reads as "a state"). Frayed cloth edge. A short wooden dowel along the left edge with a rounded cap. No lettering, no logos.

AMBER: the dowel's rounded cap is amber #E0A300. The pennant body is a muted warm ink. Exactly one amber detail.

COMPOSITION: pennant off-center right with the dowel tilted slightly, negative space at left, ~20% paper margin.
```

## Reference images
- `assets/masters/totem_master.png`

## Model + settings
- ChatGPT Pro, Art Director Custom GPT (gpt-image-1 backend), 1:1, single image.

## Selection rationale
Deviation flagged: the pennant came through as a plain jagged-edged triangular sports pennant, NOT cut in a state-outline silhouette as the prompt asked. The visual intent — "this program IS its geography" — is partially lost; what we have is just "a felt pennant." Accepted on speed-over-perfection grounds (no curation rule) but flagged as the one totem in the family that under-delivers on its archetype's specificity. The amber dowel-cap is clean. If the family starts to feel thin in this slot after everything lands, regenerate this one with a stronger state-silhouette cue ("cut in the unmistakable outline shape of a state like Oklahoma or Tennessee").

## Rejection notes
None committed — single-image workflow. Regeneration candidate if the family shows weakness here.
