# Sleeper — Archetype Totem 18

## One-line brief
A dusty trophy on a high shelf with a single wipe-streak exposing fresh metal. Visual shorthand for the program that nobody's watching yet.

## Visual family
Ligne-claire totem, warm-ink + amber on bone paper. Matches locked master plate.

## Full prompt used
```
Single standalone 1:1 square illustration — not a grid, not a contact sheet, not panels. One image only.

Match the uploaded totem_master.png style exactly: flat ligne-claire on bone paper #F3EEE4, uniform 2px warm-ink contour, flat fills only, no hatching, no shadow, no gradients. Clean mid-century European cartooning discipline.

SUBJECT: a small two-handled trophy cup sitting on a high wooden shelf. A visible thin layer of dust on the shoulders of the trophy, with a single streak where a finger has recently wiped across it. The shelf is plain wood with a simple edge. No humans, no team logos, no lettering on the trophy.

AMBER: the wiped streak — the fresh exposed metal — is amber #E0A300. The trophy body itself is warm ink. Exactly one amber detail.

COMPOSITION: trophy off-center right on the shelf, shelf horizontal across the lower third, negative space upper-left, ~20% paper margin.
```

## Reference images
- `assets/masters/totem_master.png`

## Model + settings
- ChatGPT Pro, Art Director Custom GPT (gpt-image-1 backend), 1:1, single image.

## Selection rationale
The wipe-streak is a diagonal amber lightning bolt across the trophy body — exactly the "someone just looked at this" detail the archetype demands. Dust gets implied by the dark trophy body reading as weathered/tarnished. Wooden shelf with corbel bracket grounds the object horizontally. The trophy sits with its handles silhouetted cleanly against bone-paper negative space, which is the best possible read of "still life with overlooked object."

## Rejection notes
None — single-image workflow, committed on first generation.
