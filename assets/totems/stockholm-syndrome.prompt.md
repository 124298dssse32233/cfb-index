# Stockholm Syndrome — Archetype Totem 10

## One-line brief
A losing-score tombstone with a threadbare scarf tied around it. Visual shorthand for the fan who keeps showing up to watch the same loss.

## Visual family
Ligne-claire totem, warm-ink + amber on bone paper. Matches locked master plate.

## Full prompt used
```
Single standalone 1:1 square illustration — not a grid, not a contact sheet, not panels. One image only.

Match the uploaded totem_master.png style exactly: flat ligne-claire on bone paper #F3EEE4, uniform 2px warm-ink contour, flat fills only, no hatching, no shadow, no gradients. Clean mid-century European cartooning discipline.

SUBJECT: a simple rectangular tombstone standing upright in the ground, inscribed with a losing final score (e.g., "21 — 48") in condensed serif caps. A threadbare knitted scarf is tied in a knot around the top of the tombstone, with frayed tassels hanging down. No humans, no team logos.

AMBER: the knot of the scarf is amber #E0A300. The scarf body is a muted neutral. Exactly one amber detail.

COMPOSITION: tombstone off-center right, negative space at left, ~20% paper margin.
```

## Reference images
- `assets/masters/totem_master.png`

## Model + settings
- ChatGPT Pro, Art Director Custom GPT (gpt-image-1 backend), 1:1, single image.

## Selection rationale
The "21 / 48" score underlined as a fraction reads like box-score gravitas — a score as epitaph. The scarf drapes asymmetrically with its amber knot dead-center on the tombstone face, which pulls the eye exactly where the score lives. Small tufts of grass at the base ground the object. Composition drifted slightly toward center (vs. strict off-center-right) but negative-space left panel is still honored.

## Rejection notes
None — single-image workflow, committed on first generation.
