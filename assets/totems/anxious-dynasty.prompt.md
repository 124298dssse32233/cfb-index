# Anxious Dynasty — Archetype Totem 01 (Master Plate)

## One-line brief
A crumbling stone crown on a pedestal with an empty jewel socket, ivy creeping up one side. The canonical visual — and the style anchor for every other totem.

## Visual family
Ligne-claire totem, warm-ink + amber on bone paper. This is the master plate the entire totem family references.

## Full prompt used
```
Generate 4 candidate images for a print-era editorial illustration.

SUBJECT: a single symbolic object representing an anxious dynasty — a crumbling stone crown resting on a short pedestal, one front jewel socket empty, hairline cracks running down one side, a sprig of ivy creeping up the pedestal base. No humans. No faces. No team logos. Just the object on bone paper #F3EEE4.

MEDIUM: flat ligne-claire editorial illustration. Uniform 2px warm-ink #0B0F14 contour line on every shape. No hatching. No cross-hatching. Flat local-color fills only — stone in a muted neutral, ivy in a muted green. Every element rendered at the same line precision, foreground and background. Clean mid-century European cartooning discipline. Silhouette still legible at 40px.

AMBER: the empty jewel socket's rim, or a small tag tied to the pedestal, is amber #E0A300. Exactly one amber detail.

COMPOSITION: object off-center to the right, negative space at left. ~20% paper margin on all sides. 3/4 front view. Square 1:1 aspect.

Return 4 variants. All four must be flat ligne-claire on bone paper, contain exactly one amber accent, and use off-center composition with negative space.
```

## Reference images
None — this is the master plate that all subsequent totems reference.

## Model + settings
- ChatGPT Pro, Art Director Custom GPT (gpt-image-1 backend)
- 1:1 aspect, 4-candidate contact sheet (original workflow, pre-pivot)
- Selected top-right panel. Other three cropped out to `assets/rejected/masters/`.

## Selection rationale
Top-right panel chosen over TL/BL/BR because: the amber rim on the empty jewel socket is cleanest and most legible at this scale, the ivy climbs far enough up the pedestal to read as persistent decay (not a recent sprig), and the hairline crack on the left side of the pedestal gives the eye an entry point without fighting the crown silhouette for attention. Accepted with known minor composition/shadow drift noted in earlier review; user gatekeeper override.

## Rejection notes
- `rejected/masters/anxious-dynasty-candidate-tl.png` — smaller ivy sprig; crown silhouette less damaged.
- `rejected/masters/anxious-dynasty-candidate-bl.png` — no visible ivy; crack pattern different.
- `rejected/masters/anxious-dynasty-candidate-br.png` — ivy dominant; composition too busy.
